Northeast Montana Health Services
Research Portfolio ID: NEMHS-2024

Executive Summary

Northeast Montana Health Services (NEMHS) operates in a critical frontier environment. The robotics strategy leverages technology to overcome the 22-mile geographic gap between Wolf Point and Poplar. The primary levers are Telepresence for specialist access, Autonomous Logistics for inter-facility transport, and Service Automation for long-term care efficiency.

Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Telepresence

Focus: Virtual Staffing & Stroke Care

Reduces air ambulance transfers by 20%. Connects rural patients to world-class neurologists instantly.

Est. Benefit: £300k/yr

Pathway B: Logistics

Focus: Drone/Autonomous Transport

Connects Trinity & Poplar labs. Reduces turnaround time for critical diagnostics from hours to minutes.

Est. Benefit: £80k/yr

Pathway C: Service Bots

Focus: LTC Support (Faith Lutheran)

Autonomous dietary and cleaning bots to reduce physical load on nursing staff and improve retention.

Est. Benefit: £50k/yr
