University Cancer & Blood Center (UCBC)

Robotics Consultation & Automation Strategy Portfolio

Executive Summary

UCBC operates at the intersection of high-volume community care and complex clinical trials. The primary robotics opportunity is shifting from manual to automated sterile compounding.

This strategy addresses the critical bottlenecks of hazardous drug safety (USP 800), drug waste, and infusion chair scheduling. The projected outcome is a highly efficient clinical platform with improved margins and safety.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Compounding Robots
Scope: Automated IV Robotics (e.g., KIRO/Diana) in central pharmacy.

Key Value Drivers:
  • USP 800 Compliance (Safety)
  • Drug Waste Reduction (Vial Sharing)
  • 100% Accuracy Guarantee
Priority: Critical
Pathway B: AI Scheduling
Scope: Algorithmic "Software Robotics" for infusion chair optimization.

Key Value Drivers:
  • Flatten utilization peaks
  • Increase chair capacity by 20%
  • Reduce patient wait times
Priority: High (Quick Win)
Pathway C: Logistics AMRs
Scope: Autonomous Mobile Robots for lab/pharmacy transport.

Key Value Drivers:
  • Reduced staff walking time
  • Faster Lab Turnaround Time
  • Patient/Partner Marketing
Priority: Future State
