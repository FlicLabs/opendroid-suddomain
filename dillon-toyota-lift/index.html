Executive Summary

Dillon Toyota Lift is positioned to transition from a traditional dealership model to a Smart Remanufacturing Center. By leveraging Industry 4.0 technologies, the organization can optimize its High-Mix/Low-Volume (HMLV) operations. The primary objective is to integrate flexible robotics into service hubs in Utah and Idaho, reducing technician fatigue and accelerating equipment turnaround times.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Collaborative Robots (Cobots)

Focus: Safety & Precision

Deployment of IP65-rated Cobots for repetitive tasks such as steam cleaning parts and automated sanding for refurbishment. This augments skilled labor, allowing technicians to focus on complex diagnostics.

Pathway B: AMRs (Autonomous Mobile Robots)

Focus: WIP Flow Efficiency

Integration of AMRs for internal intralogistics. Robots will transport parts from the warehouse directly to service bays, significantly reducing non-billable "walking time" for high-value technicians.

Pathway C: Automated Inspection

Focus: Quality & Throughput

Vision-guided robotic systems for end-of-line palletizing and part verification, ensuring 100% order accuracy and improving supply chain yield.
