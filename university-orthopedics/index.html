Executive Summary

University Orthopedics is positioned to transform from a standard musculoskeletal provider into a high-tech "Center of Excellence." The strategic integration of robotic-assisted surgery, automated rehabilitation, and logistics support addresses critical bottlenecks in surgical volume and patient recovery. By leveraging these technologies, the practice can expect substantial returns through increased surgical throughput, reduced implant revisions, and optimized labor allocation.

Pathway A: Robotic Surgery

Deployment of robotic arm-assisted systems for total joint arthroplasty.

  • Precision alignment reduces revision rates by 30-50%.
  • Enables outpatient conversion for 80%+ of joint patients.
  • Market differentiation attracts high-volume surgeons.
  • Projected $500k savings via outpatient shift.

Pathway C: Logistics Automation

Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for sterile instrument transport.

  • Reduces OR turnover by 15-20 minutes.
  • Gains 1-2 additional surgical slots per OR daily.
  • Improves infection control via contactless transport.
  • Reduces non-clinical staff logistics time by 50%.

Implementation Roadmap

PHASE 1 Planning & Vendor Selection (Months 1-3)

Conduct surgeon workshops, analyze OR infrastructure, and issue RFPs to leading vendors.

PHASE 2 Pilot Program (Months 4-6)

Train 2-3 high-volume surgeons. Perform first 50 cases. Collect data on OR time and discharge disposition.

PHASE 3 Scaled Deployment (Months 7-12)

Expand training to full team. Launch "Open Joint" marketing campaign. Integrate with EMR.

PHASE 4 Ancillary Integration (Year 2)

Deploy AMRs for sterile processing. Launch robotic-assisted rehab protocols.
