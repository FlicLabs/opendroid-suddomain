Executive Summary

Vision: Zero Friction, Zero Hunger.
To transform FSWVA from a traditional warehouse model into a high-velocity automated distribution hub. By implementing AMRs and AI-driven logistics, we aim to maximize the throughput of perishable goods to the 26 counties served, ensuring freshness and reducing the physical strain on the volunteer workforce.

Pathway A: AMRs

Internal Transport

Deployment of Lidar-guided Autonomous Mobile Robots to handle long-haul pallet movements from receiving to cold storage. Reduces forklift congestion and improves safety.

Pathway B: Robotics

Piece Picking & ASRS

Implementation of semi-automated picking arms for high-repetition tasks like the "Backpack Program" and Senior Boxes. Increases kitting speed and storage density.

Pathway C: AI Logistics

Predictive Efficiency

AI algorithms to predict donation inflows and optimize expiration date management (FIFO), ensuring perishable foods are distributed faster.
