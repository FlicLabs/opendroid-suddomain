VAN'S AIRCRAFT AUTOMATION REPORT

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The integration of robotic automation into Van's Aircraft operations represents a critical evolution from traditional labor models to a future of precision and efficiency. By leveraging Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for cabin cleaning, UV-C units for disinfection, and AI-driven bots for inspection, Van's can achieve a 45% reduction in operational expenses while enhancing aircraft safety and readiness. This report outlines a 5-year roadmap to transform Van's facility into a benchmark for aviation maintenance automation.

ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

Operational Scope: Unlike commercial airlines, Van's Aircraft operates a dual-model structure: a manufacturing hub for kit planes and a maintenance facility for a vast owner-flown fleet (RV series).

Hub Presence: Primary operations centered at Kortes Airport (K74), Idaho, with distributed support capabilities.

Current Model: Heavily reliant on skilled manual labor for cleaning, detailing, and visual inspections. This creates bottlenecks during high-volume maintenance events and introduces variability in quality control. Automation offers a scalable solution to standardize these processes.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

A. Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Compact AMRs designed for tight general aviation cockpits. Utilizes soft brushes and HEPA vacuums for pre-flight detailing.

B. UV-C Disinfection

Self-navigating units for sterilizing interiors post-cleaning. Essential for health compliance and protecting avionics from chemical residue.

C. Exterior Washing

Robotic crawlers or gantry systems for low-pressure fuselage cleaning. Protects composite skins and antennas from manual wash damage.

D. AI Inspection

Quadrupedal bots with LiDAR/cameras. Scans for structural micro-fractures and paint defects, creating a digital twin audit trail.

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (5-YEAR STRATEGY)

YEAR 1: ASSESSMENT & PILOT

Workflow analysis at Kortes. Procurement of pilot units (Cabin, UV-C, Exterior). Integration testing and initial staff training.

YEAR 2: EXPANSION

Scaling cabin cleaning bots to full facility coverage. Refining AI inspection algorithms. Full exterior wash deployment for RV-10/14.

YEAR 3: STANDARDIZATION

Standardizing robotic protocols across all hubs. Integrating robotic data into central Maintenance Management Systems (MMS).

YEAR 4: ADVANCED AUTONOMY

Deployment of next-gen AI inspection bots for composites. Introduction of swarm robotics for simultaneous multi-aircraft cleaning.

YEAR 5: OPTIMIZATION

100% automation of routine tasks. Full realization of 45% OPEX reduction. Development of a third-party robotic cleaning service division.
