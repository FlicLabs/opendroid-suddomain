ICAS Strategic Automation Research

International Council of Air Shows | Robotics Implementation Plan

Executive Summary

This report details the integration of robotic cleaning and maintenance automation to elevate the safety, hygiene, and operational efficiency of ICAS aerial exhibitions. By deploying autonomous systems for cabin detailing, exterior washing, disinfection, and structural inspection, ICAS can modernize ground support operations, reducing human risk exposure while maximizing aircraft readiness for airshow displays. The strategic focus is on rapid turnaround and pristine presentation standards essential for VIP tours and media events.

Organizational Profile

Operational Footprint: The ICAS network facilitates the movement of hundreds of demonstration aircraft to major hubs (EAA AirVenture, Farnborough, Dubai).

Current Model: Relies on manual labor and manual lifts. Challenges include labor availability and the need for specialized care for composite materials.

Strategic Goal: Shift from reactive manual cleaning to proactive automated maintenance.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Robots
Mobile AI platforms for seats, bins, and galleys. Reduces pre-show prep time by 60%.
Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection Robots
Autonomous light emitters for hygiene standards. 15-20 minute cycles with safety sensors.
Pathway C: Exterior Aircraft Washing Systems
Robotic gantries for high-gloss finish. Reduces water usage by 30%.
Pathway D: AI-Powered Inspection Bots
Crawling drones with thermal imaging. Ensures safety compliance for performing aircraft.
