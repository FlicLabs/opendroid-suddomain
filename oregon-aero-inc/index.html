OREGON AERO INC.

Strategic Robotics & Automation Research Report

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents a high-level vision for integrating robotic automation into Oregon Aero Inc.'s operations. The strategic shift focuses on deploying autonomous systems for cabin cleaning, UV-C disinfection, exterior washing, and AI-powered inspections. By adopting these technologies, Oregon Aero aims to standardize hygiene, optimize turnaround times, and significantly reduce operational expenditures over a five-year horizon.

ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

Hubs: PDX (Primary), EUG (Secondary)

Fleet Composition:

  • Regional Jets: 30 Units
  • Turboprops: 15 Units

Current Challenge: Manual labor dependency causing bottlenecks in turnaround times and variable cleaning quality.

FINANCIAL DASHBOARD

CAPEX Per Unit $75k - $150k
OPEX Reduction 35-45%
Payback Period < 24 Mos

Financial projections based on current labor market rates and efficiency gains.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Robots vacuuming and sanitizing cabins during short turnarounds.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Automated UV-C light cycles to eliminate pathogens post-cleaning.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Automated systems for fuselage cleaning and aerodynamic maintenance.

Pathway D: AI Inspection Bots

Drones and crawlers for structural integrity and damage detection.

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

Year 1: Foundation

Pilot deployment of 2 Cabin Cleaning Robots and 1 UV-C unit at PDX. Baseline data collection.

Year 2: Interior Scaling

Expand cabin fleet to 40% coverage. Integrate AI Inspection Bots for pre-flight checks.

Year 3: Exterior Automation

Install first Exterior Washing System. Full fleet UV-C coverage.

Year 4: Hub Expansion

Replicate protocols at EUG hub. Deploy mobile exterior units.

Year 5: Optimization

System-wide integration. Predictive maintenance synchronization. Achieve 45% OPEX reduction.
