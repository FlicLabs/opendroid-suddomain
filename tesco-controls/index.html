01. Executive Summary

To maintain market leadership in the water/wastewater sectors, Tesco Controls must pivot toward an Industry 4.0 model. This strategy leverages flexible, semi-automated manufacturing to address skilled labor shortages and increase production throughput without expanding facility footprints.

02. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobots

Application: Machine Tending & Assembly.

Automated backplate drilling and component kitting to reduce repetitive strain and layout errors.

Pathway B: AMRs

Application: Material Transport.

Autonomous delivery of wire spools and sub-assemblies to eliminate non-value-added walking time.

Pathway C: Inspection

Application: End-of-Line.

Automated vision inspection for wiring compliance and heavy-payload palletizing for shipping.

03. Financial Projections

Deployment of robotic cells creates immediate operational leverage through labor reallocation and scrap reduction.

04. Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot Pathway A (Drilling Cobot) & Establish Digital Thread.
  • Year 2: Intra-Logistics Integration (AMR Deployment).
  • Year 3: Scale High-Payload Automation for End-of-Line.
  • Year 4: ML-Driven Production Optimization.
  • Year 5: Full Smart Factory Ecosystem.
