Lakemary Center

Robotics & Automation Opportunities Portfolio

Read Time: 5 Min

Executive Summary

Lakemary Center is positioned to transform IDD (Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities) care through targeted automation. By shifting focus from industrial robotics to Socially Assistive Robotics and Ambient Intelligence, the organization can address its most critical challenge: the staffing crisis.

Primary ROI Driver: Augmenting Direct Support Professionals (DSPs) to reduce turnover, lower injury claims, and improve resident safety without intrusive 1-on-1 monitoring.

Organizational Profile

Core Operations

Psychiatric Residential Treatment (PRTF), K-12 Specialized School, Adult Residential & Employment Services.

Demographics

Children and adults with Autism, Down Syndrome, Cerebral Palsy, and behavioral health needs in Kansas (Paola/Olathe).

Pain Points

High staff turnover, regulatory complexity, and safety risks associated with behavioral escalations.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Therapeutic Robotics

Scope: Social robots (e.g., Moxie, Milo) for schools and PRTF.

Impact: Delivers consistent social skills training and assists in de-escalation, reducing critical incidents by 15-20%.

Pathway B: Ambient Safety

Scope: Privacy-preserving radar sensors for fall/elopement detection.

Impact: Reduces need for intrusive night checks, lowers liability, and enhances resident independence.

Pathway C: Med Management

Scope: Automated pill dispensers and packaging.

Impact: 99.9% accuracy and frees up 15-20 nursing hours per week per facility.

This roadmap positions Lakemary Center not just as a care provider, but as a Tech-Enabled Human Services Leader. By using robotics to support rather than replace staff, Lakemary becomes a preferred employer and a destination for families seeking the safest, most advanced care available.
