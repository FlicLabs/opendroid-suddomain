Executive Summary

Pointcore, a subsidiary of OSF HealthCare, manages critical backend infrastructure including Supply Chain, Clinical Engineering, and Laboratories. The integration of Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) represents a transformative opportunity to shift from labor-heavy manual logistics to an automated, high-precision distribution model.

Our analysis identifies $2.5M - $4.0M in annual operational savings by deploying robotics in supply chain logistics alone. Furthermore, integrating robotics into Clinical Engineering workflows positions Pointcore to offer premium "Tech-Enabled Asset Management" services to external health system partners.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Supply Chain Automation (High Priority)

Deployment of AMRs for internal distribution of linen, waste, and bulk supplies. Shifts material handling from manual labor to automated fleets.

  • Impact: 40-60% reduction in transport labor hours.
  • Financial: $520k net annual savings per major hub.

Pathway B: Clinical Engineering (Strategic)

Utilizing RFID-enabled robots for automated asset tracking and AI-driven predictive maintenance for medical devices.

  • Impact: Reduction of "lost" asset write-offs by 15-20%.
  • Strategic: Creates a premium service offering for external clients.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pilot & Validate

Deploy 5 AMRs at one flagship acute care facility. Focus on linen and waste transport. Validate elevator integration and Wi-Fi coverage.

Year 1.5 - 2: Scale Network

Roll out logistics fleet to remaining 4 major hubs. Begin "Phase 2" retrofitting for RFID asset scanning capabilities.

Year 3: Commercialize

Package the robotics-managed supply chain and HTM data insights as a service product for Pointcore's external client base.
