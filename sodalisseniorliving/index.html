Sodalis Senior Living

Robotics Opportunities & ROI Analysis Portfolio

Executive Summary

Sodalis Senior Living is positioned to transform its operational efficiency across its 35+ communities through targeted automation. The strategy focuses on three core pillars: Dining Automation, Passive Safety Monitoring, and Environmental Robotics. The primary goal is to mitigate the labor crisis while enhancing resident dignity.

Pathway A: Dining Logistics

Highest Impact

Deployment of autonomous food runners (e.g., Bear Robotics) to handle bussing and running.

Projected Net Benefit $672k / Year

Pathway B: Safety Sensors

Strategic Value

Radar-based fall detection (e.g., Vayyar) to reduce liability and improve response times.

Value Driver Liability Reduction

Pathway C: Cleaning

Operational Efficiency

Autonomous vacuums for common areas to ensure consistent facility presentation.

Payback Period 12 Months

Financial Model: Dining Automation

Metric Value
Robot Lease Cost ~$1,000 / month
Labor Savings (4hrs/day @ $20/hr) ~$2,400 / month
Net Monthly Benefit (Per Robot) $1,400
Annual Network Benefit (40 Units) $672,000

Implementation Roadmap

1
Year 1: Pilot dining robots in 3 Texas flagships; rollout to 5 sites/month.
2
Year 2: Retrofit Memory Care with passive safety sensors; market "Safety Suite".
3
Year 3: Deploy autonomous cleaning fleet & centralized management dashboard.

Strategic Positioning

Executing this roadmap moves Sodalis from a standard regional operator to a Tech-Enabled Senior Living Leader. By automating low-value logistics (carrying plates, vacuuming), staff are freed to focus on high-value resident interaction, directly addressing the industry's retention challenge.
