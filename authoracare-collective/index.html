AuthoraCare Collective

Robotics Analysis & ROI Model

Executive Summary

AuthoraCare Collective, formed from the merger of Greensboro and Alamance-Caswell agencies, faces the dual challenge of managing a distributed mobile workforce and maintaining high-touch compassionate care. The primary robotics and automation levers focus on AI-driven logistics to optimize home care routes and therapeutic social robotics. The dominant ROI driver is a projected 15-20% increase in patient capacity through reduced travel time.

Strategic Pathways

Pathway A: Logistics AI

AI-driven scheduling and routing for nurse case managers. Reduces windshield time by 20-30 mins/day, creating capacity for ~36,900 additional annual visits.

Pathway B: Therapeutic Robotics

Deployment of robotic companions (PARO, Joy for All) and telepresence bots. Reduces PRN medication usage and extends physician reach.

Pathway C: Service AMRs

Autonomous mobile robots in inpatient units (Beacon Place) for linen and meal transport, offloading physical labor from clinical staff.
