Seattle Convention Center

Robotics & Automation Research Portfolio

1. Executive Summary

Overview: Following the historic expansion with the Summit building, SCC faces increased surface area and logistical complexity. The facility is positioned to become a leader in "Smart Venue" operations.

Key Opportunity: The highest ROI lies in Pathway A (Autonomous Cleaning), offering labor recapture of $300k–$500k/year while supporting LEED sustainability goals through advanced fluid recycling.

2. Organizational Profile

  • Scale: ~1.5M sq. ft. across Arch and Summit buildings.
  • Context: Downtown urban campus; vertical logistics; LEED Platinum targets.
  • Bottlenecks: Room turnover speed, janitorial labor shortages, high cost of labor.

3. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cleaning Fleet

Scope: Industrial autonomous scrubbers for halls and concourses.

Impact: 30-40% reduction in manual floor hours; consistent quality assurance.

High ROISustainability
Pathway B: Logistics

Scope: Heavy-duty AMRs/Tugs for chair and table transport.

Impact: Faster event turnover; reduced staff injury from heavy lifting.

Pathway C: Food Service

7. Strategic Positioning

Adopting this portfolio aligns SCC with the Seattle brand as a global innovation hub. It shifts facilities management from a labor-volume model to a tech-oversight model, ensuring operational resilience and leadership in sustainable venue management.
