1. Baltimore Medical System (BMS)

Executive Summary: Baltimore Medical System is the largest FQHC in Maryland. The robotics strategy shifts from high-capex surgical tools to high-throughput operational automation. The primary opportunities lie in Pharmacy Automation, RPA for Claims, and Telepresence. These interventions target administrative bottlenecks and patient throughput, unlocking millions in retained revenue.

Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Pharmacy Automation Highest ROI

Deployment of robotic dispensing systems (e.g., Parata/ScriptPro) to automate counting and packaging.

  • Reduces dispensing errors to near zero.
  • Redeploys pharmacists to billable clinical services.
  • Payback period: 14-18 months.

Pathway B: Robotic Process Automation (RPA) High Efficiency

Software robotics for revenue cycle management and scheduling.

  • Reduces claims denial rates.
  • Automates referral loop closure.
  • Implementation time: 3-6 months.

Strategic Positioning

Adopting this portfolio transforms BMS into a Tech-Enabled FQHC. By automating low-value tasks (pill counting, data entry), BMS allows its human capital to focus entirely on patient care and complex case management, essential for sustainability in value-based care models.
