Pontiac General Hospital

Robotics & Automation Strategy Portfolio

Executive Summary: Pontiac General Hospital (PGH) is a critical safety-net institution focusing on urgent care, behavioral health, and general medicine. The robotics strategy pivots away from high-cost surgical platforms toward operational efficiency. The primary levers are logistics automation to support nursing staff and robotic telepresence to expand specialty coverage.
Robotics Opportunity Pathways
Pathway A: Logistics & AMRs

Autonomous Mobile Robots for transporting meds, labs, and linens. Reduces nursing "hunting and gathering" time.

Focus: Efficiency High Priority
Pathway B: Telepresence

Robotic carts for remote specialist rounding (Psychiatry/Neurology). Reduces transfers and improves coverage.

Focus: Access Strategic
Pathway C: Disinfection

UVC robots for terminal cleaning of isolation rooms and ORs. Reduces HAI rates and penalties.

Focus: Safety Quality

Implementation Roadmap
Year 1: Logistics Pilot
Deploy 2 tug robots for Pharmacy-to-Nursing routes. Validate labor savings and staff acceptance.
Year 2: Telepresence Expansion
Roll out units in Behavioral Health and ER for 24/7 specialist access. Integrate with EHR.
Year 3: Environmental Scale
Deploy UVC disinfection robots for ORs and isolation units to minimize HAI penalties.
