01. Executive Summary

Ljungström is positioned to transition from heavy legacy fabrication to a smart manufacturing ecosystem. This report outlines the deployment of flexible automation to address high-mix production of air preheaters and gas-gas heaters. By integrating Cobots for welding and AMRs for transport, the company can secure a competitive advantage in the decarbonization market.

02. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobots

Application: Machine Tending & Root Pass Welding.

Focuses on relieving skilled welders from repetitive tasks on structural ducts and sealing systems.

Pathway B: AMRs

Application: Material Transport.

Heavy-duty AMRs (1000kg payload) to move heating element baskets between forming lines and enamel coating stations.

Pathway C: Quality

Application: EOL Inspection.

Automated vision systems to inspect enamel thickness and pinholes on heating elements to prevent corrosion failures.

03. Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot & Validation
    Deploy Vision Inspection and 2 Welding Cobots at Center of Excellence (Wellsville or Germany).
  • Year 2: Scale
    Expand weld cells to Asian facilities and introduce AMRs for line-side replenishment.
  • Year 3-5: Optimization
    Full digital twin integration and AI-driven process parameter adjustments.
