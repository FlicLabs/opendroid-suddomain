Strategic Automation Research

Prepared for HQ Aero Management Inc.

Executive Summary

HQ Aero Management Inc. stands at a critical juncture where traditional manual cleaning protocols are becoming the primary bottleneck in aircraft turnaround times. This research proposes a strategic shift toward a hybrid workforce model. By integrating autonomous robotic systems, we aim to decouple operational capacity from labor market volatility, transforming the cleaning division into a high-efficiency asset.

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Ruggedized robotic vacuums that navigate narrow aisles to remove debris, allowing human crews to focus on deep cleaning high-touch surfaces.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Robots utilizing ultraviolet C light to destroy pathogens on surfaces and in the air, ensuring verifiable sanitation without chemical consumables.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Semi-autonomous gantry systems or mobile robotic arms capable of washing aircraft at the gate, reducing towing needs and water usage.

Pathway D: AI Inspection Bots

Drone units or crawlers equipped with HD cameras and thermal sensors to flag fuselage anomalies using computer vision.
