Executive Vision

Southworth is positioned to transition from traditional discrete fabrication to a data-driven Connected Factory. By integrating robotics into heavy metal fabrication, the company can alleviate labor shortages while mirroring its own ergonomic value proposition in its manufacturing processes.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

PATHWAY A Collaborative Welding (Cobots)
Deployment of IP67-rated cobots for structural welding of scissor lift mechanisms. Focus on reducing human exposure to arc flash and fumes while ensuring consistent weld penetration.

PATHWAY B AMR Material Transport
Utilization of heavy-payload Autonomous Mobile Robots (1000kg class) to move steel plates and WIP between laser cutting and welding stations, eliminating forklift bottlenecks.

PATHWAY C Automated Inspection
End-of-line vision systems for weld integrity checks and hydraulic seal verification, ensuring zero-defect shipping for high-volume units like PalletPal.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot deployment of 2 Welding Cells & MES Setup.
  • Year 2: International expansion to China/Sweden facilities.
  • Year 3: Full Cloud Integration & Predictive Maintenance.
  • Year 4-5: AI Optimization & Smart Factory ecosystem.
