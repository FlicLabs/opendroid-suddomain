Executive Summary

Morrison Child and Family Services is a specialized nonprofit provider operating within the Multnomah County behavioral health system in Oregon, focusing on children, youth, and families impacted by trauma, substance use, and mental health challenges. As a provider of intensive outpatient, residential, and school-based services, Morrison’s operational model prioritizes high-touch clinical care, case management, and administrative compliance over high-volume throughput. Consequently, robotics and automation opportunities at Morrison differ significantly from acute care or logistics-heavy environments; the highest ROI lies in administrative automation, facility management, and logistics support rather than clinical robotics.

The strongest automation levers are: (1) Robotic Process Automation (RPA) for insurance billing, claims, and compliance reporting; (2) Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for facility sanitization and inventory management in residential settings; and (3) Telepresence and social robotics for therapeutic engagement and remote supervision. The dominant ROI driver is the reduction of administrative burden on clinical staff, allowing increased direct care hours and higher reimbursement capture, alongside improved safety and facility throughput in residential programs.

Organizational Profile

Type: Nonprofit community behavioral health organization serving children, youth, and families in the Portland metro area.

Scale: Multiple service lines including intensive outpatient programs, residential treatment, school-based services, and crisis intervention. Staffing includes licensed clinicians, case managers, and administrative support.

Partnerships: Embedded in the Multnomah County health system, Medicaid/Medicare billing, and partnerships with local school districts and juvenile justice systems.

Economics: Primarily funded through Medicaid (OHP), county contracts, and grants. Revenue is based on fee-for-service and value-based contracts for behavioral health outcomes.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A – Robotic Process Automation (RPA) for Administrative Workflow (Highest Value)

Deployment of software robots to automate repetitive administrative tasks, including insurance eligibility verification, claims submission, payment posting, and compliance reporting (e.g., CQI, outcome tracking).

Impact (Mid-Case): Reduction in administrative FTE requirements by 15–25% for billing and compliance staff. Improved claims acceptance rates and reduced days in accounts receivable (AR) by 10–20 days.

Pathway B – Facility Automation and AMRs for Residential Programs

Deployment of autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) for environmental sanitation (UV disinfection robots) and inventory management (supply chain robots) in residential treatment facilities.

Impact (Mid-Case): Reduction in staff hours dedicated to cleaning and inventory by 10–15 hours per week per facility. Improved infection control metrics.

Pathway C – Telepresence and Social Robotics for Therapeutic Engagement

Pilot of telepresence robots for remote supervision in residential settings and social robotics (e.g., therapeutic robots like PARO) for trauma-informed care and autism spectrum disorder (ASD) interventions.

Impact (Mid-Case): Increased access to specialist care, potentially reducing external referral costs. Reduction in behavioral incidents in ASD programs.

COMPARATIVE VIEW OF PATHWAYS

  • Pathway A: RPA for Admin Scope: All administrative functions. Main value driver: Staff efficiency and revenue capture. Annual benefit (mid): $250,000–$500,000. Payback: 3–6 months. Risk: Low.
  • Pathway B: Facility AMRs Scope: Residential facilities. Main value driver: Operational efficiency and infection control. Annual benefit (mid): $100,000–$150,000. Payback: 8–12 months. Risk: Low–moderate.
  • Pathway C: Telepresence/Social Robotics Scope: Clinical programs (ASD, complex care). Main value driver: Specialist access and incident reduction. Annual benefit (mid): $50,000–$100,000. Payback: 12–18 months. Risk: Moderate.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1 – Pathway A Pilot → Rollout

3–6 month pilot of RPA in billing and compliance. Scale to all administrative functions by year-end. Train staff on robot oversight and exception handling.

Year 2 – Pathway B Pilot + Scale

Pilot AMR disinfection and inventory robots in 1–2 residential facilities. Expand to all residential sites once infection control and savings are validated.

Years 2–3 – Pathway C Pilots

Deploy telepresence robots for remote psychiatric consultations. Pilot social robotics in one ASD or trauma-focused program. Evaluate clinical outcomes and staff feedback before broader rollout.

Strategic Positioning

Implementing this roadmap positions Morrison Child and Family Services as a tech-enabled nonprofit leader in behavioral health. Automation of administrative functions allows clinical staff to focus on direct client care, improving outcomes and job satisfaction. Facility automation enhances safety and operational resilience, while telepresence and social robotics expand service capacity and innovation.
