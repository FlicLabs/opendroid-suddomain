CharterCARE Health Partners - Robotics Research & ROI Analysis

Executive Summary

CharterCARE Health Partners is a dual-hospital system in Rhode Island, comprising Our Lady of Fatima Hospital (community) and Roger Williams Medical Center (academic). With a focus on oncology, cardiology, and advanced surgery, the system is positioned to leverage robotics for surgical expansion, pharmacy automation, and facility logistics. The primary ROI driver is surgical throughput optimization, projecting an annual margin improvement of $10–$15 million with payback periods of 6–18 months.

Pathway Analysis: Comparative Value

Three primary robotics pathways were evaluated for CharterCARE. Surgical robotics offers the highest strategic and financial impact, followed by pharmacy/sterile processing automation for efficiency, and facility logistics for operational savings.

Pathway A: Surgical Robotics

  • Scope: Expansion across surgical specialties and optimization.
  • Value Driver: Revenue growth and margin per case.
  • Annual Benefit: $10–$15M
  • Payback: 6–18 months
  • Risk: Moderate (surgeon adoption, scheduling change)

Pathway B: Pharmacy & Sterile Processing

  • Scope: Automated dispensing and instrument cleaning.
  • Value Driver: Efficiency and error reduction.
  • Annual Benefit: $1–$2M
  • Payback: 12–24 months
  • Risk: Low (proven technology)

Pathway C: Facility Logistics

  • Scope: AMRs for transport and cleaning robots.
  • Value Driver: Labor savings and patient satisfaction.
  • Annual Benefit: $0.5–$1.5M
  • Payback: 18–36 months
  • Risk: Low (proven technology)

Strategic Positioning

Executing this roadmap positions CharterCARE as a technology-forward surgical hub, attracting top talent, strengthening payer relationships through lower complication rates, and creating a scalable model for operational excellence.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Assessment, pilot surgical expansion in one specialty, and implement scheduling optimization software.
  • Year 2: Scale robotics to a second specialty, launch sterile processing automation, and upgrade pharmacy systems.
  • Year 3: Full-scale rollout of surgical optimization, deploy facility logistics automation (AMRs).
  • Year 4-5: Sustain improvements, integrate AI-driven analytics, and explore emerging robotic applications.
