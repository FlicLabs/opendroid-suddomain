01. Executive Vision

Brinly-Hardy stands at the threshold of Industry 4.0. The strategic goal is to transition from traditional discrete manufacturing to a Smart Connected Operation. By leveraging collaborative robotics and AMRs, the company can decouple labor availability from production capacity, ensuring peak seasonal demands are met without reliance on temporary labor surges.

02. Organizational Profile

Facility: Jeffersonville, Indiana
Type: High-Mix, Medium-Volume Discrete Mfg.
Key Processes: Laser cutting, forming, robotic welding, powder coating, assembly.
Critical Constraint: Manual material handling between islands creates WIP bottlenecks.

03. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

PATHWAY A

Collaborative Machine Tending

Deploying IP67-rated Cobots to tend press brakes and laser cutters. This reallocates skilled operators from repetitive loading tasks to high-value quality supervision.

PATHWAY B

AMR Logistics

Introduction of Autonomous Mobile Robots to replace forklifts. AMRs will transport formed metal parts to powder coating, reducing traffic and "wait time."

PATHWAY C

End-of-Line Palletizing

Automated palletizing for heavy lawn carts and aerators. Vision systems ensure 100% traceability and defect detection before shipping to retailers.

05. Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot End-of-Line Palletizing (Pathway C) to address ergonomic risks.
  • Year 2: Expand to Machine Tending (Pathway A) and ERP Integration.
  • Year 3: Full AMR Fleet Rollout (Pathway B) for logistics optimization.
