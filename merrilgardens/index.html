Executive Summary

Merrill Gardens operates ~67 communities with a focus on hospitality and proprietary technology. Amid severe labor shortages and shifting resident acuity, the primary strategic opportunity lies in Service Robotics (AMRs) for Care Delivery and Housekeeping. By automating manual transport tasks, Merrill Gardens can realize up to $30M in annual labor savings while enhancing its hospitality brand.

Current Robotics & AI Opportunity

1. Care Delivery & Logistics (AMRs)

Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) solve the "walking problem" in large communities. By automating the transport of medication carts, meal trays, and linens, Merrill can transition its "Universal Employee" model from manual labor to high-touch resident engagement.

2. Dynamic Staffing & AI Hospitality

Building on current pilots (TSO Life/LifeLoop), AI-driven acuity tracking allows for real-time robotic resource allocation. When resident needs spike, housekeeping robots can be diverted to laundry, freeing staff for hands-on care.

Strategic Opportunity Pathways

Highest Value

Pathway A: Care Delivery & Meal AMRs

Use Case: Implementing robots for tray delivery and med-cart transport across 67 communities. This targets the largest recurring labor expense in assisted living.

Est. Annual Benefit: $20M - $30M
Scalable Savings

Pathway B: Housekeeping & Floor AMRs

Use Case: Automated floor scrubbers and vacuum systems (e.g., Avidbots) for common areas and hallways. Supports the "Universal Employee" by handling the most physically demanding tasks.

Est. Annual Benefit: $8M - $12M
