SPOKANE INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT

Strategic Robotics & Automation Research Report

Executive Summary

The integration of robotic cleaning automation represents a pivotal shift for Spokane International Airport (GEG). By deploying autonomous systems, GEG aims to modernize ground support operations, ensuring higher sanitation standards and operational resilience. This strategic roadmap outlines a transition from manual labor-intensive processes to a data-driven, automated ecosystem, targeting a 35-45% reduction in operational expenditures with a payback period under 24 months.

Organizational Profile

Hub Status: Primary Commercial Gateway for Inland Pacific Northwest.

Operational Model: Hybrid (In-house + 3rd Party) with high seasonal variance.

Focus Areas: Rapid turnaround, passenger safety, and regional connectivity.

Current Challenge: Labor shortages and manual efficiency bottlenecks in cabin cleaning and facility maintenance.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Self-navigating units for vacuuming and sanitizing aircraft interiors during turnarounds. Reduces turnaround time by 30%.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Chemical-free sterilization robots for terminals and cabins. Targets pathogens in high-traffic areas and overnight wide-body aircraft.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Automated fuselage cleaning systems. Reduces water usage by 60% and eliminates high-angle safety risks for ground crews.

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Drone and ground-bot inspection units for facades and runways. Predictive maintenance data integration for asset longevity.
