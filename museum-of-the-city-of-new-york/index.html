Museum of the City of New York

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PORTFOLIO

Executive Summary

The Museum of the City of New York (MCNY) holds a vast collection of ~1.5 million items. Current manual digitization rates threaten to leave the majority of the collection inaccessible for decades. This portfolio analyzes the deployment of automated digitization robotics, telepresence systems, and autonomous security units. The primary strategic value lies in unlocking the "Dark Archive" for global licensing revenue.

Pathway A

Automated Mass Digitization

Scope: Robotic arm photography rigs & conveyor scanning.

Value: Increases throughput 10x via automated handling and metadata tagging.

Impact: Reduces cost-per-image by 60-70%.

Pathway B

Autonomous Telepresence

Scope: Mobile robots for remote guided tours.

Value: Opens revenue streams from global classrooms and accessible tourism.

Impact: Expanded educational reach beyond NYC.

Pathway C

Security & Environment

Scope: AMRs with LIDAR & thermal sensors.

Value: 24/7 microclimate monitoring and patrol without labor fatigue.

Impact: 20-30% reduction in security labor contracts.
