01. Executive Vision

Liberty Safe is positioned to transition from traditional discrete manufacturing to a Flexible Manufacturing System (FMS). By addressing the ergonomic challenges of heavy steel fabrication through robotics, Liberty can secure its margins against rising labor costs. The strategy focuses on high-payload Cobots for welding and Heavy-Duty AMRs for intralogistics, creating a "Smart Factory" environment in Payson, Utah.

02. Robotics Opportunities

Pathway A: Welding Cobots

Implementation of heavy-payload cobots (e.g., Fanuc CRX) for high-mix seam welding. Reduces welder fatigue and ensures consistent penetration on internal safe brackets.

Pathway B: Heavy AMRs

Deployment of 1000kg+ payload AMRs (e.g., MiR1350) to transport steel bodies from bending to paint lines, replacing forklifts and improving safety.

Pathway C: Auto-Inspection

Vision-based quality control for paint depth and gloss consistency, coupled with lift-assist automated packaging at the End-of-Line.

03. Implementation Roadmap

YEAR 1
Pilot Phase: Digital Twin creation and deployment of 2 Welding Cobot Cells.
YEAR 2
Logistics Scale: Integration of 3 Heavy-Duty AMRs for material transport between fabrication and paint.
YEAR 3
Quality Data: Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) installation and predictive maintenance integration.
YEAR 4-5
Full Autonomy: "Lights-out" manufacturing capability for 4 hours/day during peak seasonality.
