Executive Summary

AdaptHealth operates a high-volume, low-margin logistics model serving over 3.9 million patients annually. The current optimization problem centers on reducing the cost-to-serve for resupply items (CPAP, Diabetes) and streamlining the revenue cycle.

The strategic pivot is to move from a "distributor" model to a "tech-enabled health logistics platform." The dominant ROI drivers are Centralized Warehouse Robotics and Process Automation (RPA).

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Centralized Robotic Fulfillment

Concept: Consolidation of high-volume small parts (resupply) into regional hubs using "Goods-to-Person" robotics (e.g., AutoStore).

Impact: Reduces labor by 40-60%. Increases throughput to 300+ lines/hour.

Est. Annual Benefit: $15M - $25M

Pathway B: Revenue Cycle RPA

Concept: "Software Robotics" to automate prior authorizations, eligibility checks, and claims submission.

Impact: Reduces Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) by 10-15 days. Reduces admin overhead.

Est. Annual Benefit: $20M - $30M

Pathway C: Fleet & Route Automation

Concept: AI-driven dynamic routing and telematics for the heavy equipment delivery fleet.

Impact: 10-15% reduction in fuel/maintenance costs. Higher stops per driver.

Est. Annual Benefit: $5M - $10M
