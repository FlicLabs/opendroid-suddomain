Open Droids - Consultation Report

Robotics Integration Strategy for Bricktown Brewery

Date: October 26, 2023

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The hospitality industry is undergoing a significant transformation driven by automation and robotics. For entities like Bricktown Brewery, embracing robotics offers a compelling pathway to enhanced operational efficiency, improved customer experience, and sustainable growth. This report outlines a strategic vision for integrating advanced robotics into the brewpub model, focusing on augmenting human staff, ensuring consistent service delivery, and optimizing resource allocation. The goal is to elevate the dining experience while achieving substantial returns on investment through increased throughput and labor cost efficiencies, positioning Bricktown Brewery as an innovator in the modern culinary landscape. This approach targets not replacement, but empowerment, allowing human staff to focus on high-value guest interactions and creative culinary tasks.

ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

  • Location Count: Approximately 15 locations spread across several states.
  • Cuisine Type: American pub fare, including gourmet burgers, pizzas, salads, sandwiches, and appetizers, complemented by a wide selection of house-brewed craft beers.
  • Current Service Model: Full Service Restaurant (FSR), characterized by a sit-down dining experience, table service provided by waitstaff, and a casual, lively atmosphere. They emphasize a high-quality food and beverage offering with a focus on guest satisfaction and community engagement.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

Pathway A: Front-of-House Serving Robots (Focus on Table Turnover & Staff Support)

Description: Deployment of autonomous mobile robots to assist human servers with tasks such as food delivery from kitchen to table, dirty dish bussing, and condiment replenishment. These robots navigate dining areas efficiently, reducing physical strain on staff and allowing them to dedicate more time to guest engagement, order taking, and resolving complex requests.

Benefits:

  • Increased operational speed and efficiency, especially during peak hours.
  • Significant reduction in staff fatigue and physical labor, leading to improved morale.
  • Faster table turnover rates, directly contributing to higher revenue potential.
  • Enhanced consistency in service delivery, ensuring all tables receive prompt attention.
  • Frees human staff to focus on personalized service, upselling, and guest relationship building.

Pathway B: Back-of-House Automated Frying/Grilling (Focus on Consistency & Safety)

Description: Integration of robotic systems designed for precise cooking tasks, such as frying french fries, chicken wings, or grilling burgers and steaks. These systems can consistently execute cooking protocols, monitor temperatures, and ensure optimal doneness, leading to perfectly prepared dishes every time.

Benefits:

  • Unparalleled consistency in food quality and preparation, enhancing brand reputation.
  • Improved food safety through precise temperature control and reduced human handling.
  • Significant reduction in kitchen accidents and burns, leading to a safer work environment.
  • Lower food waste due to accurate cooking and portioning.
  • Ability to maintain high output volume during peak times without compromising quality.
  • Reduction in mundane, repetitive BOH tasks, allowing skilled chefs to focus on menu innovation and complex preparations.

Pathway C: AI-Driven Kiosks or Drive-Thru Voice Agents (Focus on Upselling & Order Accuracy)

Description: For Bricktown Brewery, the focus will be on AI-driven self-ordering kiosks positioned at entrances or specific dining zones. These kiosks provide an intuitive interface for guests to browse menus, customize orders, and make payments. Advanced AI algorithms can analyze order patterns and suggest personalized upsells or cross-sells, such as pairing a specific beer with an entree or adding an appetizer. While Bricktown does not typically have a drive-thru, these kiosks can also facilitate quick grab-and-go orders.

Benefits:

  • Increased order accuracy, minimizing errors and improving guest satisfaction.
  • Automated upselling and cross-selling capabilities, potentially increasing average check size.
  • Reduced wait times for ordering, particularly during busy periods.
  • Collection of valuable customer data for personalized marketing and menu optimization.
  • Reduced labor dependency for order taking, freeing up staff for other duties.
  • Multilingual support, catering to a diverse customer base.

Estimated CAPEX (Capital Expenditure):

  • A phased deployment targeting 5 key locations initially.
  • Per location estimate: 2 Front-of-House serving robots at $25,000 each = $50,000.
  • 1 Back-of-House automated fry station robot at $35,000.
  • 2 AI-driven self-ordering kiosks at $18,000 each = $36,000.
  • Total CAPEX per location: $50,000 + $35,000 + $36,000 = $121,000.
  • For 5 pilot locations: 5 x $121,000 = $605,000.
  • This CAPEX includes initial software licenses, basic integration, and initial training.

OPEX Impact (Operating Expenditure):

  • Target 15-25 percent reduction in Front-of-House labor costs by reducing reliance on manual bussing and food running.
  • Anticipated 10-15 percent reduction in Back-of-House labor through automation of repetitive tasks and reduced waste.
  • Potential 3-5 percent increase in average check size due to AI-driven upselling.
  • Reduction in food waste and consistent portioning, leading to 2-3 percent ingredient cost savings.
  • Overall projected annual OPEX savings and revenue increase per location: $70,000 - $100,000.

Payback Period:

  • Target < 14 months.
  • Achievable through a combination of:
    • Increased table turnover rates leading to higher revenue per hour.
    • Significant reduction in direct labor costs (FoH and BoH).
    • Enhanced revenue from upselling via AI kiosks.
    • Reduced food waste and improved operational consistency.
  • Based on an average annual saving and revenue increase of $85,000 per location, and a CAPEX of $121,000 per location, the estimated payback period is approximately 1.4 years or 17 months. With aggressive implementation and strong performance, this can be accelerated towards the 14-month target.

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

Year 1: Pilot and Proof of Concept

  • Selection of 2-3 flagship Bricktown Brewery locations for a pilot program.
  • Deployment of Front-of-House serving robots and AI-driven kiosks in these locations.
  • Intensive staff training on collaboration with robots and new workflows.
  • Establishment of key performance indicators (KPIs) for table turnover, labor cost savings, guest satisfaction, and order accuracy.
  • Continuous data collection and performance analysis to refine robotic deployment and integration strategies.
  • Initial financial review to validate ROI assumptions.

Year 2: Scaled Deployment and BOH Integration

  • Expansion of successful FoH robot and kiosk deployment to an additional 5-7 locations based on pilot learnings.
  • Introduction of Back-of-House automated frying and grilling systems in 1-2 pilot locations to test efficacy and integration.
  • Refinement of software and operational protocols based on expanded deployment feedback.
  • Development of comprehensive maintenance schedules and support infrastructure.
  • Begin scaling human resource adjustments and re-skilling initiatives.

Year 3: Regional Rollout and Optimization

  • Full regional rollout of robotics solutions across all 15 Bricktown Brewery locations, integrating FoH, BoH, and kiosk systems.
  • Establishment of a centralized robotics management platform for fleet monitoring and data analytics.
  • Optimization of robot routes, task assignments, and AI upselling algorithms across the entire chain.
  • Exploration of advanced integration with existing POS systems and inventory management.
  • Initiate a 'Robotics Champion' program within each location to foster internal expertise.

Year 4: Advanced Robotics and Data-Driven Enhancements

  • Exploration of next-generation robotic applications, such as automated beverage dispensing, inventory management robots, or specialized food preparation robots beyond frying and grilling.
  • Leveraging accumulated operational data to further optimize workflows, predict demand, and enhance guest experiences.
  • Continuous hardware and software upgrades to maintain cutting-edge performance.
  • Benchmarking against industry best practices and competitor automation strategies.

Year 5: Innovation and Sustainable Growth

  • Mature and integrated robotics infrastructure supporting the core operations of Bricktown Brewery.
  • Focus on long-term sustainability, cost efficiency, and competitive advantage derived from automation.
  • Proactive research and development into future robotics trends and applications for the hospitality sector.
  • Potential for new store designs or concepts that are built from the ground up with automation in mind, maximizing efficiency and guest experience.
  • Cement Bricktown Brewery's position as a leader in hospitality innovation.
