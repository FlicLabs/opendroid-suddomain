SFO AUTOMATION STRATEGY

Senior Consultation Report | Open Droids Aviation Division

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

San Francisco International Airport (SFO) stands as a premier global gateway, handling millions of passengers annually with a commitment to innovation and operational excellence. The integration of robotic cleaning and maintenance automation represents a transformative opportunity to elevate SFO’s operational standards. This strategy envisions a future where autonomous systems work seamlessly alongside human crews to enhance hygiene, safety, and efficiency. By deploying specialized robotics, SFO can achieve unprecedented levels of cleanliness, reduce turnaround times for aircraft, and minimize operational disruptions. This initiative aligns with SFO’s broader goals of sustainability, technological leadership, and superior passenger experience.

ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

Hub Status

Primary base for United Airlines; Key focus for Alaska Airlines. Major transpacific gateway.

Operational Model

High-volume, rapid-turnaround environment. Current model relies on manual labor for cabin cleaning and inspection, creating bottlenecks during peak hours.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

  • Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Robots
    AMRs for vacuuming, trash collection, and surface sanitization. Reduces narrow-body cleaning time to < 15 minutes.
  • Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection Robots
    Post-cleaning deployment for hospital-grade sterilization of high-touch surfaces and air filtration.
  • Pathway C: Exterior Aircraft Washing Systems
    Precision robotic washers reducing water usage by 40% while maintaining paint integrity during overnight operations.
  • Pathway D: AI-Powered Inspection Bots
    Drones/ground bots equipped with LiDAR for rapid FOD detection and fuselage structural integrity checks.

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

YEAR 1: PILOT PHASE

Deploy Pathway A & D in Terminal 2. Establish Automation Task Force. Baseline KPI measurement.

YEAR 2: EXPANSION

Scale Pathway A to 20 units. Introduce Pathway B (UV-C) for overnight wide-body cleaning.

YEAR 3: INTEGRATION

Full terminal rollout of Pathway A. Deploy Pathway C (Exterior Washing) at remote stands.

YEAR 4: SYNERGY

Unified Digital Twin integration. Expand Pathway D to cargo hold operations.

YEAR 5: MATURITY

Target 40% OPEX reduction. SFO established as global automation reference site.
