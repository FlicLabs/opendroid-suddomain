Executive Summary

Wing Aviation is positioned at the forefront of operational efficiency through the integration of robotic cleaning and maintenance automation. This report outlines a strategic vision to transition from traditional labor-intensive cleaning methods to a fully automated, AI-driven ecosystem.

By adopting Open Droids' specialized aviation solutions, Wing Aviation can achieve significant cost reductions, enhance hygiene standards, and optimize turnaround times.

Fleet Size: 150 Active Aircraft (Narrow & Wide-body)

Hubs: Primary International Hub + Secondary Domestic Hubs

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

  • Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Robots
    Compact mobile units for seat cleaning and trash removal during deplaning/boarding sequences.
  • Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection Robots
    Autonomous UV-C light systems for chemical-free sterilization of cabin interiors and high-touch surfaces.
  • Pathway C: Exterior Aircraft Washing Systems
    Rail-guided or autonomous crawler systems for fuselage cleaning, reducing water consumption and scaffolding risks.
  • Pathway D: AI-Powered Inspection Bots
    Drones and crawlers with high-res sensors for fuselage integrity, tire wear, and nacelle cleanliness checks.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pilot Phase

Deploy Pathway A (Cabin) & B (UV-C) at Primary Hub. Establish baseline metrics and staff training.

Year 2: Expansion

Scale to 50% of fleet. Integrate Pathway D (Inspection). Begin Pathway C trials.

Year 3: Full Deployment

Complete Primary Hub automation. Deploy Pathway C (Exterior) fully. Integrate data streams.

Year 4: Secondary Hubs

Replicate ecosystem at secondary hubs. Remote management optimization.

Year 5: Optimization

Predictive maintenance maturity. Fully autonomous scheduling without manual intervention.
