WellNow Urgent Care

Robotics & Automation Research Portfolio

Executive Summary

WellNow Urgent Care is a high-volume provider facing industry-wide staffing shortages and throughput bottlenecks. The robotics opportunity focuses on micro-robotics and workflow automation rather than heavy machinery.

The dominant strategy involves shifting intake labor to automated biometric stations and utilizing telerobotics for network load balancing. This approach targets the critical metric of "Patients Per Hour" (PPH).

Primary ROI Drivers:
  • Automated Triage Kiosks
  • Telerobotic Exams
  • Autonomous Disinfection
Organizational Profile

Scale: ~200 Locations (Midwest/Northeast US)
Parent: The Aspen Group (TAG)
Operations: Lean staffing model (Provider + Rad Tech/MA). High variability in daily volume.
Economics: Volume-driven model. Approx $150 revenue per visit. Profitability relies on speed and capacity.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways
Pathway A - Immediate Value

Automated Biometric Intake

Scope: Self-service pods measuring BP, O2, Height, Weight, Temp.

Impact: Saves 3-5 mins per patient. Reduces data entry errors. Allows staff to focus on clinical tasks.

Pathway B - Strategic

Telerobotic Assistance

Scope: Handheld robotic exam kits (TytoCare style) for remote physicals.

Impact: Load balances busy clinics with quiet ones. Reduces "Left Without Being Seen" rates.

Pathway C - Operational

Autonomous Disinfection

Scope: UV-C robots or automated scrubbers for overnight cleaning.

Impact: Reduces janitorial costs and validates sanitation for marketing.

Implementation Roadmap
Phase 1: Pilot (Months 1-6)

Deploy to 10 diverse locations. Integration with EMR for vital sign data flow. Validate time savings.

Phase 2: Regional Rollout (Months 7-12)

Scale to top 50% of clinics by volume. Retrain Medical Assistants for higher-level clinical support.

Phase 3: Network Optimization (Year 2)

Full deployment. Activate telerobotic layer for cross-clinic load balancing.

Strategic Positioning

Adopting this robotics portfolio positions WellNow not just as a clinic chain, but as a Tech-Enabled Care Platform. Automation standardizes the "product" (the medical visit), reduces provider burnout, and creates a scalable infrastructure that traditional, manual-labor urgent cares cannot compete with on speed or margin.
