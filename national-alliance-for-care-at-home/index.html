PORTFOLIO ANALYSIS

National Alliance for Care at Home

Strategic evaluation of robotics integration for workforce augmentation and interoperability standards in the post-merger home care landscape.

Executive Summary

The National Alliance for Care at Home (NACH) stands at a pivotal juncture following the merger of NAHC and NHPCO. The primary robotics opportunity is not direct clinical delivery, but the establishment of a centralized infrastructure for industry adoption. By creating the standards for Assistive Robotics (Pathway A) and Connected Home Hubs (Pathway B), NACH can unlock over $80M in annual industry-wide value through injury reduction and retention alone.

PATHWAY A

Workforce Augmentation

Centralized purchasing and certification for assistive exoskeletons and transfer robotics.

  • Reduce nurse back injuries by 35%
  • Extend caregiver career longevity
  • Members-only GPO pricing models
PATHWAY B

Connected Home Hub

Standardization of social robotics and telepresence for high-efficiency monitoring.

  • 1:50 remote monitoring ratios
  • Reduce "windshield time"
  • Combat hospice patient isolation
PATHWAY C

Data Commons

Cloud infrastructure to aggregate efficacy data for reimbursement lobbying.

  • Evidence for CMS rate negotiation
  • New analytics revenue streams
  • Interoperability standards
