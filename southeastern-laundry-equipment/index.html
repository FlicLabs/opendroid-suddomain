Executive Summary

This report outlines a strategic pivot for Southeastern Laundry Equipment towards Industry 4.0 adoption. By integrating flexible manufacturing technologies into distribution and refurbishment workflows, the organization can alleviate labor constraints and enhance throughput. The focus is on transitioning from manual-heavy logistics to automated intelligence.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Collaborative Robots (Cobots)

Safety & Precision

Implementation of 6-axis cobots for kitting spare parts and assisting in refurbishment sanding/polishing. This reduces repetitive strain injuries and increases kitting accuracy.

Pathway B: Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs)

WIP Flow

Deployment of heavy-payload AMRs to transport industrial washers and dryers between receiving, storage, and staging, reducing reliance on manual forklift operations.

Pathway C: Automated Palletizing

Throughput

End-of-line automation for stacking chemical drums and parts boxes, ensuring consistent shipping configurations and faster dock turnaround.

Financial Impact Analysis

Projected savings and output comparisons over a 5-year implementation horizon.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Infrastructure upgrades (IIoT Wi-Fi) and Pilot AMR deployment.
  • Year 2: Cobot integration for Parts Kitting & Refurbishment.
  • Year 3: Full fleet scaling and Palletizing Cell installation.
  • Year 4: Predictive Maintenance & Data Dashboarding.
  • Year 5: Fully autonomous ecosystem with minimal human intervention.
