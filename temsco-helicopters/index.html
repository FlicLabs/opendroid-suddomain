OPEN DROIDS
Client: Temsco Helicopters
Project: Auto-Maint Initiative

Strategic Research Report

A comprehensive analysis on transitioning Temsco Helicopters to a fully autonomous cleaning and maintenance ecosystem, ensuring safety, efficiency, and scalability.

Executive Summary

Open Droids proposes a strategic integration of robotic automation to maximize aircraft availability during critical summer windows. The vision transitions operations from manual labor to a precision-based autonomous ecosystem, ensuring hospital-grade sanitization and extended fleet lifespan through non-abrasive maintenance.

Organizational Profile

Fleet: Eurocopter AS350 A-Star, Hughes 500, Bell 212/412.

Operations: Tourism, Charter, Firefighting, Utility.

Hubs: Ketchikan, Juneau, Skagway, Denali.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

PATHWAY A

Cabin Cleaning

Autonomous vacuum bots for AS350 fuselages. Removes glacial silt and debris in under 4 minutes, optimizing tourist turnaround times.

PATHWAY B

UV-C Disinfection

Handheld and semi-autonomous UV-C emitters. 99.9% pathogen elimination for medevac and high-traffic passenger safety.

PATHWAY C

Exterior Wash

Drone-based washing units for salt spray removal and soot cleaning. Ensures corrosion control without height risks.

PATHWAY D

AI Inspection

Optical scanning drones for rotor and airframe analysis. Detects micro-fractures and composite delamination.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot Program - UV-C deployment in Juneau & Exterior wash test.
  • Year 2: Integration - Expansion to Skagway/Ketchikan & AS350 Cabin bots.
