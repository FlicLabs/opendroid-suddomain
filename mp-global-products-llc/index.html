Executive Vision

MP Global Products is positioned to lead the sector in "Adaptive Green Manufacturing." By integrating flexible robotics into the processing of recycled fibers, the company can stabilize production variability and eliminate ergonomic hazards associated with heavy underlayment rolls. The transition to Industry 4.0 will secure operational resilience against labor volatility.

Key Challenges

  • Handling variable, non-rigid fiber materials.
  • Ergonomic risks in manual palletizing of heavy rolls.
  • Dust generation requiring IP-rated machinery.
  • Need for consistent throughput to meet national demand.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Collaborative Machine Tending

Deployment of IP6x dust-proof Cobots at cutting and lamination stations. This removes manual operators from hazardous heat/blade zones and ensures precision alignment of fiber sheets.

Pathway B: AMR Material Transport

Utilizing Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) to move raw fiber bales from receiving to hoppers. This reduces forklift traffic in dust-heavy zones and creates a consistent "pull" system for materials.

Pathway C: Automated End-of-Line

High-payload robotic arms for palletizing finished QuietWalk rolls. This addresses the highest ergonomic risk factor and guarantees 24/7 stacking capability.
