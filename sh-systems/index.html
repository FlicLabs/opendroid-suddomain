EXECUTIVE VISION

Transitioning S&H Systems from a traditional integration model to a Flexible Manufacturing Ecosystem. The strategy prioritizes "autonomation"—collaborative robotics that enhance skilled labor rather than replacing it—to handle High-Mix, Low-Volume production demands efficiently.

OPERATIONAL PROFILE

Type: Discrete Manufacturing (HMLV)

Focus: Conveyor systems, sorters, and structural fabrication.

Current Bottlenecks: Manual material transfer and machine tending. High skilled-labor dependency creates throughput ceilings during peak demand.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

Pathway A: Cobots

Machine Tending & Assembly. Use ISO-compliant Cobots for press brake loading and welding assistance to improve safety and seam consistency.

Pathway B: AMRs

Material Transport. Deploy Autonomous Mobile Robots for line-side replenishment, eliminating 15% of non-value-added operator motion.

Pathway C: QC & Palletizing

End-of-Line Automation. Vision-guided systems for weld inspection and automated palletizing to maximize throughput yield.

FINANCIAL MODEL
  • Cell Cost: $60k - $120k per robotic unit.
  • Maintenance: Est. 8% of CAPEX annually.
  • Break-Even: 14-16 months via labor reallocation and scrap reduction ($45k/yr savings).
5-YEAR ROADMAP
  • Year 1: Pilot Phase (Single Cell + AMR).
  • Year 2: MES Integration & Expansion.
  • Year 3: Predictive Maintenance & QC.
  • Year 4: AI-Driven Process Optimization.
  • Year 5: Digital Twin & Ecosystem Maturity.
