EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The aviation industry is undergoing a transformative shift toward automation to address labor shortages, rising operational costs, and heightened hygiene standards. For the Metropolitan Nashville Airport Authority (MNAA), integrating robotics offers a strategic pathway to optimize cleanliness, safety, and operational efficiency across Nashville International Airport (BNA). This report outlines a comprehensive vision for deploying advanced automation technologies, including autonomous cabin cleaning, UV-C disinfection, exterior washing, and AI-driven inspection systems. By adopting these technologies, MNAA can achieve significant long-term cost reductions, improve passenger satisfaction metrics, and position BNA as a leader in next-generation aviation maintenance.

ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

Operational Scope

BNA serves as a major civil aviation and military joint-use facility, handling millions of passengers annually. The current operational model relies heavily on traditional manual labor for cleaning and security.

Key Challenges

  • High turnover rates in ground service and cleaning roles
  • Logistical complexities of cleaning aircraft within tight turnaround windows
  • Post-pandemic expectations for hospital-grade sanitation

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Robots operate inside the aircraft cabin during turnaround periods, performing vacuuming, seat organization, trash removal, and surface sanitization.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection Robots

Autonomous mobile units emitting high-intensity UV-C light to neutralize pathogens in terminals, jet bridges, and overnight aircraft cabins.

Pathway C: Exterior Aircraft Washing

Mobile robotic platforms scrub and wax aircraft fuselage exteriors, improving aerodynamics and reducing water consumption compared to traditional methods.

Pathway D: AI-Powered Inspection Bots

Small autonomous drones or rovers equipped with thermal imaging to inspect runway lights, pavement cracks, and terminal infrastructure.

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (5-YEAR)

Year 1: Pilot Phase

Deploy 3-5 units (Pathway A & B) in a single terminal. Establish data baselines for efficiency and hygiene metrics.

Year 2: Evaluation & Expansion

Analyze pilot data. Expand Pathway A & B to all major terminals. Initiate Pathway D trials for airside pavement.

Year 3: Infrastructure Integration

Full deployment of Pathway A & B. Purchase and deploy Pathway C (Exterior Washing). Integrate AI inspection data.

Year 4: Optimization & AI Scaling

Implement predictive maintenance algorithms. Automate fleet logistics (self-charging/docking). Expand Pathway D.

Year 5: Autonomy Maturity

Achieve full OPEX reduction target (45%). Transition to fully autonomous night operations. Evaluate next-gen tech.
