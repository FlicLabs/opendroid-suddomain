Yellowstone Boys and Girls Ranch

Robotics Opportunities Portfolio

Executive Summary

Yellowstone Boys and Girls Ranch (YBGR) is a nonprofit mental health and residential treatment organization serving youth across Montana and surrounding regions. With a mission rooted in therapeutic care, YBGR operates residential treatment centers, therapeutic group homes, schools, and outpatient services. Robotics and automation offer transformative opportunities to enhance safety, improve therapeutic outcomes, reduce staff burnout, and optimize facility and logistical operations. The highest-value robotics pathways are: (1) facility automation for environmental services and security, (2) logistics and supply chain robotics for campus operations, and (3) therapeutic and assistive robotics to support youth development and staff capabilities.

Organizational Profile

Mission: Providing hope, healing, and opportunity to youth and families through a continuum of therapeutic and educational services.

Scale: Multiple residential campuses, therapeutic group homes, and schools across Montana and neighboring states; serves hundreds of youth annually with a dedicated workforce of clinical, educational, and support staff.

Programs: Residential treatment, therapeutic group homes, day treatment, outpatient mental health, and special education services.

Economics: Primarily funded through Medicaid, state contracts, philanthropy, and insurance reimbursements; operational margins are tight, necessitating efficiency gains and cost avoidance.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A – Facility Automation and Environmental Robotics (Highest Value)

  • Scope: Deployment of autonomous floor cleaning robots, disinfection robots (UV-C or hydrogen peroxide vapor), and security patrolling robots for campus perimeter and common areas.
  • Key Levers: Consistent, high-frequency cleaning to reduce infection; overnight security patrols; environmental sensors for air quality and occupancy.
  • Impact (Mid-Case): 30-50% reduction in environmental services labor hours; 15-25% reduction in incident reports related to facility conditions; potential reduction in staff overtime for facility checks.

Pathway B – Logistics and Supply Chain Robotics (High Impact)

  • Scope: Automated mobile robots (AMRs) for campus logistics, including meal delivery, laundry transport, medication logistics (non-controlled substances), and supply distribution.
  • Key Levers: Centralized supply management with automated distribution; predictive restocking and inventory tracking; integration with EHR and facility management systems.
  • Impact (Mid-Case): Reduction in non-client-facing travel time by 20-40%; improved inventory accuracy reducing spoilage by 10-20%; enhanced staff satisfaction by minimizing non-clinical tasks.

Pathway C – Therapeutic and Assistive Robotics (Complementary)

  • Scope: Social robots for therapeutic engagement, STEM-focused robotics kits for educational programs, and assistive devices for youth with physical or developmental needs.
  • Key Levers: Robotics-assisted learning for life skills and vocational training; social robots to support emotional regulation and interaction; mobility assistance for youth with physical disabilities.
  • Impact (Realistic): Enhanced engagement and retention in programs; improved social-emotional outcomes and measurable progress on IEP goals; differentiation in service offerings to attract and retain clients.

Financial Model – Pathway A (Selected)

Baseline (estimated): Residential campuses with environmental services staff of ~2 FTE per campus; annual facility maintenance and incident-related costs.

Automation Scenario: Deployment of 2-3 autonomous cleaning robots per campus, 1-2 security robots per larger campus. Platform cost: ~$50,000-$100,000 per campus. Annual operations: ~$10,000-$20,000 per campus.

Estimated Benefits (Annual, per campus): Labor reallocation: 1.5 FTE redeployed (~$60,000-$90,000). Overtime and incident reduction: ~$10,000-$25,000. Total annual benefit: ~$70,000-$115,000.

Payback and ROI: Payback: 12-24 months. 5-year cumulative net benefit per campus: ~$250,000-$400,000. Network-wide (5 campuses): ~$1.25M-$2M net benefit over 5 years.

Implementation Roadmap (High-Level)

  1. Year 1 – Pathway A Pilot → Rollout: 1-2 campuses for pilot of cleaning and security robots. Evaluate safety, efficacy, and staff acceptance. Scale to additional campuses based on pilot results.
  2. Years 2–3 – Pathway B Pilot + Scale: Deploy logistics robots in one campus or campus cluster. Integrate with inventory and EHR systems. Expand to other campuses once workflows are optimized.
  3. Years 3–5 – Pathway C Pilots: Introduce therapeutic robotics kits and social robots in specific programs (e.g., ASD support, STEM education). Measure clinical and educational outcomes. Refine and expand based on evidence.

Strategic Positioning

Implementing this roadmap positions Yellowstone Boys and Girls Ranch as a leader in innovative, technology-supported therapeutic care. By integrating robotics for facility management, logistics, and therapy, YBGR can improve safety, enhance staff focus on clients, and demonstrate cutting-edge approaches to youth mental health. This can strengthen funding appeals, payer relationships, and community partnerships, ultimately supporting the mission to provide hope and healing.
