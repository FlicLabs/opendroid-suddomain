Stepping Stones Museum: Robotics Opportunities Portfolio

Executive Summary

Stepping Stones Museum for Children (Norwalk, CT) faces unique challenges regarding sanitation, educational scaling, and cost management. The strongest automation levers are autonomous janitorial robotics, social educational robots, and automated food service. The dominant ROI driver is the reduction of maintenance labor and enhancement of visitor experience.

1. Organizational Profile

Model:
501(c)(3) non-profit focusing on STEAM, early childhood education, and healthy living.
Scale:
Located in Norwalk, CT. Regional audience (CT/NY). 150k–200k annual visitors.
Operational Context:
High-touch exhibits require rigorous cleaning. Cost structures are heavily weighted toward labor and facility upkeep.

2. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Sanitation Highest Value

Scope: Deployment of commercial-grade autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) for floor scrubbing and UV-C disinfection.

Impact: Reduction of 2–3 manual labor hours per night. Projected savings of $30k–$45k annually per unit. Reduces slip/fall liability.

Pathway B: STEAM Learning Companions Strategic

Scope: Integration of humanoid robots (e.g., Misty, Pepper) into daily programming.

Impact: Acts as teaching assistants, increasing workshop capacity. Directly supports grant applications for technology innovation.

Pathway C: Automated Food Service Revenue

Scope: Robotic kiosks for smoothies, coffee, or treats.

Impact: "Theatre of food" experience. Generates $50k–$80k incremental revenue. Zero labor cost during downtime.

5. Implementation Roadmap

1
Year 1 (Pilot): Lease one autonomous floor scrubber for the Atrium. Market the "Clean Team" initiative to parents.
2
Year 2 (Integration): Acquire 2 social robots for learning labs. Launch "Coding with Robots" workshops.
3
Year 3 (Expansion): Install robotic vending kiosk. Integrate robot data into exhibits to teach data science.

6. Strategic Positioning

By adopting this roadmap, Stepping Stones Museum transitions from a traditional museum to a living lab of future technology. It solves the critical business problem of hygiene in a high-touch environment while simultaneously using the solution as a teaching tool.
