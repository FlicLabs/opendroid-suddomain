Executive Summary: MHA & Global Budget Revenue

The Maryland Hospital Association (MHA) operates within the unique US "Total Cost of Care" model. Revenue is capped. Therefore, value is derived strictly from operational efficiency and cost avoidance rather than volume growth.

Our analysis indicates that Pathway A (Intra-Logistics AMRs) offers the most immediate relief for the critical nursing shortage, potentially liberating thousands of clinical hours annually across the network.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Intra-Logistics Automation (AMRs)

Deployment of fleet-based autonomous robots for linen, meds, and lab transport. Directly addresses the Global Budget mandate by reducing travel nurse dependency.

Pathway B: Statewide Tele-Robotics

Virtual specialist rounding (Stroke/ICU) in rural member hospitals to prevent unnecessary transfers and retain care locally.

Pathway C: Centralized Shared Services

Off-site robotic pharmacy compounding hubs shared by multiple small community hospitals to reduce waste and overhead.
