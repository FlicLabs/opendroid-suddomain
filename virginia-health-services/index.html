Executive Summary

Virginia Health Services (VHS) faces a critical juncture in post-acute care where labor shortages threaten operational continuity. This portfolio identifies a high-ROI strategy leveraging Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for facility logistics and Robotic Rehabilitation for clinical differentiation. Implementation promises to reclaim thousands of clinical hours annually, reducing staff burnout and solidifying VHS's market leadership in the Virginia Peninsula.

Pathway A: Logistics & Service

Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMR)

Deployment of fleet delivery robots (Tugs/Shelves) in Skilled Nursing Facilities.

  • Automates dietary, laundry, and waste transport.
  • Eliminates "last 100 yards" manual hauling.
  • Reduces workplace injury claims from heavy carts.

Pathway B: Clinical Differentiation

Robotic Rehabilitation Suites

Advanced exoskeletons and upper-extremity trainers at Rehab Hubs.

  • High-repetition therapy for stroke/ortho recovery.
  • Objective data reporting for payers.
  • Marketing magnet for high-acuity hospital referrals.

Year 1: Pilot

Launch AMR logistics pilot at flagship facility (Coliseum). Validate dietary workflow savings.

Deploy Rehab Robotics at primary rehabilitation center. Market new capabilities to regional hospitals.

Year 3: Network Scale
