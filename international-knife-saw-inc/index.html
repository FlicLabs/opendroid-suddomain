Executive Summary

International Knife & Saw Inc. (IKS) requires a specialized robotics strategy that prioritizes safety and heavy-material handling. By transitioning from manual forklifts to Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) and Vertical Lift Modules (VLMs), IKS will decouple human labor from hazardous steel handling, optimize storage density for heavy SKUs, and enable 24/7 material staging capabilities.

Pathway A

Heavy-Duty AMRs

Deployment of autonomous pallet jacks capable of moving 2,000lb+ loads. These units will handle long-haul transport between receiving, sharpening, and shipping, reducing manual forklift traffic by 80%.

Pathway B

Vertical Storage (VLMs)

High-density automated storage for smaller blades and parts. VLMs utilize vertical height to reclaim 85% of floor space while presenting heavy items at an ergonomic "Golden Zone" height for operators.
