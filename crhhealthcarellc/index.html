CRH Healthcare Portfolio

Robotics & Automation Feasibility Study | Urgent Care Optimization

Executive Summary

CRH Healthcare operates in a high-velocity environment where throughput is the primary revenue driver. Our analysis identifies Lab Automation and Autonomous Disinfection as the critical levers to reduce door-to-door times and increase patient census.

Organizational Profile

Brands: Peachtree Immediate Care, Patients First

Scale: 80-100+ Clinics (SE USA)

Economics: Volumetric Model (~£125/visit)

Goal: Minimize non-value-added clinical time.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A - Highest ROI

Benchtop Lab Automation

Automated rapid-diagnostic platforms ("Lab-in-a-box") to handle Flu, Strep, and COVID testing.

£6.3M
Net Annual Benefit
  • Reduces result wait-time by 5-7 mins
  • Frees up Medical Assistant capacity
  • Payback period: 3-6 months
Pathway B - Safety

Autonomous Disinfection

UV-C Robots for nightly deep cleans and rapid room turnover between infectious patients.

10 Min
Saved Per Room Turn
  • Guarantees sterility
  • Reduces chemical costs
  • Increases patient confidence
Pathway C - Strategic

Telepresence Triage

Robotic kiosks/carts to load-balance providers across the network during demand surges.

Provider Utilization +15%
  • Reduces "Left Without Being Seen"
  • Virtual specialist consults

Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Diagnostic Efficiency
Pilot benchtop automation in top 10 volume clinics. Integrate with DocuTAP EMR.
Phase 2: Environmental Safety
Deploy UV-C robots to "Hub" locations. Market safety standards to employers.
Phase 3: Network Balancing
Telepresence rollout to optimize provider float pool.

Strategic Positioning

Adopting this portfolio transitions CRH from a standard urgent care operator to a Tech-Enabled Health Logistics Network.

Key Outcome: Defensive moat against competitors via lower cost-per-visit and higher throughput capacity.
