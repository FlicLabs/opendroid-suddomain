Associated Grocers of New England

Strategic Robotics Integration & Automation Roadmap

01. Executive Summary

AGNE stands at a pivotal junction. The transition from manual-heavy workflows to a Hybrid Automation Model is essential to combat regional labor shortages and ensure 24/7 operational capability. Our strategy focuses on high-impact interventions: reducing travel time via AMRs and securing the cold chain via automated piece-picking.

02. Org Profile

Pembroke, NH Distribution Center

As the largest retailer-owned wholesale grocery distribution center in New England, the facility manages high-complexity SKUs ranging from dry goods to perishables. The cooperative model demands that efficiency gains translate directly to member retailer profitability.

03. Core Pathways

  • Pathway A: AMRs
    Automated pallet transport to reduce 'dead walking time'.
  • Pathway B: Cold Chain ASRS
    Robotic picking in frozen zones to improve safety and accuracy.
  • Pathway C: AI Logistics
    Predictive inbound routing for New England weather patterns.

05. Implementation Roadmap

YEAR 1: FOUNDATION

Site mapping, WMS integration, and deployment of initial 10-unit AMR fleet for internal transport.

YEAR 2: COLD CHAIN EXPANSION

Implementation of robotic piece-picking arms in frozen storage zones to reduce human environmental exposure.

YEAR 3-5: OPTIMIZATION

AI-driven predictive stocking based on seasonal data and exploration of autonomous last-mile delivery to rural members.
