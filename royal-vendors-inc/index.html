1. Executive Summary

Royal Vendors Inc. stands at a pivotal juncture. The transition from legacy discrete manufacturing to "Intelligent Flexibility" is essential to meet the demand for customized vending experiences. By integrating cobots for high-precision assembly and AMRs for material flow, Royal Vendors can mitigate labor risks in West Virginia while achieving superior quality yields. This strategy shifts the operational model from linear batching to agile, modular production.

2. Organizational Profile & Context

HQ & Facility: Kearns, West Virginia

Production Type: Discrete Manufacturing (High Mix / Med-High Volume)

Core Processes: Sheet metal fabrication, powder coating, foam injection, refrigeration assembly, electronics integration.

Current Bottlenecks: Manual transport of heavy chassis, ergonomic strain in deck assembly, and visual inspection consistency.

3. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobots

Focus: Machine Tending & Safety.

Deployment of heavy-payload cobots to tend press brakes and assist in sub-assembly torquing, reducing operator strain.

Pathway B: AMRs

Focus: WIP Flow Efficiency.

Autonomous Mobile Robots to transport large vending chassis between welding and painting, eliminating forklift congestion.

Pathway C: EOL Automation

Focus: Throughput & Yield.

Automated optical inspection for paint quality combined with heavy-duty robotic palletizing for final shipment prep.

4. Financial Projection Models

CAPEX: Est. $60k - $120k per robotic cell.

Payback: < 18 Months driven by labor reallocation and scrap reduction.

5. Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Digital Twin Simulation & Pilot Cobot Deployment (Fabrication).
  • Year 2: AMR Fleet Integration for Material Transport.
  • Year 3: End-of-Line Automation (Inspection & Palletizing) & Predictive AI.
  • Year 4: Full ERP Synchronization & Smart Factory Status.
  • Year 5: "Dark Factory" exploration for overnight fabrication shifts.
