Key Robotic Pathways

01. Executive Summary

Vermeer Southeast stands at the threshold of a "Service 4.0" transformation. By integrating flexible robotics into the Maintenance, Repair, and Operations (MRO) workflow, the entity can transcend traditional dealership limitations. The strategy focuses on offloading low-value material transport and heavy manipulation to autonomous systems, thereby maximizing the utilization of skilled technicians and accelerating service turnaround.

03. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Collaborative Machine Tending

Deployment of Cobots (UR10e/Fanuc CRX) to assist technicians with heavy lifting, track tensioning, and hydraulic cylinder manipulation. Focus: Safety & Ergonomics.

Pathway B: AMR Intralogistics

Utilization of Autonomous Mobile Robots (MiR250) to transport parts from inventory directly to service bays, eliminating technician "walking time." Focus: WIP Efficiency.

Pathway C: Automated QC & Kitting

Vision-based systems for scanning rental fleet returns and automated kitting of high-volume wear parts. Focus: Throughput Quality.

05. Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pilot AMR deployment in flagship service center.
Year 2: Scale to top 3 locations; WMS integration.
Year 3: Predictive maintenance scanning for rental fleet.
Year 5: Full Smart Service maturity with 40% logistics automation.
