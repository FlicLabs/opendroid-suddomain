Executive Summary

Indiana Donor Network (IDN) coordinates organ, tissue, and eye donation across 85 counties. The organization is uniquely positioned to leverage Autonomous Aerial Logistics and Clinical Robotics. By automating middle-mile logistics (drones) and tissue processing, IDN can reduce ischemia time, increase organ yield by utilizing marginal grafts via robotic perfusion, and lower transport costs by 30-40%.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Aerial Logistics

  • Scope: Heavy-lift UAS for organ transport.
  • Impact: Cut transport costs by ~40%; reduce discard rates.
  • Status: High Strategic Priority.

Pathway B: Tissue Automation

  • Scope: Robotic compounding & sterilization.
  • Impact: 25% throughput increase; standardized graft quality.
  • Status: Operational Efficiency.
