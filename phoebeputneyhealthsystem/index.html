Phoebe Putney Health System

Robotics Research & ROI Opportunities Portfolio

Executive Summary

Phoebe Putney Health System (PPHS) anchors Southwest Georgia healthcare. Facing the dual challenge of managing complex operational logistics and retaining specialized surgical volume, PPHS is primed for automation. The strongest levers are: (1) Robotic Surgical Expansion, (2) Autonomous Mobile Logistics, and (3) Advanced Simulation.

Key ROI Driver: Projected annual net benefit of $4M - $8M upon maturity through surgical case retention and labor optimization.
Organizational Profile
Model: Non-profit Regional Health System (Level II Trauma)
Scale: 3,000+ employees, 600+ beds, Dedicated Simulation Center
Market: Dominant share in SW Georgia; Rural/High-Acuity Mix

Operational Context: Campus-style infrastructure creates logistical friction. High value placed on technologies that improve staff retention and reduce agency labor costs.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways
Pathway A: Surgical Robotics

Scope: Expand Da Vinci (Soft Tissue) and Mako (Ortho) fleets to solidify regional dominance.

Impact: 20-30% increase in case volume; reduced Length of Stay (LOS).

$1.5M - $3.5M Incremental Margin

Pathway B: Intralogistics

Scope: Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for linen, pharmacy, and waste transport.

Impact: Reduction in non-clinical steps; mitigation of support staff shortages.

12-15 FTE Hours Saved Daily

Pathway C: Simulation

Scope: High-fidelity robotic manikins in the Phoebe Simulation Center.

Impact: Accelerated nurse onboarding; improved retention and patient safety.

Strategic Workforce Retention

Implementation Roadmap
  • Year 1: Procure next-gen Ortho robotics; Pilot 3-5 AMRs (Pharmacy loop).
  • Year 2: Expand AMR fleet to dietary/linen; Integrate surgical analytics.
  • Year 3: Evaluate surgical robotics for Phoebe Sumter; Full AI-Sim integration.
Strategic Positioning

Executing this portfolio transitions Phoebe Putney from a regional provider to a tech-enabled smart hospital system. This strategy directly combats rural labor shortages via automation while offering the high-tech surgical capabilities necessary to compete with Atlanta-based academic centers.
