OPEN DROIDS
Client: Oklahoma Aquarium
Executive Summary

Robotics Analysis & ROI Model

The Oklahoma Aquarium (Jenks, OK) operates a high-volume facility with distinct challenges in large-predator habitat maintenance. By shifting from manual scuba labor to automated systems, the organization can significantly reduce risk and operational costs.

Primary Opportunity: Automated underwater hull cleaning robots for the "Shark Adventure" exhibit, reducing diver exposure to Bull Sharks and saving ~1,000 labor hours annually.
Organizational Profile

Type: Non-Profit 501(c)(3)

Scale: 72,000 sq ft / 500k+ Gallons

Key Asset: Largest collection of Bull Sharks globally.

Pain Point: High-risk manual cleaning of acrylic tunnels and substrates.

Strategic Pathways

Pathway A Automated Tank Maintenance: Deployment of industrial underwater scrubbers to maintain 24/7 clarity and reduce diver risk.

Pathway B Telepresence Education: Roving robots for virtual field trips, generating revenue from remote schools globally.

Pathway C Env. Monitoring: Automated sensor arrays for real-time water quality assurance (pH, Salinity, Nitrates).
