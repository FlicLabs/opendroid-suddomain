CharterCARE Health Partners

Robotics Opportunities Portfolio & ROI Analysis

Executive Summary

CharterCARE Health Partners operates as a coordinated regional care network in Rhode Island, comprising Roger Williams Medical Center, Our Lady of Fatima Hospital, and CharterCARE Home Health. Facing competition from large academic systems and tight margins typical of safety-net models, CharterCARE is uniquely positioned to leverage automation.

Primary Levers: (1) Surgical Robotics to defend high-margin service lines, (2) Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for logistics efficiency, and (3) Home Health Automation for readmission reduction.

Organizational Profile

Scale & Reach

2 Acute Care Hospitals (~500 beds), extensive Home Health network, and cancer center capabilities serving Rhode Island.

Economic Model

Safety-net / Community system profile with heavy reliance on Medicare/Medicaid and value-based capitation contracts.

Operational Focus

"Coordinated Regional Care" model focusing on transitions of care and reducing total cost of care.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Surgical Robotics Expansion

Goal: Revenue Defense. Upgrade Da Vinci systems and introduce Orthopedic robotics (Mako/VELYS).

Impact: Retention of 50-75 complex cases/year; ~$2.0M incremental margin.

Pathway B: Logistics & Service Automation

Goal: Operational Efficiency. Deploy TUG/Aethon robots for linen, pharmacy, and dietary transport.

Impact: Save ~15k labor hours; Reduce staff burnout and injury.

Pathway C: Home Health Automation

Goal: Readmission Reduction. Robotic medication dispensers and remote monitoring for CHF/COPD.

Impact: Cost avoidance in risk-based contracts; extended hospital-at-home capacity.

Financial Model: Logistics (Pathway B)

Analysis based on deploying a fleet of 8-10 Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) across two campuses.

Net Annual Benefit: £200k - £300k
Payback Period: 18 - 24 Months
Capital Expenditure ~£350,000 (Fleet + Integration)
Labor Savings ~7.0 FTE redeployed
5-Year NPV ~£1.2 Million

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Upgrade surgical robotics at Roger Williams (Bariatric COE); Pilot pharmacy robots at Fatima.
  • Year 2: Launch Orthopedic robotics; Expand logistics robots to dietary/linens; Pilot home health dispensers.
  • Year 3-5: EHR Integration for outcome tracking; Semi-autonomous security patrols.
