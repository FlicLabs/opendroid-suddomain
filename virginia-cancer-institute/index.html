Client: Virginia Cancer Institute

Executive Summary

Virginia Cancer Institute (VCI) is a leading private oncology and hematology practice serving Central Virginia. As demand for precision oncology grows, the integration of robotics offers a transformative solution. The strongest automation levers are Robotic Automated Compounding Systems (RACS) for chemotherapy and Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for logistics. The dominant ROI driver is the reduction of hazardous drug waste and the optimization of skilled pharmacy labor.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pathway A Pilot

Select highest volume infusion center. Install IV compounding robot and integrate with oncology EMR. Validate gravimetric accuracy.

Year 2: Expansion & Logistics

Roll out compounding robotics to secondary sites. Introduce AMRs at pilot site for "last mile" delivery to infusion chairs.

Year 3: Network Optimization

Full integration of inventory analytics. Centralized dashboard for all robotic assets across the VCI network.
