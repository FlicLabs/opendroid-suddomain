GlobalMed Telemedicine

Robotics Analysis & ROI Model

Executive Summary

GlobalMed is a vertically integrated provider of virtual care solutions, serving the DoD, rural health networks, and over 4,000 healthcare institutions. The primary robotics opportunity lies in transforming static telemedicine carts into Autonomous Mobile Telepresence (AMT) units. By integrating autonomous mobile robot (AMR) bases, GlobalMed can eliminate the labor burden on nursing staff, reduce time-to-consult, and significantly increase physician throughput. The dominant ROI driver is the reclamation of nursing hours currently spent on transport and setup.

Pathway A: Autonomous Logistics

Scope: Integration of clinical stations onto AMR bases for independent navigation.

Impact: Recovers 15-20 mins nursing labor per consult. 30% reduction in hardware idle time.

ROI: High Risk: Low

Pathway B: Clinical Robotics

Scope: Robotic arms (cobots) for remote ultrasound and diagnostic manipulation.

Impact: Expands serviceable markets to unstaffed remote sites. Standardizes diagnostics.

ROI: Med Risk: High

Pathway C: Internal Automation

Scope: Manufacturing and supply chain robotics in Scottsdale facility.

Impact: Reduces COGS by 10-15%. Accelerates production for fleet rollout.

ROI: Med Risk: Low
