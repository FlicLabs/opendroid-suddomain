Executive Summary

Towill Inc. faces a strategic necessity to transition from labor-intensive workflows to a flexible, automated Cyber-Physical System. Our research indicates that deploying collaborative robotics will decouple labor costs from production capacity, aiming for a 25% production yield increase within 24 months.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Collaborative Machine Tending

High Priority

Deploying UR10e or Fanuc CRX Cobots to handle CNC loading/unloading. Allows skilled machinists to manage 3-4 machines simultaneously.

Pathway B: Autonomous Logistics (AMR)

Utilizing AMRs for line-side replenishment. Eliminates non-value-added walking time, saving approx. 450 man-hours annually per shift.

Pathway C: Automated Quality & Palletizing

Vision-guided robotic arms for final inspection and packaging to standardize freight costs and reduce shipping errors.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot Pathway A on two CNC machines & Data Collection.
  • Year 2: Scale Cobots to 50% of shop floor & MES Integration.
  • Year 3: Full "Lights Out" capability for simple components.
  • Year 4-5: AI Predictive Maintenance & Supply Chain Integration.
