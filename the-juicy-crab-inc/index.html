Robotics Strategy Report

THE JUICY CRAB INC.
Prepared by: Open Droids
Focus: Hospitality & Restaurant Automation

Executive Summary

The integration of robotics into The Juicy Crab's operations represents a strategic pivot from traditional labor models to a hybrid automated workforce. The vision focuses on augmenting human staff to handle complex hospitality needs while delegating repetitive, high-risk, and logistically heavy tasks to autonomous systems. This approach aims to mitigate labor volatility, enhance food safety, and drive significant throughput increases. The core objective is to create a seamless ecosystem where robots handle the "heavy lifting" (literal and figurative), allowing human staff to elevate the guest experience.

Organizational Profile

Business Model: The Juicy Crab operates in the fast-casual sector with a focus on Cajun-Asian seafood boils. The current service model is a hybrid of Quick Service (counter ordering) and Full Service (table delivery).

  • Primary Challenge: Inconsistent kitchen throughput and physically demanding front-of-house logistics (heavy, spill-prone food bags).
  • Target Audience: Family dining and large groups, requiring high-volume order accuracy and consistent flavor profiles.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Front-of-House Serving

Table Turns Staff Support

Deployment of Autonomous Delivery Vehicles (ADVs) to transport heavy seafood bags from kitchen to table. Reduces server fatigue and mitigates spill/burn risks.

Pathway B: Back-of-House Automation

Consistency Safety

Automated fry stations for sides (fries, calamari). Ensures precise cook times and temperatures, eliminating oil-burn injuries and food waste.

Pathway C: AI Ordering

Upselling Accuracy

AI Kiosks and Voice Agents for drive-thru. Algorithmic upselling of sides and drinks (e.g., "Add corn?") without order fatigue.

Financial Model & ROI

Investment Structure

  • CAPEX Range: $15k - $40k per robot unit. A full location package (2 Serving, 1 Fry, 2 Kiosks) estimates $50k - $90k.
  • OPEX Impact: Direct reduction in hourly labor costs. Target: 15-25% reduction in Front-of-House labor spend.
  • Maintenance: Estimated 5-8% of CAPEX annually.

Payback Strategy

  • Labor Savings: Reallocating 1 FTE equivalent per shift across operating hours.
  • Throughput Gain: Faster table turns via robotic runners.
  • Revenue Lift: 12%+ increase in average check size via AI upselling.
  • Target Payback: < 14 months.

Implementation Roadmap (Year 1 - Year 5)

  • Year 1 (Pilot & Validation): Deployment of AI Kiosks (Pathway C) and Serving Robots (Pathway A) at 5 pilot locations. Focus on data collection and workflow adjustment.
  • Year 2 (Back-of-House Integration): Introduction of Automated Fry Stations (Pathway B) to pilot sites. Standardization of kitchen-side robotics.
  • Year 3 (Regional Rollout): Expansion to 30% of the portfolio. Establishment of a centralized monitoring command center for uptime analytics.
  • Year 4 (National Standardization): 75% of locations equipped with the full automation package. Standardized maintenance contracts and GM technical training.
  • Year 5 (Ecosystem Maturity): 100% of new franchise builds include automation as a baseline requirement. Evaluation of next-gen fully autonomous cooking systems.
