Executive Summary & Profile

Vision: Perma Pure is positioned to pivot from manual-heavy assembly to a flexible, high-mix automated ecosystem. By leveraging Nafion technology with Industry 4.0 precision, the Lakewood facility can meet surging demands in medical and hydrogen sectors.

Organizational Context: Currently operating a hybrid extrusion/assembly model. The primary challenge is handling delicate polymer membranes where human error leads to high-value scrap. Automation offers a path to stabilize yield and throughput.

PATHWAY A

Cobot Assembly

Focus: Safety & Precision

Deployment of force-sensitive collaborative robots for the insertion of Nafion tubing into dryer headers. Reduces ergonomic strain and prevents micro-damage to membranes.

PATHWAY B

AMR Transport

Focus: WIP Flow Efficiency

Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) handling material transfer between extrusion, clean rooms, and packaging. Eliminates manual carrying of sub-assemblies.

PATHWAY C

Vision & Palletizing

Focus: Throughput & Yield

End-of-line systems utilizing vision (Cognex/Keyence) for seal integrity checks, followed by automated palletizing to remove bottlenecks at shipping.

Implementation Roadmap

Y1
Pilot Phase: Audit Lakewood workflows and deploy first two Cobot assembly cells for MD Series.
Y2
Expansion: Deploy AMRs for floor transport and integrate IIoT dashboards for real-time visibility.
Y3
Optimization: Scale automation to lower-volume high-mix lines; implement predictive maintenance.
Y4-5
Full Autonomy: ERP integration with robotics; automated inventory reordering; evaluation of "lights-out" shifts.
