Horizon House

Robotics Consultation & ROI Analysis Portfolio

Executive Summary

Horizon House, a premier urban CCRC in Seattle, faces challenges unique to vertical campuses: elevator logistics, dining throughput, and labor shortages. This analysis identifies high-impact opportunities in Autonomous Hospitality Logistics and Ambient Fall Detection. The strategy focuses on shifting staff from low-value transport tasks to high-value resident interactions.

Core Opportunity: Integrating autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) for dining and vertical transport to generate ~£115k-£200k in annual labor efficiencies.
Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Service Logistics

Highest Impact

Scope: Autonomous dining runners and vertical delivery bots.

Impact: Reduces staff travel by 60-70%. Handles peak dining volume with fewer runners. Repurposes labor to direct resident care.

Payback: 6-9 Months

Pathway B: Ambient Safety

Strategic Value

Scope: Radar-based fall detection (no cameras/wearables) in suites.

Impact: 20-30% reduction in hospitalizations. Lowers liability premiums. Extends Independent Living tenure.

Payback: 18-24 Months

Pathway C: Cognitive Support

Niche Value

Scope: Social robots (Paro) and telepresence in Memory Care.

Impact: Reduces agitation/medication use. Improves family connection. Marketing differentiator.

Payback: Indirect

Pathway D: Vertical Infrastructure

Enabler

Scope: Smart elevator integration APIs.

Impact: Essential prerequisite for campus-wide logistics. Prevents robots from monopolizing elevators during resident peak times.

Payback: Embedded

Implementation Roadmap
1

Year 1: Dining Pilot

Deploy 2-4 food runner robots in main dining venue. Validate elevator integration protocols.

2

Year 1-2: Safety Retrofit

Pilot ambient fall detection sensors in 20 high-risk apartments. Gather data to justify full rollout.

3

Year 3: Heavy Logistics

Deploy autonomous tugs for back-of-house laundry and waste transport to reduce staff injuries.

Strategic Positioning

Adopting this roadmap positions Horizon House as the Technological Leader in Seattle Senior Living. It transforms the "vertical challenge" of a high-rise into a logistics advantage through automation, ensuring staff energy is preserved for what matters most: human connection and clinical excellence.
