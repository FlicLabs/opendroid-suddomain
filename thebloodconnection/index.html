The Blood Connection

Robotics Opportunities Portfolio & ROI Analysis

Executive Summary: The Blood Connection (TBC) operates a critical biological supply chain serving 100+ hospitals. The most potent automation levers involve lab processing throughput and phlebotomy labor optimization. Our model projects millions in retained value through yield protection and efficiency.

Organizational Profile

Scale & Reach

Serves SC, NC, and GA with 100+ hospital partners. Collects hundreds of thousands of units annually via mobile bloodmobiles and fixed centers.

Core Operations

Donor recruitment, whole blood/apheresis collection, component manufacturing (RBC, Plasma, Platelets), and cold-chain logistics.

Key Pain Points

Phlebotomist staffing shortages, manual handling risks in labs, and strict FDA temperature compliance during processing.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Lab Automation Highest ROI

Scope: Total Laboratory Automation (TLA) for sorting, decapping, and centrifugation.

Impact: 25-30% throughput increase and reduction of 4-6 FTEs per shift.

£0.8M - £1.1M
Net Annual Benefit
Pathway B: Robotic Phlebotomy Strategic

Scope: Autonomous venipuncture devices (e.g., Vitestro/Veebot) in fixed centers.

Impact: Solves "hard stick" issues, improves donor retention, and multiplies staff capacity.

Staff Multiplier
1 Nurse : 3 Robots
Pathway C: Internal AMRs Logistics

Scope: Autonomous Mobile Robots for moving blood cassettes in the manufacturing hub.

Impact: Ensures strict cold-chain compliance and digital audit trails; reduces walking time.

Compliance
100% Audit Trail
