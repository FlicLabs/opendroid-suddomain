Executive Summary

This strategy proposes a high-level vision for robotic cleaning automation, transitioning from manual labor to a hybrid autonomous model. The focus is on achieving unprecedented hygiene consistency and operational efficiency through targeted robotics pathways.

By integrating autonomous systems, the airline will mitigate labor shortages and standardize quality, ensuring a competitive edge in aviation maintenance.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Robots vacuuming, wiping, and collecting trash during tight turnarounds. Reduces ground time significantly.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Automated sanitization cycles using UV-C light to eliminate pathogens. Boosts passenger confidence.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Automated gantry systems for eco-friendly fuselage cleaning. Saves water and reduces risk.

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Drones and crawlers for micro-fracture detection. Enhances safety compliance.

Implementation Roadmap (5-Year Strategy)

Year 1: Pilot Phase

Single hub deployment. Testing Cabin Cleaning (Pathway A) and Inspection Bots (Pathway D). Focus on data collection.

Year 2: Evaluation & Scaling

Refine protocols. Expansion to 50% of pilot hub fleet. Introduction of UV-C Disinfection (Pathway B).

Year 3: Multi-Hub Expansion

Rollout to second major hub. Deploy Exterior Washing (Pathway C). 25% total fleet coverage.

Year 4: Full Integration

Standardize workflows across all hubs. Integrate AI data streams. 70% fleet coverage.

Year 5: Optimization

Fully autonomous operations. Predictive maintenance schedules. 100% fleet coverage.
