OPEN DROIDS
Consultation Report: Stein LTC

Executive Summary

Stein LTC faces critical industry pressures regarding staffing shortages and operational costs in the skilled nursing sector. Our analysis identifies Logistics Automation (AMRs) and Robotic Patient Transfer as high-impact interventions. By offloading non-clinical tasks, Stein LTC can reclaim thousands of labor hours annually, reduce staff injury rates, and significantly improve resident care quality.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Logistics AI

Deployment of Autonomous Mobile Robots for dietary, laundry, and supply transport to reduce staff walking time.

Pathway B: Transfer Assist

Powered exoskeletons and smart lifts to mitigate back injuries during patient transfer and improve safety.

Pathway C: Social Robotics

AI-driven companions for memory care to reduce resident isolation and improve cognitive engagement.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Pilot launch of dietary transport AMRs in primary wing. Map creation and kitchen staff training.
  • Phase 2 (Months 4-12): Expansion to laundry logistics and introduction of robotic patient lifts in high-acuity units.
  • Phase 3 (Year 2+): Full integration of social robotics in memory care and Building Management System (BMS) data integration.
