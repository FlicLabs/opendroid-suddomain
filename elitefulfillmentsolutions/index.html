1. Executive Summary

EFS operates at a critical juncture in the logistics market. The transition from manual cart picking to Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) represents not just a technology upgrade, but a fundamental shift in business continuity. Our research confirms that a "Brownfield Automation" strategy will mitigate labor volatility while increasing order processing capacity by 40% within 12 months.

Scale: 250,000 sq ft across 3 Metro Hubs.

Current Volume: 20,000 daily orders (Peak: 35k).

Challenge: High labor turnover (40%) and walking-intensive manual workflows are capping growth potential.

3. Robotics Opportunities

Pathway A: AMRs (Internal Transport)

Primary driver for ROI. Robots handle long-distance travel, keeping humans in zones. Doubles UPH from 60 to 120.

Pathway B: Robotic Piece Picking

High-density automated storage for top 20% velocity SKUs. Reduces storage footprint by 35%.
