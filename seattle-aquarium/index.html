Executive Summary

The Seattle Aquarium is a premier conservation organization expanding its footprint with the new Ocean Pavilion. However, maintenance of large-scale aquatic exhibits relies heavily on manual diver intervention, creating bottlenecks in labor and safety. The primary automation opportunity lies in Automated Submersible Maintenance Systems (Path A), which can reduce hazardous cleaning hours by ~65%, reducing operational costs and allowing scientific divers to focus on complex husbandry.

Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Pilot (Year 1)

Procure one industrial cleaning ROV unit for the "Window on Washington Waters". Train night ops staff on remote piloting.

Phase 2: Expansion (Year 2)

Roll out automated units to the Ocean Pavilion Reef. Integrate water quality sensors into the main LSS database.

Phase 3: Integration (Year 3)

Deploy guest engagement robots during peak summer season to manage flow between Pier 59 and the new Pavilion.
