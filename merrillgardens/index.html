Merrill Gardens

Robotics Opportunity Portfolio & ROI Analysis

Executive Summary

Merrill Gardens is uniquely positioned to adopt a robotics strategy that enhances its high-touch "Anytime Dining" service model. The primary automation levers are Dining Robotics, Autonomous Floor Care, and Fall Detection. This portfolio projects a potential $2.5M+ annual portfolio-wide impact by shifting staff focus from logistics to resident connection.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

HIGHEST VALUE

Pathway A: Dining Logistics

Deployment of autonomous runners/bussers to support the "Anytime Dining" model. Allows servers to stay on the floor with residents rather than running to the kitchen.

Impact: +30-40% Table Capacity per Server
EFFICIENCY

Pathway B: Facility Care

Autonomous scrubbers and vacuums for expansive common areas, lobbies, and corridors. Ensures consistent cleanliness standards during off-hours.

Impact: Reallocates housekeeping to deep cleaning
SAFETY

Pathway C: Fall Prevention

Radar/Optical fall detection systems in memory care. Reduces "long lie" times and hospitalization rates, improving length-of-stay.

Impact: Liability premium reduction (3-5yr)

Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Validation (Months 1-3) Pilot dining robots at 3 flagship Seattle communities. Stress-test "Anytime Dining" workflow integration.
Phase 2: Standardization (Months 4-12) Rollout to top 50% of sites by volume. Pilot cleaning bots in large-footprint locations.
Phase 3: Integration (Year 2+) Full fleet deployment. Install fall detection in Memory Care. Connect robot data to corporate dashboards.
