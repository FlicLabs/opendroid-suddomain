Executive Summary

The adoption of robotic automation represents a transformative leap for Strike Aviation Group, positioning the airline at the forefront of operational efficiency and safety standards. By integrating autonomous systems into cleaning and maintenance workflows, Strike Aviation can significantly reduce turnaround times, ensure consistent hygiene protocols, and optimize labor allocation.

This strategic shift moves the organization from reactive manual labor to proactive, data-driven maintenance. The vision is to create a seamless, automated ecosystem where aircraft are cleaned, inspected, and ready for service with minimal human intervention, ensuring maximum fleet availability.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

A. Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Mobile robots for vacuuming and wiping. Reduces turnaround time by 50%.

B. UV-C Disinfection

Post-cleaning sanitization for high-touch surfaces. Medical-grade hygiene.

C. Exterior Washing

Automated gantry systems. Reduces water usage by 30% and safety risks.

D. AI Inspection Bots

Thermal imaging and HD cameras for predictive maintenance.

Labor Hour Redistribution

Shift in Focus: Post-automation, ground crews transition from repetitive cleaning tasks to specialized maintenance and customer service roles.

Implementation Roadmap (5 Years)

PHASE 1: Pilot Program (Months 1-12)
Hub: Singapore Changi (SIN)

Deployment of 5 units (Cabin Cleaning & UV-C). Baseline KPI establishment and staff training.

PHASE 2: Hub Expansion (Months 13-24)
Hubs: Dubai (DXB) & London (LHR)

Scale fleet coverage to 40%. Introduction of Exterior Washing Systems.

PHASE 3: Fleet Integration (Months 25-36)
System-Wide Integration

60% coverage achieved. AI Inspection Bots rolled out for narrow-body fleet. Data integration.

PHASE 4: Global Standardization (Months 37-48)
Network Optimization

Full exterior washing coverage. Protocol standardization across all stations.

PHASE 5: Autonomous Ecosystem (Months 49-60)
Full Automation

100% standard cleaning automation. Synchronized robot swarms. Transition to supervisory roles.
