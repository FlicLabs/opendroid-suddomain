OPEN DROIDS
Strategic Robotics Analysis for Rooted Hospitality Group

Executive Summary

Rooted Hospitality Group (RHG) is a premier multi-brand restaurant operator based in Long Island, NY, operating high-volume, experiential concepts like RUMBA, COWFISH, and RHUM. Facing the dual challenge of high regional labor costs and the operational complexity of peak seasonal dining, RHG is uniquely positioned to benefit from front-of-house logistics automation.

Our analysis indicates that deploying autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) for food running and bussing can unlock significant value by increasing table turnover, reducing staff fatigue, and improving guest interaction time.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: FOH Logistics & Service Bots

Scope: Deployment of autonomous "runner" and "busser" bots to handle transit between the kitchen pass and dining zones.

Impact: Reduces server step count by 40-60%, allowing staff to remain in sections to upsell and engage guests. Directly improves table turn speed during peak summer rushes.

Pathway B: Culinary & Bar Automation

Scope: Automated beverage dispensing and precision prep robotics (e.g., auto-fryers).

Impact: Standardizes drink consistency, reduces alcohol waste, and speeds up bar ticket times.

Pathway C: Inventory AI

Scope: Computer vision for waste tracking and predictive prep analytics.

Impact: Reduces food waste by 2-5% and optimizes labor scheduling.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pathway A Pilot
    Deploy 4 AMRs at a flagship location (e.g., RUMBA) for a 3-month summer pilot. Validate turn-time reduction.
  • Year 2: Network Rollout & Bar Tech
    Expand AMRs to all high-volume locations. Introduce automated dispensing in high-traffic bars.
  • Year 3: Full Stack Optimization
    Integrate Inventory AI and predictive kitchen scheduling for full BOH/FOH synchronization.
