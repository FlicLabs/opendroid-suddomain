Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Nursing Support

Scope: Fleet of 30-50 Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for delivery of labs, meds, and amenities.

Impact: Returns 20k-30k hours to bedside care annually. Reduces reliance on travel nursing.

Highest ROI

Pathway B: Cardio/Neuro Bots

Scope: Endovascular robotics (e.g., CorPath) for precision PCI and stroke intervention.

Impact: Radiation safety for staff, sub-millimeter precision, and market differentiation.

Strategic Value

Pathway C: Heavy Logistics

Scope: Automated tugs for basement transport of linens, dietary, and waste.

Impact: Reduced workplace injury and labor savings in support services.

Implementation Roadmap
Year 1: Pilot & Stabilization

Deploy 5 units at Flagship (KC) in Neuro-ICU. Optimize mapping. Expand to 20 units by Q4.

Year 2: Integration

Install endovascular robotics in Cath Lab. Deploy heavy tugs for dietary transport.

Year 3: Network Scale

Roll out nursing support bots to community hospitals. Evaluate tele-robotics for rural stroke centers.

Strategic Positioning

By adopting the Automated Care Environment model, Saint Luke's moves beyond simple device usage to systemic workflow automation. This strengthens the system's reputation as a premier employer in the Midwest by removing low-value logistical burdens from high-value clinical staff, ultimately sustaining its leadership in heart and brain health.
