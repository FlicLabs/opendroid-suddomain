AUTOMATION STRATEGY

Strategic Research for Smarte Carte Aviation Operations

Executive Summary

Integration of robotic systems into Smarte Carte operations signifies a shift from manual labor dependency to autonomous efficiency. This strategy leverages UV-C disinfection and autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) to enhance sanitation standards across airport terminals and baggage handling areas. By automating routine tasks, Smarte Carte can reduce operational costs by 35-45% while maintaining high passenger safety standards. The vision is a hybrid workforce where robots handle repetitive sanitation, and human staff manage complex logistics and passenger interaction.

Opportunity Pathways

A. Cabin Cleaning Robots

Autonomous units for trash removal and surface sanitization during quick turnarounds.

B. UV-C Disinfection

Nightly autonomous rounds for touchpoints: lockers, carts, and charging stations.

C. Exterior Washing

Robotic arms for GSE fleets reducing water usage and downtime.

D. AI Inspection Bots

Computer vision for predictive maintenance on baggage carts and smart lockers.

Implementation Roadmap (5 Years)

  • Year 1: Pilot deployment of UV-C robots and AI inspection at MSP hub. Establish baseline metrics.
  • Year 2: Scale UV-C fleet to 3 hubs; introduce exterior washing systems for GSE.
  • Year 3: Deploy Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Robots; smart locker sanitization integration.
  • Year 4: Network-wide rollout to top 10 airports; centralize command center operations.
  • Year 5: Full OPEX target realization; upgrade cycles for next-gen battery tech.
