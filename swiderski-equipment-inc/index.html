Executive Vision

Swiderski Equipment Inc. is positioned to transition from traditional heavy equipment handling to a Smart Manufacturing Hub. By integrating flexible automation into attachment fabrication and remanufacturing, the organization will reduce lead times and mitigate skilled labor shortages.

Focus: High-Mix/Low-Volume (HMLV) optimization via collaborative robotics and data-driven material transport.

Organizational Profile

Sector: Heavy Equipment & Ag-Tech

Process Type: Discrete Manufacturing & Remanufacturing

Current Constraints:

  • High manual forklifts traffic creating safety bottlenecks.
  • Dependency on master welders for repetitive tasks.
  • Manual data logging creating operational lag.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Collaborative Welding (Cobots)

Deployment of IP67-rated Cobots for arc welding and plasma cutting. Allows skilled welders to focus on complex fit-ups while robots handle repetitive "arc-on" time.

Pathway B: AMRs for Material Transport

Heavy-payload Autonomous Mobile Robots to move hydraulic components and engine blocks between bays, eliminating non-value-added walking time.

Pathway C: Automated Inspection

Vision-system gantries for end-of-line inspection of hydraulic hose assemblies and paint quality, ensuring 100% OEM compliance.
