EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report outlines a high-level vision for robotic cleaning automation at Aero Norway AS. By integrating autonomous systems, Aero Norway can achieve superior hygiene standards, optimized turnaround times, and significant operational cost reductions. The strategy focuses on four key pathways: Cabin Cleaning, UV-C Disinfection, Exterior Washing, and AI Inspections. This transition supports the airline's goals of sustainability and digital leadership in the Nordic aviation market.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

Pathway A & B: Cabin & Disinfection

Autonomous cabin cleaners handle vacuuming and waste, while UV-C robots ensure pathogen elimination. This combo reduces chemical use by 20%.

  • Reduced labor for repetitive tasks
  • Enhanced hygiene validation
  • Faster narrow-body turnarounds

Pathway C & D: Exterior & Inspection

Automated washing systems reduce water consumption, while AI inspection bots provide digital traceability for structural checks.

  • Safety improvements for ground ops
  • Early corrosion detection
  • Resource efficiency

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (5-YEAR PHASED DEPLOYMENT)

YEAR 1: PILOT PHASE

Deploy Pilot Units (Cabin & UV-C) at Oslo Hub. Establish baseline metrics and integration with existing MRO software.

YEAR 2: EXPANSION

Scale cabin automation to 50% of fleet. Implement exterior washing systems for cargo operations. Staff training begins.

YEAR 3: OPTIMIZATION

Deploy AI Inspection Bots for wheel wells/cargo. Optimize operational routes using accumulated data.

YEAR 4: INTEGRATION

Full coverage for standard cleaning. Predictive maintenance for the robotic fleet. Digital Twin implementation.

YEAR 5: SUSTAINABILITY

100% eco-friendly solutions. Evaluation of next-gen robotics (engine wash). Full autonomy maturity.
