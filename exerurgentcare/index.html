Exer Urgent Care: Robotics Analysis & ROI Strategy

Executive Summary Exer Urgent Care operates 55+ locations with a high-acuity "ER Alternative" model. The primary robotics opportunity lies in Intelligent Intake Automation and Rapid Lab Diagnostics. By reducing door-to-door times, Exer can unlock an estimated $16M in annual incremental revenue through increased throughput without expanding physical real estate.

Organizational Profile

Type: High-acuity Urgent Care (ER Staffed)
Scale: ~55 Locations (Southern California)
Volume: ~600k Annual Visits (Est.)
Key Partner: Providence Health

Operational bottlenecks focus on wait times during surges, manual registration labor, and room turnaround time due to lab processing and sanitation.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Intake Highest ROI

Concept: Biometric kiosks + AI triage.

Impact: Reduces door-to-provider time by 15 mins. Automates vitals and insurance entry.

Result: +3-4 patients/day per clinic capacity.

Pathway B: Automated Labs Strategic

Concept: Benchtop automated analyzers.

Impact: Faster test results decrease room occupancy time.

Result: Higher room turnover and improved NPS.

Pathway C: Auto-Disinfection Operational

Concept: UV-C Robots & Scrubbers.

Impact: Standardization of cleanliness and labor reduction.

Result: Brand safety & overnight labor savings.

Strategic Positioning

By adopting this portfolio, Exer transitions from a standard service provider to a Tech-Enabled Care Platform. This cements the brand as the "Fastest Path to Care" in SoCal, maximizing the value of the Providence partnership by effectively diverting lower-acuity traffic away from overburdened ERs through superior speed and efficiency.
