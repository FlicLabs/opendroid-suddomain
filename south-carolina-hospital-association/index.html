Executive Summary

The South Carolina Hospital Association (SCHA) is uniquely positioned to drive a statewide robotics strategy to address critical workforce shortages and advance the Zero Harm safety initiative. By aggregating demand across 100+ member hospitals, SCHA can implement fleet-based autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) and telepresence solutions. The dominant ROI driver is the recapture of clinical hours (2-3 hours/nurse/shift), enabling network-wide savings of roughly £25M+ over 5 years.

Pathway A: Logistics & AMRs

Scope: Autonomous transport for linen, meals, and meds to relieve nurses.

Impact: Recapture 100+ FTE equivalent hours state-wide.

Status: Highest Priority

Pathway B: Rural Telepresence

Scope: Remote specialist robots for rural/critical access hospitals.

Impact: Reduced transfers & improved stroke/trauma response.

Status: Strategic Layer

Pathway C: Sterile Processing

Scope: UV-C disinfection and automated instrument tracking.

Impact: Direct support of Zero Harm safety goals.

Status: Quality Enhancer
