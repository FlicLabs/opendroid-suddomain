01. Executive Vision

Sigma Thermal operates in a High-Mix, Low-Volume (HMLV) environment where precision engineering meets heavy fabrication. The Industry 4.0 strategy focuses not on replacing the skilled workforce, but on augmenting human capability through collaborative robotics and autonomous material handling. This shift aims to reduce non-value-added time (NVAT) and ensure delivery velocity for large-scale capital projects.

02. Organizational Profile

Primary Facility: Marietta, GA (HQ)

Process Type: Hybrid (Process Engineering + Discrete Heavy Fabrication).

Bottlenecks Identified:

  • Scarcity of certified pressure vessel welders.
  • Inefficient material flow of large steel plates.
  • Time-intensive manual NDT quality inspections.

03. Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobot Welding
Utilization of long-reach Cobots for seam welding on heater shells and pipe spools to reduce welder fatigue.

Pathway B: Heavy AMRs
Deployment of high-payload Autonomous Mobile Robots for moving sub-assemblies between cutting, fit-up, and painting.

Pathway C: Automated QC
Robotic arms equipped with ultrasonic sensors for rapid weld integrity testing.

Output Analysis: Panel Assembly
