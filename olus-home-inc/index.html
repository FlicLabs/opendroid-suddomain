Robotics Analysis & ROI Portfolio

01. Executive Summary

Olu's Home Inc. faces the critical healthcare industry challenge of staff shortages and burnout in the residential care sector. Our analysis identifies three high-impact robotics levers: Social/Cognitive Assistance for resident engagement, Wearable Exoskeletons for staff safety, and Service Automation for facility hygiene.

The dominant ROI driver is not direct labor substitution, but rather Labor Retention and Injury Cost Avoidance. By reducing physical strain on Direct Support Professionals (DSPs), Olu's Home can save significant capital on turnover and workers' compensation while improving resident outcomes.

02. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Social & Cognitive Assistance

Deployment of AI-driven social robots (e.g., ElliQ, QTrobot) to engage seniors and individuals with developmental disabilities. This reduces behavioral escalations and offloads repetitive social monitoring from clinical staff.

Pathway B: Staff Safety (Exoskeletons)

Implementation of active lumbar support wearables for staff performing transfers. This directly targets the high cost of musculoskeletal injuries and extends the career longevity of older caregivers.

Pathway C: Facility Automation

Autonomous floor scrubbers and dining runners to handle "dull/dirty" tasks, ensuring hygiene compliance and allowing staff to focus purely on resident interaction.
