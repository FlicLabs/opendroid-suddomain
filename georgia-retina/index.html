Executive Summary

Georgia Retina is positioned to transition from a high-volume specialty practice to a technology-enabled regional institute. By leveraging robotic microsurgery and AI-driven diagnostics, the practice can solve critical volume constraints and enhance surgical precision. The dominant ROI driver is increased surgical case volume and the operational efficiency of diagnostic clinics.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Robotic-Assisted Microsurgery

Deployment of robotic surgical assistance platforms specifically designed for intraocular procedures to filter tremors and scale motion.

  • Reduction in iatrogenic retinal trauma.
  • Marketing differentiation as a Center of Excellence.
  • Extension of surgeon operating life by 5-7 years.

Pathway B: AI-Driven Diagnostic Automation

Implementation of automated diagnostic imaging interpretation integrated with robotic OCT positioning systems.

  • Automated pre-screening of OCT scans.
  • 20% reduction in patient wait times.
  • Capacity for 4-6 additional patients per day per provider.

Pathway C: Remote Tele-Ophthalmology

Deployment of semi-autonomous fundus photography kiosks in primary care offices.

  • Capture of asymptomatic diabetic retinopathy patients.
  • Low overhead expansion into rural markets.
