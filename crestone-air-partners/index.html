Executive Summary

Crestone Air Partners stands at the precipice of a technological renaissance. Integrating robotic automation offers a transformative opportunity to redefine operational efficiency, hygiene standards, and cost management. This report outlines a strategic vision where autonomous systems handle repetitive tasks, allowing human crews to focus on complex quality assurance. By deploying a fleet of specialized robots, Crestone can achieve a reduction in operational expenditures, ensure compliance with stringent health protocols, and elevate the passenger experience through consistently pristine cabin environments.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

A. Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

SLAM-enabled AMRs for seat cleaning and debris collection. Ensures 100% coverage consistency.

B. UV-C Disinfection

Autonomous UV-C towers neutralizing pathogens. Reduces chemical reliance and dwell time.

C. Exterior Washing

Automated gantry systems for fuselage cleaning. Improves aerodynamics and reduces corrosion.

D. AI Inspection

Drone/Crawler units with LiDAR for wear-and-tear analysis. Data-driven maintenance planning.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot Phase & Feasibility. Deploy 3 units per pathway at a single hub. Establish KPIs.
  • Year 2: Optimization & Expansion. Refine algorithms. Expand to 50% of primary hub fleet.
  • Year 3: Hub-Wide Standardization. Full deployment at primary hub. Achieve 35% OPEX reduction.
  • Year 4: Multi-Hub Rollout. Replicate model at secondary hubs. Introduce AI-Inspection Bots.
  • Year 5: Fleet-Wide Maturity. 100% coverage. Predictive maintenance fully operational.
