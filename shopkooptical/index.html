Executive Summary

Shopko Optical stands at a critical juncture where precision manufacturing meets logistics. By transforming the Green Bay central facility into a semi-automated hub, the company can secure its competitive edge. The proposed integration of AMRs for lens tray transport and ASRS for frame inventory will drastically reduce cycle times and errors.

PATHWAY A

AMRs for Transport

Deployment of Autonomous Mobile Robots to handle the movement of lens trays between surfacing and finishing labs. This replaces manual cart transport, reducing breakage and idle machine time.

PATHWAY B

Robotic Piece Picking

Implementation of Cube Storage or Vertical Lift Modules (VLMs) for high-density frame and contact lens storage. This maximizes vertical space and ensures 99.9% picking accuracy.

PATHWAY C

AI Logistics

Utilizing predictive modeling to pre-position inventory based on regional fashion trends, significantly reducing expedited shipping costs and ensuring stock availability.

Implementation Roadmap

1
Foundation: Data audit and pilot deployment of 3-5 AMRs for lab transport.
2
Expansion: Installation of VLMs for high-velocity contact lens SKUs.
3
Integration: AI forecasting integration with retail POS systems.
4
Optimization: Full "Lights-Out" capability for storage zones.
