01. Organizational Profile

Republic Plastics operates at the intersection of high-volume process and discrete manufacturing. Headquartered in McQueeney, Texas, the facility faces the challenge of managing massive throughput of lightweight foam tableware. The primary objective is to transition from rigid, energy-intensive lines to flexible, automated ecosystems.

Pathway A: Cobots

Machine Tending & Assembly

Deployment of IP67 Food-Grade Cobots for delicate stacking of thermoformed plates. Reduces ergonomic strain and ensures precise counts.

Pathway B: AMRs

Material Transport

Autonomous Mobile Robots to handle raw polystyrene rolls and finished WIP pallets. Creates "fork-free" safety zones.

Pathway C: QC & EOL

Palletizing & Inspection

High-speed vision systems for defect detection coupled with centralized robotic palletizing for end-of-line efficiency.

02. Financial Model

  • Single Cobot Cell CAPEX $60k - $80k
  • Heavy Payload AMR CAPEX $75k - $95k
  • Palletizing Cell CAPEX $100k - $120k
  • *Target payback period < 18 months via labor reallocation.

03. Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pilot

Launch single Cobot cell at McQueeney. Test AMR Fleet Manager.

Year 2: Integration

Expand packing automation to 40% of lines. Connect OEE Dashboard.

Year 5: Smart Factory

Full "Dark Factory" capability for night shifts with AI Quality Control.
