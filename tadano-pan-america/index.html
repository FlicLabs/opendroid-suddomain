Executive Summary

Tadano Pan America is positioned to revolutionize heavy lifting equipment manufacturing through the adoption of Flexible Manufacturing Systems (FMS). By transitioning from static manual assembly to a dynamic Industry 4.0 model, Tadano can address critical skilled labor shortages in welding while enhancing safety standards.

Our research identifies specific opportunities in high-mix/low-volume production, specifically utilizing Cobots for precision fabrication and AMRs for heavy material transport.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobots

Focus: Safety & Precision

Deployment of collaborative robots for long-seam welding on boom sections and torque tightening on chassis assembly. This ensures 100% compliance with load-bearing safety specs.

Pathway B: AMRs

Focus: WIP Flow Efficiency

Heavy-duty Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) utilizing SLAM navigation to transport hydraulic kits and counterweights, reducing forklift traffic in pedestrian zones.

Pathway C: EOL Automation

Focus: Throughput & Yield

Automated vision systems for paint inspection and hydraulic routing verification, alongside automated kitting for spare parts shipment.
