STRATEGIC AUTOMATION REPORT

National Business Aviation Association (NBAA)

Prepared by Open Droids Senior Consultation & Research

Executive Summary

The National Business Aviation Association stands to revolutionize its operational efficiency through the strategic integration of robotic automation. By deploying autonomous cleaning and inspection systems, NBAA can achieve significant reductions in operational expenditures, enhance hygiene standards, and optimize asset utilization. This report outlines a pathway to a 35-45% reduction in OPEX with a payback period of under 24 months, positioning NBAA as a leader in aviation innovation.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

A

Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Robots

AMRs for vacuuming and surface wiping in member aircraft and facilities. Uses LiDAR for precise navigation in complex environments.

B

UV-C Disinfection Robots

Autonomous UV-C systems for terminals and cabins. Reduces chemical usage and ensures pathogen-free environments.

C

Exterior Aircraft Washing

Automated gantry systems reducing water consumption and paint damage while accelerating turnaround times.

D

AI-Powered Inspection Bots

Ground-based drones and crawlers utilizing thermal sensors and AI to detect structural anomalies pre-emptively.

Implementation Roadmap (5-Year Phased Strategy)

Year 1: Pilot Phase

Deployment of 2 Cabin Cleaning units and 1 UV-C unit at primary hub (KORL). Establishment of baseline metrics.

Year 2: Infrastructure Integration

Expansion to Exterior Washing systems. Facility network upgrades for seamless robot connectivity.

Year 3: Scaling & Optimization

Regional hub expansion. Integration of AI Inspection Bots. Data-driven SOP refinement.

Year 4: Advanced Automation

Full hangar coverage. Integration of robotic data into Maintenance Management Software (MMS).

Year 5: Maturity & Innovation

100% facility coverage. Predictive maintenance model realization. Swarm robotics exploration.
