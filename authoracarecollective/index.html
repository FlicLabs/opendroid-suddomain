AuthoraCare Collective

Robotics Research & ROI Analysis

Executive Summary

AuthoraCare Collective, a leading non-profit hospice and palliative care provider in NC, is uniquely positioned to leverage bio-behavioral robotics and logistical automation. By shifting focus from acute intervention to quality of life augmentation, the organization can generate significant ROI through pharmacy cost avoidance (reduction in antipsychotics), improved staff retention, and reduced after-hours crisis visits.

Organizational Profile

Model: Comprehensive end-of-life care, hospice, palliative medicine, and grief support.

Scale: Serving Guilford, Alamance, Caswell, Rockingham, and Randolph counties. Thousands of annual patients.

Economic Driver: Capitated per-diem reimbursement. Financial success relies on managing the daily cost of care while maximizing patient comfort.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways
HIGHEST IMPACT Pathway A: Companion Robotics

Deployment of FDA-cleared therapeutic robotic pets (e.g., PARO) for dementia and agitation management.

Pharmacy Savings 15-20%
Payback Period < 9 Months
EFFICIENCY Pathway B: Telepresence

Remote "tuck-in" visits and specialist consults via mobile robots to reduce travel to rural counties.

Visit Capacity +25%
Payback Period 12-18 Months
ADMINISTRATION Pathway C: Admin RPA

Software robotics to automate referral intake, compliance auditing, and claims processing.

Processing Time -40%
Payback Period 6 Months
Financial Model: Pathway A Focus

Scenario: Deployment of 50 therapeutic units for dementia cohort.

$40k Est. Initial CAPEX
$100k Annual Net Benefit
High Staff Retention Impact

*Financials driven by reduction in PRN medication spend and avoidance of costly after-hours nursing crisis visits.

Implementation Roadmap
Year 1: Pilot & Process
Procure 20 companion robots for dementia team; implement intake RPA.
Year 2: Expansion
Roll out telepresence to rural counties (Caswell/Rockingham); expand robot fleet to inpatient units.
Year 3: Integration
Full EMR integration of remote monitoring data; explore physical assist robotics for aides.
