New Vista KY

Robotics & Automation Opportunities Portfolio

Executive Summary

New Vista is a vital Community Mental Health Center (CMHC) serving 17 counties in Central Kentucky. Facing staffing shortages and high administrative burdens in residential care, New Vista is uniquely positioned to leverage automation.

The strategic focus identifies Medication Management Automation, Socially Assistive Robotics (SAR), and Telepresence as key drivers to improve patient safety, optimize nursing labor, and extend clinical reach into rural areas.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Meds Automation Highest ROI

Scope: Automated dispensing cabinets and packaging robotics in residential/detox units.

Impact
40-50% Nurse Time Savings

Eliminates counting errors and diversion risks while freeing nurses for direct patient care.

Pathway B: Social Robotics

Scope: Humanoid robots for Autism & IDD therapy curricula.

Impact
Capacity Multiplier

Delivers consistent social skills training and reduces anxiety for neurodivergent clients.

Pathway C: Telepresence

Scope: Mobile robots for rural clinics and crisis response.

Impact
Immediate Access

Connects remote counties to central psychiatrists instantly, reducing unnecessary ER transports.

Financial Model: Pathway A

Scenario: Deployment of automated dispensing across 5 residential sites.

Metric Value
Capital Investment $150k - $200k (Hardware + Implementation)
Annual Labor Savings ~$157,500 (4,500 nursing hours recaptured)
Risk Avoidance ~$50,000 / year (Fines & Diversion)
Payback Period 12 - 14 Months

Implementation Roadmap

1
Year 1: Pilot & Stabilize
Deploy Medication Automation in one high-volume substance use facility. Pilot 2 social robots in the Lexington IDD center.
2
Year 2-3: Network Expansion
Roll out automated dispensing to all residential units. Deploy telepresence robots to the 3 most rural county offices.
3
Year 3-5: Integration
Link robotic data feeds to EHR for population health analytics. Explore fall detection sensors for geriatric housing.

Strategic Positioning

Adopting these technologies transforms New Vista from a labor-constrained provider into a tech-enabled regional leader. This strategy mitigates the rural workforce crisis and strengthens grant appeal by demonstrating innovation in the behavioral health sector.
