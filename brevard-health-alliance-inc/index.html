Brevard Health Alliance Inc.

Robotics & Automation Strategic Analysis

Executive Summary

Brevard Health Alliance Inc. (BHA) is a Federally Qualified Health Center (FQHC) system serving the Brevard County, Florida region. The most viable robotics and automation opportunities center on logistics, workflow automation, and patient handling. The primary ROI drivers are increased patient volume per provider through streamlined workflows, reduced staff injury rates, and enhanced reach via mobile health optimization.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A – Mobile Health Unit Logistics & Telehealth Integration (High Impact)

Optimization of the mobile health unit's routing, scheduling, and inventory management. Integration of telepresence robotics or high-fidelity telehealth carts to extend specialist reach. Impact includes increasing mobile unit visits from ~20 to 30+ per day and expanding reach to 5,000+ additional patient encounters annually.

Pathway B – Patient Handling & Physical Therapy Support (Safety & Efficiency)

Deployment of sit-to-stand lifts and patient transfer robots for geriatric and disabled patients. Benefits include significant reduction in workplace injuries for clinical staff and faster turnover in exam rooms.

Pathway C – Administrative & Pharmacy Automation (Workflow)

Robotic Process Automation (RPA) for scheduling and billing; automated dispensing cabinets for pharmacy. Liberation of staff time from repetitive administrative tasks to focus on patient care.

Strategic Positioning

BHA can transform from a traditional community clinic model into a "Smart Community Health Hub." By leveraging robotics to optimize mobile outreach and automate administrative burdens, BHA can serve a larger population with existing staff, a critical capability for an FQHC facing workforce shortages.

Other Entities Analysis

DispatchHealth: Focus on AI-driven mobile team logistics and smart scheduling to lift utilization from ~6.5 to 9-10 visits/team/day.
