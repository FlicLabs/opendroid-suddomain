Executive Summary

West Michigan Transport is positioned to transition from a traditional freight carrier to a technology-first logistics hub. By integrating autonomous systems, the company can decouple operational capacity from labor market fluctuations in the Grand Rapids region.

Core Vision: Synchronize fleet schedules with robotic warehousing to eliminate dwell time and create a "Continuous Flow" ecosystem.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: AMRs for Cross-Docking

Deployment of heavy-payload Autonomous Mobile Robots to handle pallet movement between receiving and outbound staging.

  • Eliminates forklift travel for long-haul distances.
  • Allows for "Lights-Out" staging during night shifts.

Pathway B: High-Density ASRS

Automated Storage and Retrieval Systems for e-commerce and break-bulk clients.

  • Increases storage density by 300%.
  • Picking accuracy improves to 99.9%.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pilot Year 3: Scale Year 5: Dark Warehouse

From initial deployment of 5 AMR units in the first year to a fully automated "Dark Warehouse" capability by Year 5, enabling 24-hour continuous operations with minimal supervision.
