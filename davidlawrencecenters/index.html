David Lawrence Centers

Behavioral Health Robotics & Automation Portfolio

Executive Summary

David Lawrence Centers (DLC) is the anchor for mental health and substance use recovery in Southwest Florida. As the primary Baker Act receiving facility, DLC faces acute capacity pressures and psychiatrist shortages. This research identifies automation levers to shift focus from physical operations to telepresence efficiency, automated safety, and therapeutic robotics.

Primary Goal: Multiply scarce psychiatric labor & reduce boarding times via Telepresence Robotics.
Organizational Profile
Scope: Crisis Stabilization, Residential Addiction Treatment, Community Response.
Volume: 30,000+ clients/year.
Geography: Collier County, FL.
Key Bottlenecks:
  • High psychiatrist vacancy/cost.
  • Limited bed capacity (Crisis Unit).
  • Administrative nursing burden.
Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Telepresence

Focus: Remote Assessment & Crisis Response.

Mobile robots allow psychiatrists to round on multiple units or assess jail intakes without travel.

+25% MD Consult Volume

Pathway B: Rx Automation

Focus: Medication Safety.

Automated dispensing cabinets in detox units to ensure 99.9% dosing accuracy.

3 hrs Saved per Nurse/Shift

Pathway C: Therapeutic

Focus: Patient Outcomes.

Social robots (e.g., Paro) for geriatric and high-anxiety pediatric patients.
