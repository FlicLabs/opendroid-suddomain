Strategic Robotics & Automation Analysis Portfolio

Executive Summary

Associated Urological Specialists (AUS) stands at a pivotal junction between traditional urologic care and the future of ambulatory surgery. The primary ROI opportunity lies in the Site-of-Service Shift: moving high-margin robotic procedures (prostatectomies, nephrectomies) from hospital partners to AUS-owned Ambulatory Surgery Centers (ASCs). This strategy, supported by lab automation and administrative AI, maximizes facility fee capture and operational sovereignty.

Organizational Profile

  • Core Model: Consolidated private urology practice with ASC capabilities.
  • Primary Volume: Prostate cancer, BPH, Kidney stones, diagnostics.
  • Economic Driver: Transitioning from Professional Fees (Consults) to Facility Fees (Surgical Center margins).
  • Bottleneck: Operating Room scheduling conflicts and manual lab throughput.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Surgical

ASC Robotic Expansion

Deployment of da Vinci or equivalent systems in outpatient centers to capture facility fees.

ROI: £600k+/year per robot

Pathway B: Laboratory

Automated Lab Lines

Modular automation for urinalysis and culture plating to increase throughput and reduce staffing needs.

ROI: Efficiency & Speed

Pathway C: Admin

RPA & Scheduling AI

Software robotics to handle insurance verification and patient scheduling, reducing call center load.

ROI: 30% Call Reduction
