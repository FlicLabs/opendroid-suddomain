BRIDGER AEROSPACE

Strategic Robotics & Automation Implementation Report

Prepared by Open Droids Consultancy Division | Senior Analysis Unit

Executive Summary

Bridger Aerospace is positioned to lead the next evolution of aviation ground operations through the strategic adoption of robotic cleaning and maintenance automation. This transition moves beyond incremental efficiency gains, aiming for a fundamental transformation in how aircraft are serviced between flights.

By deploying a specialized fleet of autonomous systems, Bridger can achieve unprecedented standards in hygiene and exterior care, directly translating to enhanced passenger safety and brand reputation. The core vision is a synchronized ecosystem of robots where autonomous cabin cleaners, UV-C disinfection units, exterior washing systems, and AI inspection bots operate in tandem.

Organizational Profile

Fleet & Operations

  • Models: Embraer E-Jets, Q400 Turboprops
  • Hubs: Concentrated High-Frequency
  • Current Model: Labor-intensive manual cleaning

ROI Horizon

Implementation Roadmap: 5-Year Phased Deployment

Phase 1: Pilot & Feasibility (Year 1)

Vendor selection, hardware testing on designated aircraft. Deployment of Autonomous Cabin Robots at primary hub.

Phase 2: Hub Integration (Year 2)

Scale cabin automation to 50% of fleet. Deploy UV-C disinfection units. Establish SOPs for human-robot collaboration.

Phase 3: Exterior Automation (Year 3)

Implement Exterior Washing Systems at two hubs. Introduce AI Inspection Bots for paint and tire analysis.

Phase 4: Fleet-Wide Expansion (Year 4)

100% coverage of active fleet for cabin cleaning. Expand exterior washing to regional outstations.

Phase 5: Optimization & AI Maturity (Year 5)

System autonomy and energy optimization. Predictive maintenance fully active. Full ROI realization.
