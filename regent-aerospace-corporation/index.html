ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

Regent Aerospace Corporation operates a diverse fleet of commercial aircraft, ranging from narrow-body single-aisle jets to wide-body international carriers. The airline maintains a significant presence at major hub airports, facilitating a high-volume schedule with tight turnaround windows. Currently, the operational model relies heavily on manual labor for cabin cleaning, exterior detailing, and routine maintenance inspections. This traditional approach faces challenges including labor shortages, variable quality control, and high overtime costs during peak travel seasons. The introduction of robotics offers a solution to these bottlenecks by enabling parallel processing of tasks and ensuring consistent, high-quality output.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Robots

Deployment of SLAM-enabled mobile robots for vacuuming, surface wiping, and trash removal. Ensures uniform cleaning standards and reduces turnaround time.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection Robots

Autonomous units emitting concentrated ultraviolet light to neutralize pathogens. Residue-free, rapid cycle for high-touch surfaces and air sanitation.

Pathway C: Exterior Aircraft Washing Systems

Automated gantry or crawler systems using high-pressure, low-volume techniques. Reduces water consumption by 40% and eliminates safety risks for ground crews.

Pathway D: AI-Powered Inspection Bots

Robots equipped with thermal imaging and high-res cameras to detect micro-cracks and corrosion. Shifts maintenance from reactive to predictive models.
