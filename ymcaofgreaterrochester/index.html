YMCA of Greater Rochester

Robotics & Automation Portfolio Analysis

Executive Summary: The YMCA of Greater Rochester manages a vast footprint of community wellness centers and camps. The primary robotics opportunity is Operational Stewardship—automating floor care and aquatics maintenance to recapture labor hours. This enables a shift from manual facility tasks to high-value member engagement.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: High ROI

Autonomous Floor Care

Scope: Deployment of Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for scrubbing and vacuuming gymnasiums and lobbies.

Impact: Reduces manual floor care by 20-30%. Ensures consistent hygiene standards during peak and off-hours.

Pathway B: Operational

Automated Aquatics

Scope: Robotic pool cleaners and automated chemical dosing systems for 10+ aquatic centers.

Impact: Energy reduction, chemical savings, and improved overnight cleaning without labor costs.

Pathway C: Strategic

Assistive Wellness

Scope: Sensor-based mobility robotics and anti-gravity treadmills for Active Older Adults.

Impact: Differentiates the Y as a post-rehab destination and supports "Aging in Place" grants.

Implementation Roadmap

1
Validation (Months 1-6)
Pilot autonomous scrubbers at flagship location (e.g., Schottland Family YMCA). Validate navigation in high-traffic zones.
2
Network Expansion (Months 7-18)
Roll out fleet to remaining 10 branches. Standardize aquatic automation. Retrain staff as "Robot Managers."
3
Innovation (Months 18-24)
Launch "Center of Excellence" for Active Aging with mobility robotics. Pilot robotic turf mowers at overnight camps.
