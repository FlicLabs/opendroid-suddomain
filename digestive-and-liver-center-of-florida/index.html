Executive Summary

The Digestive and Liver Center of Florida (DLCFL) operates in a high-volume procedural environment where diagnostic accuracy and throughput are critical. The strongest automation levers identified are Computer-Aided Detection (CADe) AI systems for colonoscopies and Automated Endoscope Reprocessing (AER) robotics. The dominant ROI driver is the augmentation of Adenoma Detection Rates (ADR), leading to improved pathology revenue and significant quality incentives.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Computer Aided Detection (CADe)

Implementation of "visual robotics" (AI overlay) across endoscopy towers. This acts as a second set of eyes, highlighting potential polyps in real-time.

  • Standardization of detection rates across providers.
  • Direct increase in pathology volume due to higher biopsy rates.
  • Marketing differentiation as an AI-driven center.

Pathway B: Automated Endoscope Reprocessing (AER)

Upgrading sterilization suites with robotic pass-through systems to automate leak testing and disinfection.

  • 15-20% reduction in scope turnover time.
  • Reduced labor costs and chemical exposure for staff.
