Executive Summary

Northwest Radiology Network (NWR) stands at a pivotal juncture where scan volume demand outpaces technologist availability. This analysis proposes a hybrid automation strategy combining Software Robotics (RPA) for administrative burden and Tele-robotic Ultrasound for clinical expansion. The dominant ROI driver is the recovery of net revenue lost to denials and the optimization of high-value imaging assets.

Strategic Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Intelligent Process Automation

"Software Robotics" targeting the revenue cycle. AI agents handle prior authorizations, eligibility checks, and denial management automatically.

  • Reduces administrative cost-to-collect by 30-40%.
  • Eliminates appointment leakage due to authorization delays.

Pathway B: Tele-Robotic Ultrasound

Deployment of robotic arm systems allowing specialized sonographers to scan patients remotely from a central hub.

  • Expands reach to rural/satellite clinics without travel costs.
  • Centralizes scarce talent (cardiac/vascular techs).

Pathway C: Automated Acquisition

AI-guided patient positioning and protocol selection for CT/MRI to standardize image quality.

  • Reduces technical retakes from ~6% to <1%.
  • Increases machine throughput by 2-3 patients per day.
