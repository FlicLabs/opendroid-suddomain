OPEN DROIDS
Research Portfolio: CAAC-2024-X

Carolina Asthma & Allergy Center

The largest asthma and allergy practice in the Charlotte metro area faces scalability challenges due to manual compounding and scheduling. The dominant ROI driver is the centralization of antigen mixing via robotics, potentially unlocking over $800k in annual net benefit while virtually eliminating dosing errors.

Pathway A: High Impact

Automated Compounding

Centralized robotic unit for mixing patient-specific antigen vials. Replaces manual technician labor with high-speed precision automation.

  • 60-75% reduction in labor hours
  • Near-zero inventory waste
  • Payback: < 9 months
Pathway B: Process Automation

Admin RPA Software

Software robots handling prior authorizations for biologics and automated scheduling for recurring injection appointments.

  • Instant Prior-Auth processing
  • Reduced reception workload
  • Payback: 3-6 months
Pathway C: Inventory

Smart Logistics

RFID-enabled smart cabinetry to track expensive biologic inventory across 15 satellite locations, preventing expiration and stock-outs.

  • Real-time stock visibility
  • Automated re-ordering
  • Payback: 12-18 months
