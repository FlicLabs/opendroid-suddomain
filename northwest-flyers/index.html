Aviation Maintenance Automation
Strategic Research for Northwest Flyers

Executive Summary

Northwest Flyers stands at a critical juncture in aviation operations where traditional manual maintenance models are becoming financially unsustainable. This research outlines a strategic pivot toward robotic cleaning automation, focusing on reducing aircraft turnaround times, elevating hygiene standards to hospital-grade levels, and significantly lowering labor overheads.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cabin Cleaning

Autonomous LiDAR robots for vacuuming and debris removal.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Pathogen eradication using concentrated Ultraviolet-C light.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Gantry-based systems recycling 90% of water used.

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Computer vision bots for fuselage damage detection.
