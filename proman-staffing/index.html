01. Executive Summary

Vision: The strategic vision for Proman must shift toward a Hybrid Workforce Solutions model. This involves integrating human flexibility with robotic consistency.

By incorporating Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) into client sites, Proman can reduce the learning curve for temporary staff, lower physical strain to improve retention, and offer a premium, tech-enabled staffing solution. We are transitioning from volume-based staffing to efficiency-based workforce integration.

02. Organizational Profile

Scale: Top-tier global staffing player with significant US footprint (Memphis, Chicago, Dallas, Inland Empire).

The Pivot: Moving from "Headcount" to "Robotics as a Service (RaaS)". Instead of just filling roles, Proman will supply a workforce specifically trained to work alongside cobots, securing long-term contracts with major logistics clients.

03. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: AMRs for Internal Transport

Immediate ROI

The Problem: Staff spend 50% of shifts walking.
The Solution: Deploy goods-to-person AMRs (e.g., Locus, Fetch).
Impact: Reduces onboarding from 2 weeks to 2 hours; keeps staff in picking zones.

Pathway B: Robotic Piece Picking & ASRS

High Density

The Problem: High SKU counts and human picking errors.
The Solution: Automated Storage and Retrieval Systems combined with robotic arms.
Impact: Shifts human role to "Robot Supervisor," a higher billable rate for Proman.

Pathway C: AI-Driven Logistics

Predictive

The Solution: Computer Vision AI for inbound docks.
Impact: AI predicts staffing needs based on inbound freight volume for dynamic scheduling.

05. Implementation Roadmap
YEAR 1
Pilot & Data: Select one major DC. Deploy Pathway A (AMRs). Establish "Tech-Enabled Worker" curriculum.
YEAR 2
Expansion: Expand to three metro hubs. Integrate AI planning for workforce scheduling.
YEAR 3-5
Optimization: Full scale rollout. Achieve 40% reduction in cost-per-unit. Proman becomes a technology integrator.
