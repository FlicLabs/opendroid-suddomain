Executive Summary

The DIA faces unique pressures to maximize efficiency while maintaining world-class security for its 65,000+ works. Robotics offers a dual benefit: cost reduction via autonomous facility management and revenue expansion through telepresence. The dominant ROI driver is the automation of high-frequency tasks like floor care and security patrols.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Security & Facilities Highest ROI Immediate

Deployment of Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for floor scrubbing and patrols.

  • 24/7 autonomous monitoring.
  • 40-60% reduction in janitorial labor hours.
  • Reduced insurance risk via thermal/LIDAR sensing.
Pathway B: Telepresence Revenue Driver Accessibility

Robotic avatars for remote docent-led tours.

  • Global "Virtual Tickets" for international visitors.
  • ADA compliance for mobility-impaired patrons.
  • Estimated $150k-$300k new annual revenue.
Pathway C: Conservation Long-Term Strategic

Robotic arms for multi-spectral imaging and scanning.

  • Touch-free analysis of fragile artifacts.
  • Creation of "Digital Twins" for research.
  • Enhances status as a research institution.

Strategic Positioning

Executing this roadmap positions the DIA as a "Smart Museum," demonstrating fiscal responsibility to taxpayers while using technology to democratize access to art. It transforms the institution from a static repository into a dynamic, globally accessible platform.
