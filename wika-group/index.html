Executive Summary

Wika Group is poised to transition from high-precision discrete manufacturing to a "Smart Factory" ecosystem. The strategic vision centers on Industry 4.0 adoption to enhance flexibility and maintain competitive precision. By integrating cognitive manufacturing principles, Wika can automate repetitive tasks while elevating skilled technicians to value-added calibration and R&D roles.

Organizational Profile

Production Scale

Global leader in measurement technology with over 10,000 employees. Operates on a Low-to-Medium Volume, High-Mix model requiring extreme precision.

Facilities

Major hubs in Germany (R&D), USA (Sarasota, Lawrenceville), China (Shanghai), and Brazil (São Paulo).

Process Type

Predominantly Discrete Manufacturing with critical assembly and calibration steps. Current reliance on manual/semi-automated assembly with high sensitivity to human variability.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Path A

Collaborative Robots (Cobots) - Assembly & Tending

Focus: Safety & Precision. Integration into machine tending for CNC components and repetitive sub-assembly (screw driving, insertion). Cobots allow co-existence with technicians in tight spaces.

Path B

AMRs - Material Transport

Focus: WIP Flow Efficiency. Autonomous Mobile Robots for JIT delivery of sensitive components to assembly lines, reducing manual forklift dependency and clutter.

Path C

Automated End-of-Line - Palletizing & Inspection

Focus: Throughput & Yield. Robotic palletizing integrated with machine vision for serial number verification and physical defect detection.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1

Pilot Phase

Site audits (USA). Deployment of 2 Cobot cells for machine tending. Technician training & KPI validation.

Year 2

Expansion & Logistics

Scale Cobots to additional lines. Deploy AMR fleet for material transport and line-side replenishment.

Year 3

End-of-Line Automation

Implement Automated Palletizing & Vision Inspection. Integrate all systems with central MES.

Year 4

Global Standardization

Replicate US pilot models to Germany and China facilities. Standardize hardware/software platforms.

Year 5

Smart Factory & AI

Implement AI-driven predictive quality control. Initiate "Lights-Out" manufacturing for high-volume shifts.
