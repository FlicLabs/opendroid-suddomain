Executive Summary

The vision for Industry 4.0 adoption at CL Boyd centers on the concept of the Smart Service Workshop. By integrating robotics and automation into repair bays and parts warehousing, CL Boyd can transition from reactive manual servicing to proactive, tech-enabled component processing. This strategic pivot aims to reduce technician fatigue and accelerate equipment turnaround time (TAT).

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: COBOTS

Collaborative Robots for Component Refurbishing. Deployment of IP67-rated Cobots to automate repetitive tasks such as sanding hydraulic cylinders or cleaning engine block surfaces.

Pathway B: AMRS

Autonomous Mobile Robots for Intralogistics. Heavy-payload AMRs deployed to transport parts from the warehouse directly to service bays, keeping technicians focused on billable hours.

Pathway C: QC CELLS

Automated Inspection. Vision-system inspection cells to detect micro-cracks in welding or structural steel before repair work begins.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot Phase - OKC Flagship deployment (1 Cobot, 2 AMRs).
  • Year 2: Data Link - Integration with Dealer Management System (DMS).
  • Year 3: Expansion - Rollout to Tulsa and secondary hubs.
  • Year 5: Autonomy - Self-optimizing service workflow.
