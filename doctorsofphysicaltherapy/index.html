Doctors of Physical Therapy (DPT)

Executive Summary

Doctors of Physical Therapy operates in the high-volume outpatient rehabilitation sector. The primary constraints are therapist labor capacity and administrative overhead. The most effective robotics levers are Therapeutic Robotics (Exoskeletons) to differentiate clinical care and increase reimbursement, and Robotic Process Automation (RPA) to streamline the revenue cycle. The dominant ROI driver is the shift toward higher-reimbursement CPT codes and increased patient throughput.

Organizational Profile

Business Model

Outpatient physical therapy clinics focusing on manual therapy, orthopedics, and sports medicine.

Operational Scale

Regional network (Midwest/Arizona) with standard clinic setups (gym + treatment rooms).

Economics

~£80-£100 ($100-$120) revenue per visit. Margins sensitive to provider productivity.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Therapeutic Robotics
Highest Clinical Value

Scope: Deploy exoskeletons (e.g., Ekso) for neuro-rehab.

Impact: Access to higher-paying CPT codes (Neuromuscular Re-ed) and reduced therapist physical strain.

Pathway B: Admin RPA
Highest Efficiency

Scope: Software robotics for scheduling & billing.

Impact: 30% reduction in admin hours; reduced claim denials.

Pathway C: Wearable Sensors
Complementary

Scope: Remote monitoring sensors for home care.

Impact: New revenue streams via RTM codes; better compliance.
