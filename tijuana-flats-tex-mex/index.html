01. Executive Summary

The fast-casual dining landscape is facing pressure from rising labor costs. For Tijuana Flats, automation is not about replacing hospitality, but elevating it. By shifting the "runner" model to a robotic-assisted model, human staff can focus on the guest experience and maintaining the signature Hot Sauce Bars, ensuring brand culture survives while margins improve.

02. Organizational Profile

  • Entity: Tijuana Flats Tex-Mex
  • Scale: ~65-90 Locations
  • Model: Fast-Casual (Counter Order / Table Delivery)
  • Pain Points: Runner fatigue, Lunch rush bottlenecks, Dining room cleanliness.

03. Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: FOH Service

LIDAR-equipped robots for food running. Increases turnover and reduces physical strain.

Pathway B: BOH Automation

Automated chip frying systems to ensure consistency and safety.

Pathway C: AI Ordering

Smart Kiosks focused on upselling add-ons like Queso and Guacamole.

04. Financial Model

CAPEX: $35k - $45k per location (2 Robots + Setup).

OPEX Impact: Reduction of 40-50 labor hours/week.

ROI Target: Break-even in under 14 months via labor savings and increased table turns.

05. Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot in 5 Orlando locations.
  • Year 2: Regional rollout to 50% corporate stores.
  • Year 3: Full integration & Franchise incentives.
  • Year 4: Back-of-House Fryer automation.
  • Year 5: Fully autonomous ecosystem.
