Prosser Memorial Health: Robotics & Automation Portfolio

Executive Summary

Prosser Memorial Health (PMH) is transforming from a traditional Critical Access Hospital into a high-tech rural health hub. Anchored by the da Vinci Xi surgical system and a new $112M facility, PMH is uniquely positioned to leverage automation not just for efficiency, but for market share retention. The primary robotics opportunities lie in maximizing surgical case retention, deploying autonomous logistics in the new larger footprint, and utilizing tele-presence to reduce patient transfers.

Organizational Profile

  • Type: Independent Critical Access Hospital (CAH).
  • Location: Prosser, Washington (Lower Yakima Valley).
  • Key Asset: New replacement facility & da Vinci Xi Surgical System.
  • Challenge: Rural staffing shortages and competition from Tri-Cities health systems.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Surgical Robotics

High Strategic Value

Scope: Optimization of the existing da Vinci Xi platform for General Surgery, Urology, and Gynecology.

Impact: Recruitment of top-tier surgeons and retention of $2M+ in gross charges annually.

Pathway B: Logistics & EVS

Operational Efficiency

Scope: Deployment of TUG robots and autonomous floor scrubbers in the new hospital facility.

Impact: Saves 1.5-2 FTEs in labor; ensures consistent cleanliness in high-traffic corridors.

Pathway C: Tele-Specialty

Clinical Expansion

Scope: Virtual rounding robots for Stroke and ICU consults.

Impact: Reduces patient transfers; keeps acuity and revenue within the community.

Financial Model (Pathway A Focus)

The "Retention Moat" Model:

  • Cost Baseline: ~$180k/year in service contracts/leases.
  • Target Uplift: +50 cases/year via recruitment.
  • Incremental Revenue: $750k - $1.25M Annually.
  • ROI Speed: Immediate upon surgeon recruitment. The cost of not having the robot is the loss of the entire surgery department's viability.

Implementation Roadmap

1
Phase 1: Surgical Stabilization (Current)
Secure fellowship-trained surgeons using the robot as a recruitment tool. Market "Big City Tech, Small Town Care."
2
Phase 2: The Smart Hospital (New Facility Launch)
Deploy autonomous scrubbers and tugs immediately upon opening the new building to signal innovation.
3
Phase 3: Network Extension (Year 2+)
Partner with tertiary centers for tele-stroke and tele-ICU coverage via mobile robotic carts.

Strategic Positioning

Prosser Memorial Health effectively differentiates itself by breaking the "rural obsolescence" cycle. By investing in robotics, PMH proves that quality care is defined by technology and talent, not zip code. This strategy secures the hospital's financial future by preventing outmigration to larger competitor systems.
