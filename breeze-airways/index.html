BREEZE AIRWAYS: ROBOTIC AUTOMATION STRATEGY

Executive Report by Open Droids Consultation & Research

Executive Summary & Organizational Profile

Strategic Vision: Breeze Airways is positioned to lead the regional aviation sector through the "Touchless Turnaround" initiative. By deploying a fleet of autonomous cleaning and inspection robots, Breeze aims to achieve unprecedented operational efficiency, reduce variable costs, and elevate hygiene standards.

Current State: Operating a lean fleet of Embraer E-190/E-195 aircraft, Breeze utilizes a point-to-point model. Current manual cleaning processes, while effective, present bottlenecks in rapid turnaround times and labor dependency.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cabin Cleaning

Autonomous vacuum and wipe robots for E-195 interiors.

  • Self-navigation in narrow aisles
  • 30 min reduction in TAT
  • HEPA filtration integration

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Mobile units for rapid pathogen neutralization.

  • 10-minute cycle time
  • High-touch surface focus
  • Compliance documentation

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Automated gantry systems for fuselage care.

  • 20% water reduction
  • Hangar integration
  • Safety hazard elimination

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Drone/Crawler tech for structural integrity.

  • Thermal imaging detection
  • Micro-fracture analysis
  • Reduced downtime

Implementation Roadmap (5-Year Strategy)

Year 1: Pilot & Integration
Site assessment and vendor selection. Deployment of 2 Cabin Robots and 4 UV-C units at primary hub. Data collection on TAT and labor hours.
Year 2: Expansion
SOP refinement. 50% fleet coverage for cleaning robots. Deployment of Exterior Washing prototype at main hangar.
Year 3: Fleet-Wide Rollout
Full deployment across major hubs. Integration of AI inspection data into MOC. 35% OPEX reduction achieved.
Year 4: Advanced Autonomy
Predictive logistics integration. De-icing capable exterior systems. Adjacent automation exploration (baggage).
Year 5: Ecosystem Maturity
Full "Touchless Turnaround" workflow. 45% OPEX reduction target. 15% increase in aircraft utilization.

Labor Hour Redistribution

Shift from manual sanitation to quality assurance and tech supervision.
