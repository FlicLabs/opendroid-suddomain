Shepherd Center

Robotics Analysis & ROI Model

Executive Summary

The Shepherd Center (Atlanta, GA) is a top-10 rehabilitation hospital specializing in Spinal Cord Injury (SCI) and Brain Injury. The institution is positioned to leverage robotics not just for clinical therapy (current state) but for operational logistics and home-based continuity of care. The dominant ROI drivers are increased throughput, labor efficiency, and readmission reduction.

Organizational Profile
152 Specialized Beds
~1,000 Annual Inpatients
Top 10 U.S. Ranking

Core Focus: SCI, Acquired Brain Injury, Stroke, Chronic Pain.
Current Tech: Heavy user of Lokomat, Indego, and ZeroG systems.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Therapeutic

Scope: Expand exoskeletal fleet & AI-driven biofeedback.

Impact: 20-30% therapist efficiency gain; shorter Length of Stay (LOS).

ROI: High (Capacity driven)

Pathway B: Operations

Scope: Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for linens, meals, and waste.

Impact: Recover 4,000+ clinical hours/year; reduce workplace injury.

ROI: Medium (Labor savings)

Pathway C: Home Care

Scope: Rental fleet of wearable robotics for post-discharge home use.

Impact: Reduce readmissions; new Remote Therapeutic Monitoring revenue.

ROI: Emerging (New Revenue)

Financial Model: Pathway A Focus

Investment: ~$1.5M for advanced gait & upper-extremity stations.

Throughput Lift: Treat 10-15% more patients via LOS efficiency.

Net Benefit: $2M - $3M incremental annual revenue.

Payback: 9 - 14 Months.
