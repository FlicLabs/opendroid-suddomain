Executive Summary & Profile

Overview

Mo' Bettahs stands at the intersection of high-volume fast-casual dining and the next generation of labor automation. The strategy focuses on creating a hybrid ecosystem where robotic systems handle repetitive tasks (frying, bussing, order taking), allowing human staff to elevate the guest experience. This approach addresses critical labor shortages while maximizing throughput and consistency in the Hawaiian plate lunch segment.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Strategic Verticals
Pathway A: Front-of-House Serving Robots

Autonomous bussing and runners to reduce physical strain on staff and increase table turnover rates during peak lunch hours.

Pathway B: Back-of-House Automated Frying/Grilling

Robotic arms for Katsu and chicken frying. Ensures perfect consistency, reduces oil waste, and improves kitchen safety.

Pathway C: AI-Driven Kiosks & Voice Agents

Self-service ordering for visual customization and AI voice agents for drive-thru accuracy and upselling.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1 - Year 5

Year 1: Pilot & Integration

Deploy AI Kiosks and pilot 1 BOH Frying station. Data collection on upselling metrics and kitchen throughput.

Year 2: Expansion

Scale BOH robots to 20% of fleet. Introduce FOH bussing robots in high-traffic locations. Implement AI Voice Agents.

Year 3: Standardization

All new builds are "Robotics Ready." Full integration of data systems. Focus on Menu Engineering via robot data.

Year 4: Optimization

Predictive maintenance integration. Software upgrades for energy efficiency and cooking precision.

Year 5: Maturity

Full ROI realization. Staff retrained as "Robotics Coordinators." Brand positioning as high-tech hospitality leader.
