Network180

Healthcare Robotics & Automation Opportunities Portfolio

1. Executive Summary

Network180 acts as the Community Mental Health Authority for Kent County, Michigan. Unlike acute care facilities, its challenges are rooted in longitudinal care management, social determinants of health, and administrative complexity. The research identifies that the highest value robotics applications are not surgical, but process-driven and assistive.

Key Opportunity: Implementing "Software Robotics" (RPA) for intake and "Clinical IoT" for remote medication adherence can generate millions in capacity savings and cost avoidance.

2. Organizational Profile

Scale & Scope

Region: Kent County, MI (Pop ~650k)
Volume: ~20,000 lives managed annually
Budget: ~$150M+ (Public/Medicaid)

Core Function

Public safety net for Mental Illness (MI), Intellectual/Developmental Disabilities (IDD), and Substance Use Disorders (SUD). Moves from crisis response to long-term case management.

Operational Bottlenecks

• Severe caseworker shortage
• Excessive compliance documentation
• Medication non-adherence leading to readmission

3. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Intelligent Automation

Scope: Robotic Process Automation (RPA) for the 24/7 Access Center.

Key Levers:

  • Auto-transcription of crisis calls.
  • Bots for insurance verification & state reporting.
  • Predictive staffing models.

Impact: Equivalent to 15 FTEs in capacity gain.

Pathway B: Assistive Robotics

Scope: Social robots and smart monitoring for Group Homes (IDD).

Key Levers:

  • Cognitive prompting (hygiene/meds).
  • Emotional regulation support.
  • Fall detection without cameras.

Impact: Reduced caregiver burnout & lower acuity staffing needs.

Pathway C: Med-Adherence Bots

Scope: Automated dispensers for high-risk SMI/SUD populations.

Key Levers:

  • Locked, timed medication release.
  • Cellular alerts to case managers on missed dose.
  • Data tracking for court-ordered treatment.

Impact: 15-20% reduction in psychiatric readmissions.

6. Implementation Roadmap

1

Year 1: Foundation & Pilots

Deploy RPA for claims processing to clear backlogs. Launch pilot of 50 automated medication dispensers for the highest-risk schizophrenia cohort.

2

Year 2: Expansion

Scale medication dispensers to 300 units. Introduce social robotics in 5 partner group homes to validate reduction in behavioral escalations.

3

Year 3: Integration

Launch unified command center dashboard visualizing real-time community health data (adherence, crisis location, bed availability).

7. Strategic Positioning

Adopting this portfolio allows Network180 to transition from a reactive funding authority to a proactive population health manager. Automation resolves the workforce shortage crisis, while assistive technologies restore dignity and independence to clients. This positions Network180 as a national leader in tech-enabled community psychiatry.
