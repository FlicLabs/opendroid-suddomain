Executive Summary

Empowering People's Independence (EPI) is a vital human services agency in New York supporting individuals with developmental disabilities and TBI. Facing a critical Direct Support Professional (DSP) staffing crisis, EPI's automation strategy must pivot from general efficiency to safety augmentation and labor substitution. The primary levers are automated medication management, remote telepresence monitoring, and social robotics.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Meds High ROI

Enterprise automated dispensing systems in residential homes.

  • Eliminates travel for nurse verification.
  • Reduces MAP error penalties.
  • Benefit: ~£250k/year net.

Pathway B: Monitoring

Telepresence & sensor arrays for overnight supervision.

  • 1 Remote staff monitors 4 homes.
  • Replaces "sleep-over" shifts.
  • Risk: High (Regulatory).

Pathway C: Social

Cognitive assistive robotics for Day Habilitation.

  • Consistent social skills therapy.
  • Reduces staff burnout.
  • Focus: Quality of Life.

Strategic Positioning

Implementing this roadmap transitions EPI from a traditional labor-dependent agency to a Tech-Enabled Care Platform. This decoupling of growth from linear staffing requirements allows for sustainable expansion and higher quality of care despite industry-wide labor shortages.
