1. Executive Summary

East Haven Nursing and Rehab faces critical staffing shortages and rising labor costs. By deploying Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs), the facility can offload 4,000+ hours of non-clinical logistics annually, shifting focus back to direct patient care and improving margins.

2. Organizational Profile

  • Model: Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) & Rehab
  • Scale: 120-150 Beds, ~120 Staff
  • Challenges: Staff turnover, agency reliance, walking fatigue.

3. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Logistics (High Value)

Deployment of 3-5 Service AMRs for dietary, laundry, and waste.

  • Reduces staff walking by 4-6 miles/day.
  • Reclaims ~1,500 clinical hours per robot annually.
  • Immediate reduction in agency overtime costs.

Pathway B: Social Robotics (Quality)

Therapeutic robots (PARO/ElliQ) for dementia care.

  • Reduces resident agitation without medication.
  • Differentiation for Memory Care marketing.

Pathway C: Staff Safety (Retention)

Exoskeletons for patient transfers.

  • Reduces workers' comp claims.
  • Improves staff retention and safety culture.

Strategic Positioning

Implementing this roadmap positions East Haven as a "Tech-Enabled Smart Care Center." This branding attracts higher-acuity residents and improves staff retention by demonstrating a commitment to reducing their physical workload.
