ARMICK INC. AUTOMATION STRATEGY

Executive Report on Robotic Cleaning & Maintenance Integration

Executive Summary

Armick Inc. is positioned to revolutionize its operational efficiency through the strategic deployment of robotic cleaning automation. This initiative aims to reduce OPEX by 35-45% while enhancing hygiene standards and aircraft availability. By integrating Autonomous Cabin Cleaning, UV-C Disinfection, Exterior Washing, and AI Inspection systems, Armick secures a competitive edge in aviation maintenance, ensuring a payback period of under 24 months.

Organizational Profile

  • Fleet Size: 150 Aircraft (Mixed Body)
  • Hub Presence: 3 Major International Hubs
  • Current Model: Labor-Intensive Manual Cleaning
  • Target: High-Frequency Turnarounds

Financial Dashboard

$75K-$150K CAPEX / Unit
35-45% OPEX Reduction
<24 Mo Payback Period

*Financial projections based on current labor costs and utilization rates at primary hubs.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

  • Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Mobile vacuuming and surface sanitizing robots utilizing SLAM navigation for rapid turnarounds.
  • Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection Autonomous UV-C towers for germicidal disinfection of high-touch surfaces and air systems.
  • Pathway C: Exterior Washing Automated gantry systems reducing wash time by 60% while conserving water and chemicals.
  • Pathway D: AI Inspection Drones and crawlers with thermal sensors for automated fuselage and structural integrity checks.
