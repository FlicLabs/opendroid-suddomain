JETBLUE AUTOMATION STRATEGY

Strategic Report: Robotics & AI Integration for Aviation Cleaning & Maintenance

✓ OPEX Reduction: 45% ✓ Payback: < 24 Months ✓ 5-Year Roadmap

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

JetBlue is positioned to lead the aviation industry in maintenance automation. By integrating Open Droids' robotic solutions, JetBlue will transition from labor-intensive cleaning protocols to a high-efficiency, technology-driven model. This strategy focuses on four key pathways: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning, UV-C Disinfection, Exterior Washing, and AI Inspection. The result is a projected 35-45% reduction in operational expenditures, enhanced aircraft hygiene, and a sub-24-month return on investment.

DEPLOYMENT ROADMAP

Phase 1: Pilot (Year 1)

Focus on JFK Hub. Deployment of 5 Cabin Cleaning units and 2 AI Inspection bots. KPI establishment.

Phase 2: Expansion (Year 2)

Scale to 40 Cabin Cleaning units. Introduce 15 UV-C Disinfection units at JFK & BOS.

Phase 3: Network (Year 3)

50% Fleet coverage for Cabin Cleaning. Pilot Exterior Washing systems.

Phase 4: Standardization (Year 4)

90% Fleet coverage. Full integration of UV-C and Exterior Washing at MRO facilities.

Phase 5: Optimization (Year 5)

AI-Driven scheduling. Level 4 Autonomous Inspection. Full OPEX reduction realized.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

  • Pathway A (Cabin Cleaning): Autonomous AMRs for vacuuming, seat sanitization, and debris collection.
  • Pathway B (UV-C Disinfection): Autonomous UV-C light units for pathogen neutralization on high-touch surfaces.
  • Pathway C (Exterior Washing): Automated gantry systems reducing water consumption and ground safety risks.
  • Pathway D (AI Inspection): LiDAR-equipped bots for predictive maintenance and fuselage integrity checks.
