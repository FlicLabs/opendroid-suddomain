Home of Hope Inc.

Robotics Consultation & ROI Portfolio

Executive Summary

Home of Hope Inc. faces critical challenges in staffing, physical labor strain, and resident engagement within the Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities (IDD) sector. This portfolio identifies automation as a stabilizer. The strategy prioritizes Safe Patient Handling Robotics to reduce injury costs, followed by Socially Assistive Robotics for cognitive support. The projected impact includes a 50-70% reduction in lift-related injuries and significant workforce stabilization.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A - Highest ROI

Mobility & Safety Robotics

Scope: Autonomous mobile lifters and smart ceiling hoists in high-acuity facilities.

Impact: drastically reduces musculoskeletal injuries for staff, enables single-caregiver transfers, and lowers Workers' Comp liability.

Pathway B - Resident Value

Socially Assistive Robotics (SAR)

Scope: Social robots in day programs to lead exercises and memory games.

Impact: Provides consistent cognitive stimulation, reduces staff burden for routine activities, and assists in emotional regulation.

Pathway C - Independence

Smart Environment Automation

Scope: Passive sensors (Lidar/Radar) and voice controls in group homes.

Impact: Increases resident autonomy, reduces intrusive night checks, and detects falls without cameras.
