Hamilton Community Health Network

Robotics & Automation Opportunities Portfolio
Executive Summary: HCHN is a critical safety-net provider in Flint, MI. Unlike surgical centers, HCHN's robotics opportunity lies in operational efficiency. The dominant ROI driver is Pharmacy Automation to maximize 340B revenue capture, followed by Robotic Process Automation (RPA) for administrative relief.

Organizational Profile

The Model

Federally Qualified Health Center (FQHC) offering integrated primary care, dental, behavioral health, and pharmacy services to underserved populations.

The Economics

Driven by Medicaid, grants, and the 340B Drug Pricing Program. Tight margins require volume optimization and cost control.

The Pain Points

High administrative burden, pharmacy wait times causing revenue leakage, and staffing retention challenges.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Pharmacy Automation

Highest ROI Revenue Generator

Scope: Robotic dispensing systems for counting, bottling, and labeling maintenance meds.

Impact: Increases daily script volume by 30-40%. Reduces leakage to retail pharmacies, retaining critical 340B revenue.

$500k - $1.5M Est. Annual Incremental Revenue

Pathway B: Administrative RPA

Efficiency Staff Retention

Scope: Software bots for referral processing, billing reconciliation, and appointment scheduling.

Impact: Reduces front-desk burnout, lowers claim denials, and improves patient engagement.

3-6 Months Est. Payback Period

Pathway C: Facility Robotics

Safety Quality

Scope: Autonomous mobile robots for floor cleaning and UV-C disinfection.

Impact: Ensures consistent sanitation standards and signals high-tech safety to the community.

High Patient Confidence Boost

Implementation Roadmap

1

Year 1: Pilot & Stabilization

Install high-volume dispensing robot in the main center. Launch RPA pilot for appointment reminders to reduce no-shows.

2

Year 2: Expansion

Roll out pharmacy automation to secondary sites. Deploy UV-C disinfection robots in main lobbies. Integrate RPA into billing.

3

Year 3: Network Integration

Full inventory management integration. Evaluate telepresence robots for remote specialist consults across the network.

Strategic Positioning

Executing this roadmap transforms Hamilton Community Health Network into a Tech-Enabled Integrated Care System. By automating the routine (script filling, data entry, cleaning), HCHN empowers its human staff to focus on clinical excellence, securing financial sustainability and improving access for the Flint community.
