01. Executive Vision

Wilson Logistics is positioned to transition from a traditional trucking power into a Smart Logistics Hub. By integrating robotics into cross-docking and maintenance supply chains, the organization will reduce terminal dwell times and operational overhead. This report outlines the shift from manual forklift dependency to an orchestrated fleet of Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) and automated storage systems.

03. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: AMRs for Internal Transport

Goal: Decouple travel time from operator skill.

Deployment of heavy-payload AMRs to handle long-haul horizontal transport across terminal floors. Increases throughput by 35% and reduces forklift collision risks.

Pathway B: Robotic Piece Picking (ASRS)

Goal: Zero-delay maintenance parts supply.

Implementation of high-density shuttle systems for spare parts inventory. Reduces storage footprint by 60% and ensures mechanics have instant access to required components.

Pathway C: AI-Driven Logistics

Goal: Predictive efficiency and automated validation.

Computer vision gates at inbound/outbound docks to automate claim processing, load planning, and dimensioning.
