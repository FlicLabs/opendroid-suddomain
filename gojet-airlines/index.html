GoJet Airlines

Strategic Robotics Automation Report

Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive strategy for integrating autonomous robotics into GoJet Airlines' cleaning and maintenance operations. The objective is to transition from labor-intensive methods to a high-tech, data-driven model. By deploying autonomous systems, GoJet can achieve unprecedented hygiene efficiency, reduce operational costs by 35-45%, and significantly improve fleet turnaround times. This transformation positions GoJet as an industry leader in aviation maintenance innovation.

Organizational Profile

Fleet Overview

GoJet operates a fleet of approximately 50 CRJ-700 and CRJ-900 aircraft. This standardized fleet provides an ideal platform for robotic integration due to uniform cabin dimensions and exterior profiles.

Hub Presence

Operations are centralized at major American Airlines hubs including Chicago O'Hare (ORD), Dallas Fort Worth (DFW), and Washington Reagan (DCA). These high-volume environments necessitate rapid gate turnarounds.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Robots Mobile units utilizing LiDAR for trash collection, seat organization, and vacuuming. Reduces gate turn time by 15-20 minutes.
Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection Robots Deployable post-deplaning for hospital-grade pathogen neutralization on high-touch surfaces. Reduces chemical usage by 30%.
Pathway C: Exterior Aircraft Washing Automated systems maintaining aerodynamic efficiency, reducing fuel consumption by approx. 0.5% and extending paint life.
Pathway D: AI-Powered Inspection Bots Drones/Crawlers with thermal imaging for structural anomaly detection. Creates digital twins for predictive maintenance.

5-Year Implementation Roadmap

  • Phase 1 (Year 1): Pilot deployment of Cabin Cleaning and UV-C robots at DFW hub (2 Aircraft).
  • Phase 2 (Year 2): Infrastructure installation at ORD; integration with MOC; expand fleet to 30%.
  • Phase 3 (Years 3-4): Fleet-wide rollout of cleaning bots; deployment of AI Inspection for C-Checks.
  • Phase 4 (Year 5): Full autonomous operation; data optimization for predictive maintenance; evaluation of hydrogen power.
