Robotics & Automation Strategic Portfolio

Executive Summary

Tryon Medical Partners is a multi-specialty medical practice operating across the Charlotte, North Carolina metropolitan area, with a strong emphasis on gastroenterology, pulmonary medicine, and interventional cardiology. As a private, physician-owned practice, Tryon’s operational focus is on maximizing clinical efficiency within high-volume procedural centers and outpatient clinics. The primary robotics and automation opportunities center on: (1) Robotic-assisted procedures to improve precision and throughput, (2) AI-driven workflow automation, and (3) Laboratory automation. The dominant ROI driver is the reduction of procedure times and staff labor costs, leading to increased case volumes per day.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Procedural Robotics (GI & Cardiology)

Highest Value Driver: Implementation of robotic endoscopy and catheter navigation systems.

  • Impact: 10-15% reduction in average procedure time.
  • Throughput: Increase daily case capacity by 1-2 procedures per room.
  • ROI: Driven by increased reimbursement volume and reduced labor strain.

Pathway B: Workflow Automation

High Efficiency: AI-driven scheduling and automated pre-authorization.

  • Impact: Reduction in administrative overhead and "white space" in procedure suites.
  • Speed: Faster room turnover and patient intake.
  • ROI: Low capex, rapid implementation, immediate cost savings.

Pathway C: Laboratory Automation

Operational Backbone: Automated sample processing and slide preparation.

  • Impact: Faster turnaround times for lab results critical to procedure scheduling.
  • Accuracy: Reduction in manual processing errors.
  • ROI: FTE savings and support for increased procedural volume.

Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation (Year 1)

Focus: Pathway B (Workflow Automation) & Pathway A Pilot

Roll out AI scheduling tools across all locations. Launch robotic endoscopy pilot in 2 GI suites to validate case time reduction.

Phase 2: Expansion (Year 2)

Focus: Pathway C (Lab Automation) & Pathway A Scale

Automate lab processing to handle increased volume. Expand robotic systems to Cardiology suites based on pilot data.

Phase 3: Optimization (Year 3+)

Focus: Full Network Integration

Integrate data streams from robotics, workflow, and lab systems to optimize staffing models and predictive maintenance.
