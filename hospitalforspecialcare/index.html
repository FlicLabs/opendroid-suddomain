Hospital for Special Care

Robotics Analysis & ROI Portfolio

Executive Summary

Hospital for Special Care (HSC) is a leading Long-Term Acute Care Hospital (LTACH) serving complex medical, spinal cord, and brain injury patients. The robotics strategy focuses on functional restoration rather than speed. The primary ROI drivers are reduced length of stay, staff safety, and Center of Excellence differentiation.

228 Beds Scale of Operations
~15-20% Potential Reduction in LOS (Neuro-Rehab)
Robotics Opportunity Pathways
Pathway A: High Clinical Value

Neuro-Rehab Ecosystem

Scope: Powered exoskeletons, Lokomat gait trainers, and upper-extremity robotics for SCI/TBI units.

Impact: Increases therapy repetition by 10x per session; reduces physical strain on therapists.

$200k+ Annual Savings in Staff Injury/Turnover
Pathway B: Niche Strategic

Social & Behavioral Robotics

Scope: Robots (e.g., QTrobot) for the Autism Inpatient Unit to assist in social skills and emotional recognition.

Impact: Provides consistent, non-judgmental interaction and objective behavioral data logging.

Pathway C: Operational

Service Automation

Scope: Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for linens, meals, and pharmacy transport.

Impact: Keeps nurses at the bedside; alleviates staffing shortages in long-term care settings.

Financial Summary (Pathway A)

  • Throughput: +40% therapy capacity per FTE.
  • Payback Period: 18–24 months.
  • Strategic Value: Referrals from acute trauma centers.
