Yellowstone Boys and Girls Ranch

Robotics Opportunity Portfolio & Strategic Analysis
Executive Summary: YBGR is uniquely positioned to leverage robotics for both operational efficiency (managing a 400-acre campus) and clinical innovation (socially assistive robots for trauma care). The strategy focuses on high-impact, low-risk deployments that reduce staff burnout and operational overhead while enhancing the therapeutic milieu.

Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Therapeutic High Impact

Scope: Socially Assistive Robots (SARs) like Paro or Moxie for trauma and ASD therapy.

ROI Driver: Reducing critical incidents and staff burnout/turnover.

Est. Impact: +15-20% Patient Engagement

Pathway B: Facilities Mod. Impact

Scope: Autonomous mowing fleets and industrial floor scrubbers.

ROI Driver: Redeploying 1,500+ labor hours annually to skilled maintenance.

Est. Savings: $35k-45k/year (Labor equiv.)

Pathway C: Educational Strategic

Scope: STEM robotics curriculum and vocational repair training.

ROI Driver: Grant funding acquisition and student vocational outcomes.

Est. Impact: New Grant Revenue Streams

Financial Projection (Years 1-5)

$200,000+ Cumulative Net Benefit
14-18 Mo. Payback Period
1,500 hrs Annual Labor Redeployed
5-10% Projected Turnover Reduction

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1
Pilot & Prove: Deploy autonomous floor scrubbers in the school. Introduce 2 pilot therapeutic units in the highest-acuity lodge.
Year 2
Scale Operations: Roll out autonomous mowing fleet for the 400-acre campus. Expand therapeutic robotics to all residential lodges.
Year 3
Data Integration: Link robotics usage data to Electronic Health Records (EHR). Launch student-led Robotics Repair vocational track.
