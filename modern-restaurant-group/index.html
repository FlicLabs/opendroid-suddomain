Executive Summary

Modern Restaurant Group is positioned to transition from a traditional labor model to a hybrid-automated ecosystem. By integrating Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) and kitchen automation, the group can expect to alleviate expo bottlenecks, ensure culinary consistency, and repurpose human capital towards high-touch guest hospitality. The strategic goal is operational resilience against labor volatility while increasing throughput capacity.

Financial Projection & Efficiency Models

Pathway A

FOH Serving Robots

Deployment of runners/bussers to handle logistics. Servers stay in their sections to focus on refills and upselling.

Pathway B

BOH Auto-Frying

Robotic arms for basket management. Ensures precise cooking times, safety, and consistent food quality.

Pathway C

AI Order Kiosks

Voice agents and smart kiosks focused on algorithmic upselling to increase average check size.

Implementation Roadmap (5-Year Horizon)

Year 1 Pilot Program: 2 high-volume locations, install Serving Robots (Pathway A).
Year 2 Standardization: Rollout A to all units. Begin Kitchen Automation pilot.
Year 3 Full Integration: Data synchronization between POS and Robot fleet.
Year 4 Optimization: Permanent reduction of FOH staffing baselines.
Year 5 Innovation: Next-gen fleet upgrades and fully autonomous satellite kitchens.
