Executive Summary

AAON Inc. stands at the precipice of Industry 4.0 transformation. The strategic vision focuses on integrating flexible manufacturing systems to support high-mix customization while maintaining high-volume efficiency. By leveraging IoT interoperability and autonomous systems, AAON aims to transition from traditional discrete manufacturing to a resilient, data-driven "Smart Factory" ecosystem. This transition mitigates labor volatility and aligns with corporate sustainability goals through energy-optimized automation.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

PATHWAY A

Collaborative Robots

Focus: Safety & Precision

Application: Machine tending (CNC/Press Brakes) and repetitive assembly (tube insertion). Enables HRC without caging.

Impact: 15% reduction in injuries; 25% uptime increase.

PATHWAY B

Autonomous Mobile Robots

Focus: WIP Flow Efficiency

Application: Material transport and line-side replenishment of raw materials (coils) and finished sub-assemblies.

Impact: 30% reduction in WIP inventory; dynamic navigation.

PATHWAY C

Automated End-of-Line

Focus: Throughput & Yield

Application: High-speed palletizing and 3D machine vision for leak detection/cosmetic QA.

Impact: 99.9% packaging accuracy; reduced scrap waste.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pilot Phase

Audit & Process Mapping. Deployment of 2 Cobot cells in Tulsa (Heat Exchanger Assembly). Establish OEE baseline.

Year 2: Material Flow

Integrate AMR fleet for internal logistics. Expand Cobots to Longview facility. Focus on operator training.

Year 3: End-of-Line

Implementation of Automated Palletizing & Vision Inspection (Pathway C). Full ERP/MES integration.

Year 4: Data & Digital Twins

Shift to predictive maintenance. Deployment of Digital Twins for simulation of production changes.

Year 5: Full Autonomy

AI-driven dynamic scheduling. "Lights-out" manufacturing for specific sub-assemblies. High-mix flexibility.
