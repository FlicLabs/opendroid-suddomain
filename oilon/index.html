Executive Summary

Oilon is positioned to lead the clean energy transition by adopting a Hybrid Automation Strategy. Moving beyond traditional manual assembly, the integration of collaborative robotics (Cobots) and AMRs will address key bottlenecks in the high-mix production of industrial burners and heat pumps.

This strategy focuses on decarbonizing production through efficiency, reducing ergonomic risks for the skilled workforce, and increasing throughput capacity to meet global energy technology demands.

Robotics Opportunities

  • Pathway A (Welding/Tending): Deploy heavy-payload Cobots for consistent root pass welding on burner chassis and CNC machine tending.
  • Pathway B (Logistics): Implement AMRs (500kg-1000kg) to autonomously transport heavy heat pump components, replacing manual forklifts in tight aisles.
  • Pathway C (End-of-Line): Automated palletizing systems and AI Vision Inspection to ensure zero-defect delivery of complex electromechanical assemblies.
