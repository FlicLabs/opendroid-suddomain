FARGO JET CENTER

STRATEGIC AUTOMATION RESEARCH REPORT

Prepared by Open Droids Consultation Division

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This strategic plan envisions a fully autonomous cleaning ecosystem for Fargo Jet Center. By integrating robotics into cabin detailing, sanitization, and exterior maintenance, the facility will transition from labor-dependent models to a high-efficiency, data-driven operation. The primary goals are to reduce operational expenditures by 35-45%, enhance aircraft turnaround velocity, and establish a market-leading standard in hygiene assurance.

ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

Hub & Fleet

Operating from Fargo Hector International Airport (KFAR), the center services a diverse fleet of business jets and turboprops. The current operational model relies on high-touch manual labor for cabin and exterior upkeep.

Operational Challenges

  • Volatile labor costs tied to flight schedules.
  • Manual bottlenecks during peak flight times.
  • Variable quality in manual sanitization protocols.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

Pathway A: Cabin Cleaning

Autonomous vacuuming and wiping robots for efficient cabin detailing during turnarounds.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Mobile UV-C units for pathogen elimination, ensuring "hospital-grade" cabin air and surfaces.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Automated fuselage washing systems reducing water usage and eliminating manual height risks.

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Drone-based visual inspections for airframe integrity and predictive maintenance data.

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (5 YEARS)

  • Year 1 (Phase 1): Pilot testing of Autonomous Cabin Cleaning Robots. Infrastructure setup.
  • Year 2 (Phase 2): Expansion of cabin units. Introduction of UV-C Disinfection Robots.
  • Year 3 (Phase 3): Deployment of Exterior Washing Systems. AI Inspection integration.
  • Year 4 (Phase 4): Full fleet automation. Optimization of energy and workflow.
  • Year 5 (Phase 5): Scalability review and expansion of sustainable practices.
