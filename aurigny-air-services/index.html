Strategic Automation Research

CLIENT: AURIGNY AIR SERVICES

Executive Summary

The integration of robotic automation into Aurigny Air Services operations presents a transformative opportunity to enhance operational efficiency. As a regional airline serving the Channel Islands, automation will maintain the fleet's aesthetic integrity while significantly lowering labor dependencies and ensuring higher aircraft availability.

Organizational Profile

Fleet

Consolidated ATR 72-600 series and Dornier 228 aircraft optimized for island operations.

Hub

Primary base at Guernsey Airport (GCI) with critical links to Alderney and Southampton.

Model

High-frequency short-haul transit requiring rapid turnaround and high schedule integrity.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

PATHWAY A

Cabin Cleaning Robots

Compact autonomous vacuuming units designed for ATR aisles to execute rapid cleaning during short turnarounds.

PATHWAY B

UV-C Disinfection

Robotic arms with UV-C emitters to neutralize pathogens on seats and trays, ensuring hospital-grade hygiene.

PATHWAY C

Exterior Washing

Autonomous rigs for fuselage cleaning to reduce parasitic drag and improve fuel efficiency.

PATHWAY D

AI Inspection Bots

Computer vision drones and crawlers for detecting lightning strikes or structural stress on turboprops.

Workforce Evolution

Automation does not replace the workforce; it evolves it. By automating repetitive cleaning tasks, labor hours are shifted toward high-value technical maintenance and safety oversight.

Implementation Roadmap

  • PHASE 1 (Year 1-2): Pilot deployment of 2 Cabin Bots at Guernsey Hub.
  • PHASE 2 (Year 3): Introduction of Exterior Washing Systems and AI Inspection.
  • PHASE 3 (Year 4-5): Full fleet autonomy and predictive maintenance integration.
