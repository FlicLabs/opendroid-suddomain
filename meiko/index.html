01. Executive Summary

Meiko stands at the threshold of Industry 4.0. To maintain dominance in high-density interconnect (HDI) PCB manufacturing, the strategic pivot must move from rigid semi-automation to a Flexible Manufacturing System (FMS). By integrating collaborative robotics and autonomous logistics, Meiko can achieve Zero-Defect Manufacturing—a critical requirement for automotive clients.

02. Organizational Profile

  • ▸ Production Scale: High-volume global footprint (Japan, China, Vietnam).
  • ▸ Process Type: Discrete Manufacturing within Cleanroom environments.
  • ▸ Current Friction: "Islands of Automation" disconnected by manual material transport.

03. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobots

Machine tending for In-Circuit Test (ICT) fixtures. Force-limiting sensors allow safe human-robot collaboration without caging.

Pathway B: AMRs

Autonomous Mobile Robots for magazine transport between wet (plating) and dry (solder mask) processes.

Pathway C: EOL Automation

Delta robots for high-speed palletizing combined with AI-driven Optical Inspection (AOI) for final quality checks.
