Hospice of the Valley

Robotics & Automation Research Portfolio

Executive Summary

Hospice of the Valley, a leading non-profit provider in Arizona, faces operational challenges in logistics, clinician travel time, and high-touch dementia care. The strongest robotics levers are Socially Assistive Robotics for dementia management and Autonomous Last-Mile Logistics for supply delivery. These interventions can recover tens of thousands of clinical hours and significantly reduce operational overhead.

Pathway A: Social Robotics

Focus: Dementia & Palliative Care

Deployment of therapeutic robotic companions (e.g., PARO) to manage agitation and reduce psychotropic medication use without increasing nursing staff.

15% Reduction In behavioral crisis visits

Pathway B: Auto-Logistics

Focus: Supply Chain & Delivery

Utilizing drones or autonomous vehicles for "comfort kit" and pharmacy delivery, removing nurses from courier duties.

10k+ Hours Clinical time recovered/year

Pathway C: Inpatient Service

Focus: Facility Operations

Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for linen, waste, and meal transport in inpatient hospice homes.

2-3 FTE Labor savings per facility
