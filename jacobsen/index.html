EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Jacobsen stands at the precipice of a transformative operational shift by integrating robotics into its aviation cleaning and maintenance protocols. This research outlines a strategic pathway to automate key ground handling processes, moving from traditional manual labor to a hybrid human-robot workforce. The vision is to leverage robotic automation not merely for cleaning but for holistic asset management. By deploying autonomous systems for cabin sanitation, disinfection, exterior washing, and AI-driven inspections, Jacobsen can significantly elevate hygiene standards, reduce aircraft turnaround times, and enhance fleet availability. This transition is designed to drive substantial cost efficiencies while future-proofing operations against labor volatility and regulatory stringency.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

  • Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning AMRs equipped with vacuums and sanitizers. Reduces turnaround time by 40-50%. Features autonomous docking and self-charging.
  • Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection Robots Autonomous units utilizing high-intensity UV-C light for pathogen neutralization. Ensures log-reduction in bacterial load post-cleaning.
  • Pathway C: Exterior Aircraft Washing Automated gantry systems or scrubbers reducing water usage by 30% and minimizing paint damage risks through precision application.
  • Pathway D: AI-Powered Inspection Bots HD/Thermal drones and crawlers for predictive maintenance. Automates anomaly detection and integrates directly with MMS.
