ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

SEA Wire and Cable Inc. operates a diverse fleet servicing critical domestic and international hubs. The current operational model relies on traditional manual labor for cleaning and maintenance, presenting challenges in consistency, labor availability, and turnaround speed. Automation offers a direct solution to scale operations sustainably.

  • 1. Fleet Composition: Mixed narrow-body and wide-body aircraft.
  • 2. Hubs: High-traffic metropolitan airports requiring rapid gate availability.
  • 3. Current Model: Manual intensive cleaning and inspection protocols.
  • 4. Target: Transition to hybrid human-robot workflows.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

PATHWAY A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

AMRs for vacuuming and sanitizing cabin interiors. Utilizes LiDAR for safe navigation during tight turnarounds.

PATHWAY B: UV-C Disinfection Robots

Automated UV-C light cycles to eliminate pathogens on high-touch surfaces post-cleaning.

PATHWAY C: Exterior Washing

Gantry or robotic arm systems for eco-friendly, water-efficient exterior cleaning and polishing.

PATHWAY D: AI Inspection Bots

Drones and crawlers with thermal imaging for automated structural and component integrity checks.

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

YEAR 1: PILOT & ASSESSMENT

Deploy pilot fleet (5-10 units). Establish baseline metrics for cleaning time and quality. Train core operational teams.

YEAR 2: OPERATIONAL INTEGRATION

Expand fleet by 25%. Introduce exterior washing systems. Integrate with maintenance management software.

YEAR 3: FLEET-WIDE DEPLOYMENT

Scale deployment across all major hubs. Implement AI inspection bots for routine checks.

YEAR 4: OPTIMIZATION & MATURITY

Full autonomy for cleaning schedules. Predictive maintenance driven by AI inspection data.

YEAR 5: FUTURE-PROOFING

Evaluate emerging tech. Ensure adaptability to new aircraft models. Achieve full ROI.
