Prepared for Cape Air

Executive Summary

Cape Air operates a unique high-frequency regional model that relies on rapid turnaround times and high utilization rates of smaller airframes such as the Cessna 402 and the Tecnam P2012 Traveller. The introduction of robotic cleaning and maintenance automation presents a transformative opportunity to reduce ground time, enhance safety compliance, and alleviate labor shortages. Open Droids proposes a tailored automation strategy focusing on compact robotics suitable for narrow-fuselage aircraft and drone-based exterior maintenance.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

A. Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Deployment of Compact UGV vacuums designed for the narrow aisles of Cessna 402s and Tecnam P2012s. Focus on rapid debris removal.

B. UV-C Disinfection

Handheld or cockpit-mounted UV-C emitter bots to perform full pathogen sweeps in under 3 minutes, ensuring medical-grade sterility.

C. Exterior Washing Drones

Tethered drone systems with atomized fluid capability to clean high-wing aircraft, reducing water usage by 60%.

D. AI Inspection Bots

Optical crawling robots utilizing computer vision to detect rivet fatigue and corrosion on aging airframes overnight.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Phase 1 (Year 1): Pilot Deployment of UV-C bots at BOS Hub.
  • Phase 2 (Years 2-3): Fleet Integration of Compact UGVs and AI Inspection assistance.
  • Phase 3 (Years 4-5): System-wide automation and centralized predictive maintenance dashboard.
