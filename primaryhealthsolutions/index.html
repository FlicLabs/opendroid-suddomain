Primary Health Solutions

Robotics Consultation & Automation Strategy

Executive Summary

Primary Health Solutions (PHS) is a critical FQHC network in Ohio. Our analysis indicates that the highest ROI for PHS lies not in surgical robotics, but in Service Automation. By implementing robotic pharmacy dispensing and AI-driven mobile logistics, PHS can address chronic staffing shortages, maximize 340B revenue, and expand access to underserved populations without proportional cost increases.

Profile: FQHC Integrated Care
Scale: 10-12 Sites + Mobile Fleet
Key Goal: Operational Efficiency

Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Pharmacy Automation

High Priority Immediate ROI

Central robotic dispensing units to handle high-volume prescriptions.

$1.5M/yr

Estimated incremental benefit via 340B optimization and labor savings.

Pathway B: Mobile Logistics

Strategic Access

AI routing for school-based units and telepresence robots for remote specialists.

20% Uplift

Increase in mobile fleet utilization and patient throughput.

Pathway C: Admin RPA

Operational Software

Automated eligibility verification, billing, and scheduling.

30% Less

Reduction in front-desk administrative burden.

Financial Model (Pathway A)

Focusing on the immediate deployment of Automated Pharmacy Dispensing.

Capital Expenditure (One-time) $250,000
Annual Labor Savings +$90,000
Incremental 340B Revenue +$800k - $1.2M
Net Annual Benefit ~$1.25 Million

*Payback period estimated at 4-6 months based on current volume projections.

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Foundation & Quick Wins

Deploy Central Fill Robotics at main hub. Implement Admin RPA for insurance verification.

Year 2: Mobility & Reach

Retrofit mobile fleet with AI telematics. Pilot Telepresence robots in 3 remote school clinics.

Year 3-5: Scale & Network

Expand robotic dispensing to secondary clinics. Full network-wide telepresence rollout.

Strategic Positioning

Executing this roadmap transforms Primary Health Solutions from a standard FQHC into a Tech-Enabled Safety Net System. This allows PHS to deliver higher quality care to more patients with improved financial sustainability, setting a regional benchmark for community health innovation.
