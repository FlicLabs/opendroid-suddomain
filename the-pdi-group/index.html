The PDI Group

Strategic Report: Aviation Cleaning & Maintenance Automation

Executive Summary

The PDI Group is positioned to revolutionize its operational efficiency through the integration of advanced robotics. This strategy outlines a high-level vision where autonomous systems manage routine cleaning and inspection tasks, reducing operational expenditures by 35-45%. The transition ensures enhanced safety, superior hygiene standards, and a projected payback period of under 24 months.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

A. Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Mobile robots utilizing computer vision for rapid debris removal and surface sanitization during turnarounds.

B. UV-C Disinfection

Post-cleaning robotic deployment to neutralize pathogens on high-touch surfaces, ensuring health safety.

C. Exterior Washing

Automated gantry systems for eco-friendly fuselage scrubbing, improving aerodynamics and reducing labor risk.

D. AI Inspection Bots

LiDAR-equipped drones for pre-flight and hangar maintenance inspections, identifying micro-fractures.

Implementation Roadmap (5-Year Phased Strategy)

Year 1

Pilot Program & Infrastructure

Procurement of 20 units (across all pathways). Integration of charging infrastructure and fleet management software.

Year 2

Core Hub Integration

Full deployment of Cabin Cleaning & UV-C units at primary hubs. Staff training and KPI establishment.

Year 3

Secondary Hub Expansion

Rollout of Exterior Washing and AI Inspection Bots. Optimization based on Year 1-2 data.

Year 4

Fleet-Wide Standardization

100% coverage of cabin cleaning automation. Integration of robotics data into central maintenance records.

Year 5

Optimization & Analytics

Full predictive maintenance utilization. Continuous improvement loops and potential third-party revenue streams.
