Executive Summary

North River Home Care operates in a high-demand, labor-constrained environment. By shifting from a purely manual labor model to a tech-augmented workforce, the agency can unlock significant margin. The strategy leverages AI Scheduling to solve the "traveling salesman problem" of home care, and Wearable Exoskeletons to reduce the physical toll on caregivers, directly impacting retention.

Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: AI Logistics & Scheduling

Implementation of "Software Robotics" to manage the distributed workforce. Algorithms optimize routes based on traffic, acuity, and caregiver location.

  • Impact: Reduces unbilled travel time by 15-20%.
  • Financial: ~£250k-400k annual savings.

Pathway B: Wearable Safety Robotics

Active soft exoskeletons deployed to staff managing high-mobility patients. Provides back support during patient transfers.

  • Impact: drastically reduces workers' comp claims and extends career longevity.
  • Strategy: Position North River as the "Safest Employer" in MA.
