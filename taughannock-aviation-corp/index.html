EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Taughannock Aviation Corp. stands at the precipice of a transformative operational shift. The integration of robotic cleaning and maintenance automation offers a pathway to redefine efficiency, safety, and passenger experience. This research proposes a strategic vision where autonomous systems handle routine cleaning and inspection tasks, liberating human staff for higher-value roles. The objective is to reduce operational costs, minimize aircraft turnaround times, and elevate hygiene standards to industry-leading levels.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

1. Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Robots handle vacuuming and surface wiping autonomously. Optimized for rapid turnaround (15 mins per narrow-body).

2. UV-C Disinfection

Mobile units targeting pathogens on high-touch surfaces. Enhances passenger trust and reduces bio-risks.

3. Exterior Washing Systems

Automated gantries reducing water usage by 30% and eliminating fall risks for ground crews.

4. AI Inspection Bots

Drones/Crawlers performing NDT. Real-time data feeds into predictive maintenance models.

LABOR HOUR REDISTRIBUTION

Post-automation, labor hours shift from repetitive manual tasks to supervisory and quality control roles.

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP (5-YEAR STRATEGY)

YEAR 1

Pilot and Validation

Deploy 5 Cabin Cleaning Robots and 2 UV-C units at primary hub. Establish maintenance protocols.

YEAR 2

Expansion and Integration

Rollout to secondary hubs. Procurement of Exterior Washing Systems. 15% fleet automation coverage.

YEAR 3

Standardization and AI Adoption

Network-wide cleaning robot adoption. Introduction of AI Inspection Bots. 40% coverage.

YEAR 4

Full Operational Synergy

Unified digital ecosystem. Predictive maintenance implementation. 70% coverage.

YEAR 5

Optimization and Future-Proofing
