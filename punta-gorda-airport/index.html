** Open Droids: Strategic Automation Report for Punta Gorda Airport

PUNTA GORDA AIRPORT

Strategic Report: Robotic Cleaning & Maintenance Automation
Prepared by Open Droids Senior Consultation & Research Agent

Executive Summary

The integration of robotic automation at Punta Gorda Airport represents a pivotal shift toward next-generation aviation operations. By deploying specialized autonomous systems, PGD can address labor shortages, optimize flight turnaround times, and elevate hygiene standards. This report proposes a strategic pathway to reduce operational costs by 35-45% while ensuring the airport remains a competitive regional hub through state-of-the-art facility management.

Organizational Profile

Operational Footprint

  • Primary Carrier: Allegiant Air (Boeing 737/MD-80)
  • Hub Type: Non-Primary Commercial Service
  • Current Model: Lean operations with manual cleaning reliance

Key Challenges

  • High-frequency aircraft turnaround requirements
  • Labor constraints in cleaning and maintenance
  • Need for consistent sanitization standards

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Robots performing vacuuming, surface sanitization, and trash removal during tight turnaround windows.

  • 30-minute narrow-body cleans
  • HEPA filtration integration
  • Automated scheduling via FMS

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Autonomous units utilizing UV-C light to neutralize pathogens in cabins and gate areas overnight.

  • Targeted pathogen elimination
  • Safe for occupied/unoccupied spaces
  • Log-free operation

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Robotic platforms cleaning fuselages and wings with precision water jets and eco-friendly agents.

  • 60% water reduction
  • Improved aerodynamics (fuel savings)
  • Reduced manual scaffolding risks

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Drones and ground rovers for automated visual inspections of infrastructure and ramp safety.

  • Proactive maintenance alerts
  • Debris detection (FOD)
  • Facade integrity checks
