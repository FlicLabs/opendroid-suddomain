Executive Summary

Candler County Hospital, a Critical Access Hospital in Metter, GA, faces rural staffing pressures and patient outmigration. The robotics strategy focuses on Telepresence for specialty retention, UV-C Disinfection for safety, and Robotic Rehab to boost Swing Bed revenue. The model predicts >$1.2M cumulative benefit over 5 years.

Organizational Profile

  • Type: Critical Access Hospital (25 Beds)
  • Location: Metter, Georgia
  • Economics: Cost-based reimbursement (Medicare); ~$20M Budget.
  • Challenge: Specialist access & labor efficiency.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Telepresence

Deploying mobile specialist robots in ER/Acute Care. Reduces transfers to urban centers by 15-20%, retaining revenue locally.

Pathway B: Auto-Disinfection

UV-C robots for OR and patient rooms. Reduces HAIs, lowers liability, and saves ~1.5 hours of nursing logistics labor daily.

Pathway C: Robotic Rehab (Swing Bed)

Exoskeletons and gait trainers to differentiate the post-acute program, attracting regional volume and increasing average daily census.
