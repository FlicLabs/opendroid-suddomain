Minnesota Autism Center: Robotics & Automation Portfolio

Executive Summary: Minnesota Autism Center (MAC) is positioned to transform therapeutic delivery through "Clinical Augmentation." By leveraging Socially Assistive Robotics (SAR) and automated data systems, MAC can reduce high industry turnover, enhance client engagement, and streamline operations across its multi-center network.

1. Organizational Profile

Multi Location Network
ABA Core Therapy Model
High Admin Intensity

MAC operates a center-based model integrating mental health, behavior therapy, speech, and OT. The primary bottleneck is the balance between intensive 1:1 care and the administrative burden required for insurance compliance and data tracking.

2. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Socially Assistive Robotics (SAR)

Concept: Deploying robots (e.g., NAO, Moxie) as clinical bridges to assist therapists in social skills drills and emotional regulation.

  • Benefit: Consistent repetition without fatigue.
  • Outcome: 15-20% increase in client engagement duration; reduced therapist burnout.

Pathway B: Smart Environmental Automation

Concept: Adaptive sensory rooms with automated lighting and sound masking based on biofeedback.

  • Benefit: Faster behavioral de-escalation and standardized safety.

Pathway C: Administrative AI

Concept: Voice-to-text documentation and computer-vision data logging.

  • Benefit: Recovery of 1-2 hours of billable capacity per clinician/week.
