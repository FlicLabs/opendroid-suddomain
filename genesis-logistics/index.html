Executive Summary

Genesis Logistics is positioned to transition from labor-intensive operations to a data-driven, automated ecosystem. The strategic integration of robotics will enable a "Dark Warehouse" capability during off-peak hours, maximizing asset utilization and minimizing energy costs. This report outlines a phased approach focusing on AMRs, ASRS, and AI-driven logistics to reduce cost-per-pick and enhance throughput consistency, ensuring market leadership in the metropolitan distribution sector.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

  • Pathway A: AMRs for Internal Transport
    Focus: Replacing manual forklifts for "miles walked" efficiency. ROI via labor reduction and dynamic routing.
  • Pathway B: Robotic Piece Picking & ASRS
    Focus: Vertical storage density and picking accuracy. Reduces mis-picks and defers facility expansion.
  • Pathway C: AI-Driven Inbound/Outbound
    Focus: Predictive logistics using AI to pre-position inventory. Optimizes energy usage and prevents bottlenecks.

Performance Projections

Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pilot & Integration

Deploy AMRs in single zone. Integrate ASRS for high-velocity SKUs. Focus on staff training.

Year 2: Expansion & Optimization

Scale AMR fleet to 50% coverage. Implement AI routing for energy and path optimization.

Year 3: Systemic Automation

Full robotic picking for medium/slow movers. Achieve 30% OPEX reduction.

Year 4: Predictive Logistics

Deploy AI-driven inbound/outbound. Enable "Dark Warehouse" night operations.

Year 5: Ecosystem Scaling

Standardize tech stack for secondary hubs. Explore drone counting and advanced vision.
