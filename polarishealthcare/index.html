Polaris Healthcare: Robotics Portfolio

Executive Summary

Polaris Healthcare is positioned to transition from a traditional labor-heavy post-acute model to a tech-enabled operator. By leveraging Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for logistics and Therapeutic Robotics for rehab, Polaris can reclaim thousands of nursing hours annually and establish regional market dominance.

1. Organizational Profile

Model: Regional network of Skilled Nursing Facilities (SNFs) and Post-Acute Rehabilitation.

Scale: 10–15 facilities serving short-term rehab and long-term care populations.

Challenge: Critical staffing shortages (CNA/RN) and tight reimbursement margins necessitating operational efficiency.

2. Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Logistics

Technology: Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs)

Focus: Automating dietary, laundry, and trash transport.

Impact: High Efficiency

Pathway B: Clinical

Technology: Exoskeletons & Upper-Limb Robotics

Focus: Enhancing neuro-rehab outcomes and marketing.

Impact: Strategic Growth

Pathway C: Admin

Technology: Robotic Process Automation (RPA)

Focus: Insurance verification and billing speed.

Impact: Cash Flow

3. Financial Model (Pathway A - Logistics)

Based on a single facility deployment of 3 AMRs under a Robots-as-a-Service (RaaS) model.

Annual Transport Labor Cost £78,840
Robot Lease & Maintenance £41,000
Labor Value Reclaimed £63,000
Net Annual Benefit £22,000 + Soft Savings

4. Implementation Roadmap

  • Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Logistics Pilot. Deploy AMRs in flagship facility to validate labor hour reduction.
  • Phase 2 (Months 3-9): Admin Automation. Launch RPA for admissions to decrease denial rates.
  • Phase 3 (Year 2): Clinical Scaling. Invest in therapeutic robotics for "Center of Excellence" marketing.

5. Strategic Positioning

Adopting this portfolio allows Polaris Healthcare to market itself as a "High-Tech, High-Touch" provider. By automating the "fetching and carrying," staff return to the bedside, directly improving patient satisfaction and clinical outcomes while mitigating the risks of the national caregiver shortage.
