Executive Summary

The Cerebral Palsy Association of Nassau County (CPANC) is positioned to transform its delivery of care through strategic robotics. Currently reliant on manual labor for therapy and residential support, CPANC faces bottlenecks in staff endurance and safety. The proposed portfolio introduces Advanced Robotic Gait Training (Pathway A) and Assistive Daily Living Robotics (Pathway B). These initiatives will drive a projected 40% increase in clinical throughput and significantly reduce staff injury costs.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Robotic Rehabilitation (High Value)

Scope: Deployment of exoskeleton suits (e.g., EksoNR, Trexo) in the Fortunoff Treatment Center.
Impact: Increases therapy repetitions without staff fatigue, gamifies recovery for pediatric patients, and serves as a major differentiator for donor funding.


Pathway B: Assistive Daily Living (Operational)

Scope: Robotic feeding devices and powered transfer robots in residential homes.
Impact: Directly addresses the high rate of staff back injuries and grants residents greater autonomy in dining and movement.
