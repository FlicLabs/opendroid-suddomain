01. Executive Summary

Keltec Technolab is positioned to transition from traditional manufacturing to a Flexible Manufacturing System (FMS). By integrating Collaborative Robotics and AMRs, the organization can decouple labor hours from production volume, ensuring higher throughput reliability and reduced cost-per-unit.

02. Organizational Profile

Environment: High-Mix, Low-to-Mid Volume (HMLV).

Current State: Hybrid model utilizing Discrete Manufacturing for assembly and batch-processes for finishing. Current functional layouts create bottlenecks during shift handovers.

Strategy: Shift from functional layouts to cellular manufacturing to maximize automation potential.

03. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobot Machine Tending

SafetyPrecision

Deployment of IP67-rated Cobots for CNC machine tending. This addresses the "Idle Spindle" problem and enables lights-out manufacturing potential.

Pathway B: AMR Logistics

EfficiencyFlow

Autonomous Mobile Robots to replace manual forklifts for WIP transport, reducing floor congestion and ensuring Just-In-Time delivery.

Pathway C: Auto-Palletizing & QA

YieldSpeed

Vision-guided robotic arms for final packaging and automated optical inspection (AOI), guaranteeing 100% inspection rates.

04. Financial Model & Projections

The financial strategy relies on a CAPEX of $60k-$120k per cell with a target operational saving driven by labor reallocation and scrap reduction.

05. Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Pilot Phase
Digital twin simulation and deployment of first Cobot Tending cell.
Year 2: Expansion
Rollout of AMR fleet and additional tending cells.
Year 3: Integration
Full MES integration and automated palletizing.
Year 5: Smart Factory
AI-driven generative design and 24/7 autonomous capability.
