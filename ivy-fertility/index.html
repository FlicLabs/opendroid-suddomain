Ivy Fertility

Robotics Analysis & ROI Model

Executive Summary

Ivy Fertility is a nationally recognized network of reproductive medicine clinics and laboratories known for high success rates and advanced embryology services. The integration of robotics and AI automation offers a pathway to standardize lab procedures, secure the chain of custody for biomaterials, and increase throughput without compromising the delicate nature of gametes and embryos. The primary ROI drivers are the reduction of manual labor in cryostorage, standardization of embryo culture via automated incubation systems, and eventually, robotic-assisted Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI).

Strategic Implementation

Phase 1: Immediate Deployment (Months 1-12)

Deploy Automated Cryostorage. Retrofit main biobanks in California and New Jersey hubs. Integrate RFID tracking with EMR to secure chain of custody.

Phase 2: Intelligence Layer (Months 12-24)

Upgrade Incubators. Install time-lapse systems with AI grading software. Train staff on data interpretation to standardize embryo selection.

Phase 3: Robotic Intervention (Months 24-48)

Pilot Robotic ICSI. Partner with hardware startup for beta testing in a single high-volume lab to validate scalability.
