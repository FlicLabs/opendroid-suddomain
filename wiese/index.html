01. Executive Summary

Wiese is positioned to transition from traditional mechanical fabrication to a Flexible Manufacturing Ecosystem. By integrating collaborative robotics and autonomous transport into the production of rubber chains and bucket assemblies, Wiese can move from fixed-cycle batch production to a dynamic, high-mix/low-volume model. This strategic shift addresses ergonomic risks and unlocks significant throughput potential.

02. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

PATHWAY A: Collaborative Assembly

Deployment of high-payload Cobots (10-20kg) to assist in the vulcanization press tending and bucket fastening. Removes operators from heat zones and ensures precision torquing.

PATHWAY B: AMR Intralogistics

Utilization of Autonomous Mobile Robots for the transport of raw rubber bales and heavy steel inserts, reducing forklift traffic and line-side congestion.

PATHWAY C: Automated Inspection

End-of-Line robotic cells equipped with vision systems to perform palletizing and micro-fissure detection in rubber chains simultaneously.
