Strategic Automation Report

Prepared for Global Repair Group | Aviation Maintenance & Cleaning
Executive Vision

Global Repair Group faces a pivotal opportunity to transition from variable manual labor to high-precision robotics. By integrating Open Droids automation, the group will significantly reduce turnaround times (TAT), enhance safety compliance, and secure a 35-45% reduction in long-term operational expenses.

Organizational Profile
  • Fleet Volume: 250+ Narrow Body, 80 Wide Body annually.
  • Infrastructure: 3 Major International Hubs.
  • Current Model: High-variance manual labor.
Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Roaming HEPA-filtered units for carpets and seating. drastically reducing aircraft turn time between legs.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Hospital-grade sanitization using germicidal light, eliminating pathogens without corrosive chemicals.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Automated gantries reducing wash time to < 2 hours and improving fuel efficiency via drag reduction.

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Computer vision drones creating digital twins for auditability, checking for lightning strikes and micro-cracks.
