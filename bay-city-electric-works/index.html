01. Executive Vision

Bay City Electric Works is positioned to transition from a traditional distributor model to a Flexible High-Mix, Low-Volume Integrator. The strategic objective is to leverage "Power-as-a-Service" efficiency by reducing internal bottlenecks in heavy assembly and material transport. By adopting smart robotics, BCEW will enhance safety, ensure traceability in critical electrical assembly, and optimize inventory flow.

02. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

  • PATHWAY A: Collaborative Assembly (Cobots) Focus on high-torque assistance for enclosure mounting and vision-based validation for control panel wiring. Improves ergonomics and reducing defect rates.
  • PATHWAY B: Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) Deployment of heavy-duty AMRs (e.g., MiR600) to transport generator chassis and engines. Eliminates manual forklift dependency and increases floor layout flexibility.
  • PATHWAY C: Automated Quality & Testing Integration of robotic sensor arms in Load Bank testing to automate data logging and thermal scanning, providing digital quality reports instantly.

04. 5-Year Implementation Roadmap

YEAR 1: Foundation — Wi-Fi surveys, AMR pilot for receiving, "Center of Excellence" team formation.
YEAR 2: Assembly — Cobot integration for torque assist and MES software rollout.
YEAR 3: Connectivity — Automated testing rigs and central fleet dashboarding.
YEAR 4: Expansion — Replication of pilot cells to Nevada and Northern CA facilities.
YEAR 5: AI Optimization — Predictive ordering and fully autonomous internal logistics.
