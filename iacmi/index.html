Executive Summary

The Institute for Advanced Composites Manufacturing Innovation (IACMI) stands at the intersection of robotics and materials science. The primary optimization vector is the transition from labor-intensive layup to Automated Fiber Placement (AFP) and robotic finishing. This shift addresses critical bottlenecks in cycle time and material yield, essential for automotive and wind energy scaling.

Pathway A: Automated Fiber Placement

Scope: Deployment of multi-axis robotic arms equipped with AFP heads to replace manual layup.

Impact: Reduces material scrap by 25% through precision nesting. Enables complex geometries for automotive lightweighting.

Pathway B: Robotic Post-Processing

Scope: Force-controlled robotic cells for trimming, drilling, and deflashing cured parts.

Impact: Eliminates hazardous dust exposure for workers. Ensures consistent tolerance for Tier 1 assembly requirements.
