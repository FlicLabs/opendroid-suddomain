Suncoast Community Health Centers

Robotics Analysis & ROI Portfolio

Executive Summary

Suncoast Community Health Centers Inc. is a critical Federally Qualified Health Center (FQHC) serving the Tampa Bay region. Operating with high volumes and low margins, the primary optimization goal is minimizing administrative overhead to expand patient access.

Key Insight: The dominant ROI driver is not labor replacement, but the reduction of uncompensated administrative time via Robotic Process Automation (RPA) and Pharmacy Automation.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: RPA (Software Robotics)

Scope: Automating eligibility checks, claims scrubbing, and referral management.

Impact: Saves 40-60 admin hours/week per clinic. Reduces claim denials by 15-20%.

Pathway B: Pharmacy Automation

Scope: Robotic pill counters and dispensing cabinets.

Impact: Near-zero dispensing errors, faster throughput, tight 340B inventory control.

Pathway C: Intake Kiosks

Scope: Automated vital sign collection stations.

Impact: Reduces rooming time by 3-5 mins/patient. Increases provider capacity.

Implementation Roadmap

1

Year 1: Digital Foundation

Deploy RPA for Medicaid eligibility verification to stop denials. Pilot pharmacy robotics in the highest volume clinic (e.g., Ruskin).

2

Year 2: Clinical Efficiency

Roll out automated intake kiosks to top 5 busiest locations. Expand RPA to referral tracking.

3

Year 3: Integration

Full pharmacy robotics integration. Explore telepresence for remote migrant health consults.

Strategic Positioning

Adopting this portfolio transforms Suncoast from a labor-constrained safety net provider into a Tech-Enabled Community Health System. The efficiency gains directly fund the mission: hiring more doctors to treat the underserved.
