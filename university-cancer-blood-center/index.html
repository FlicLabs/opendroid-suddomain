University Cancer & Blood Center

Executive Summary

University Cancer & Blood Center (UCBC) is a private oncology and hematology practice serving Georgia and the broader southeastern United States, operating across multiple locations including Atlanta, Conyers, Decatur, Fayetteville, Lawrenceville, Newnan, and Snellville. As a large-scale community oncology provider, UCBC manages a high volume of chemotherapy infusions, diagnostic imaging, laboratory testing, and clinical trial administration. The practice’s operational model relies heavily on nurse-led infusion workflows, precise medication compounding, and seamless coordination between clinical and administrative departments. The strongest robotics and automation levers for UCBC are: (1) AI-driven inventory and medication management to reduce waste and ensure safety, (2) robotic-assisted logistics and transport for internal supply chain management, and (3) robotic process automation (RPA) for back-office billing and prior authorization workflows. The dominant ROI driver is improved staff efficiency and reduced operational costs, enabling millions in annual savings with short to moderate payback periods.

Organizational Profile

  • • Model: Community-based oncology and hematology practice offering chemotherapy infusions, immunotherapy, diagnostic services, and clinical trials.
  • • Scale: 7+ locations across Georgia, serving a large patient population in the Atlanta metro area and surrounding communities.
  • • Partnerships: Affiliated with major hospitals and health systems in the region; participates in value-based care initiatives and clinical research networks.
  • • Economics: Revenue primarily from fee-for-service oncology treatments, diagnostic procedures, and clinical trial reimbursements. Annual revenue is estimated in the tens of millions based on scale and service breadth.

Operational bottlenecks are primarily in medication inventory management, supply chain logistics, patient flow during infusion hours, and administrative backlogs in billing and prior authorization, making UCBC ideal for targeted robotics and automation interventions.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A – Medication Inventory & Compounding Automation (High Value, Safety Focus)

Scope: Robotic pharmacy systems for automated medication storage, retrieval, and compounding of chemotherapy and supportive care drugs.

Key Levers:

  • Automated dispensing cabinets and robots for secure medication storage and tracking.
  • Robotic compounding systems that prepare IV solutions with high precision, reducing human error.
  • AI-driven inventory forecasting to minimize drug waste and expiration.

Impact (Mid-Case):

  • Reduction in medication waste by 10–20%, saving approximately $200k–$500k annually.
  • Decrease in compounding errors by up to 90%, enhancing patient safety and reducing liability.
  • Improved nurse time allocation, allowing 15–20% more patients per nurse per day.

Pathway B – Internal Logistics & Supply Chain Robotics (Medium Value, Efficiency Focus)

Scope: Autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) for transporting medications, supplies, and lab samples between pharmacy, infusion centers, and laboratories.

Key Levers:

  • AMRs equipped with temperature-controlled compartments for chemotherapy drugs and biological samples.
  • RFID tracking integrated with robotic transport to ensure chain-of-custody and reduce loss.

Impact (Mid-Case):

  • Reduction in manual transport time by 30–40%, saving approximately $150k–$250k annually in staff hours.
  • Decreased turnaround time for lab results and medication delivery by 25%, improving patient throughput.

Pathway C – Robotic Process Automation (RPA) for Administrative Workflows (High Value, Scalability Focus)

Scope: RPA bots for prior authorization, billing, claims processing, and appointment scheduling.

Key Levers:

  • Automated submission and follow-up of prior authorizations with insurance payers.
  • RPA-driven claims scrubbing and submission to reduce denials and rework.
  • Intelligent scheduling bots that optimize appointment slots based on clinician availability and patient acuity.

Impact (Mid-Case):

  • Reduction in administrative labor costs by 20–30%, saving $300k–$600k annually.
  • Decrease in claim denial rates by 10–15 percentage points, improving cash flow by $500k–$1M annually.
  • Faster prior authorization turnaround, reducing treatment delays by 2–3 days on average.

Financial Model – Pathway A (Selected)

Network baseline (estimated):

  • Annual chemotherapy infusions: ~20,000–30,000 across all locations.
  • Medication acquisition cost: ~$10–15 million annually.
  • Nursing staff cost: ~$3–5 million annually for infusion services.

Automation scenario:

  • Medication waste reduction: 15% of $12M acquisition cost → $1.8M savings.
  • Compounding error reduction: Liability and quality cost savings of ~$200k–$500k annually.
  • Nurse efficiency uplift: 15% more patients per nurse → equivalent to 1.5 FTE savings (~$120k–$180k annually).

Platform cost (robotic pharmacy systems + software):

  • Initial capital: $500k–$1M per major location (scaling to 3–4 key sites).
  • Annual maintenance and SaaS: $150k–$300k.

Result:

  • Net incremental benefit: $1.5–2.5M annually after platform costs.
  • Payback: 6–18 months depending on site volume.
  • 5-year cumulative net: $7.5–12.5M.

Implementation Roadmap (High-Level)

  • Year 1 – Pathway A Pilot + Pathway C Scaling: Implement robotic pharmacy systems at 1–2 flagship locations (e.g., Atlanta main campus). Deploy RPA for billing and prior authorization across all locations, starting with highest-volume payers. 3–6 month pilot for medication automation; scale to 3–4 key sites within 12 months.
