01. Executive Vision

Froehling & Robertson Inc. is poised to transition from a manual testing service model to an Automated Laboratory Manufacturing ecosystem. By treating sample intake, preparation, and destructive testing as a linear manufacturing process, F&R can leverage robotics to eliminate technician fatigue, ensure data integrity, and drastically increase testing throughput for concrete and soil mechanics.

02. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobot Machine Tending

Focus: Safety & Precision

Deployment of UR10e or Fanuc CRX units for automated concrete cylinder compression. The cobot lifts 30lb cylinders, loads the machine, and removes debris, eliminating Repetitive Strain Injuries (RSI).

Pathway B: AMR Logistics

Focus: WIP Flow Efficiency

Autonomous Mobile Robots transporting samples from intake docks to curing rooms and testing labs. Digital chain-of-custody tracking via RFID during transport.

Pathway C: Automated QA

Focus: Throughput & Yield

Computer Vision systems for aggregate analysis and automated disposal sorting. Removes human error in visual grading and accelerates post-test processing.

03. Implementation Roadmap

Year 1: Foundation & Pilot

Audit Richmond HQ workflows. Deploy single Cobot cell for compression testing. Digitize sample tracking.

Year 2-3: Expansion

Introduce AMRs for logistics. Integrate machine data into LIMS. Replicate "Smart Lab" model in Raleigh and Baltimore.

Year 4-5: The Autonomous Lab

Predictive maintenance via IoT. Full "Lights-Out" third shift capability. Intake-to-disposal automation.
