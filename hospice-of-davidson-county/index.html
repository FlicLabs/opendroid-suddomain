Hospice of Davidson County

Robotics & Automation Portfolio

Executive Summary

Hospice of Davidson County, a leading non-profit provider in North Carolina, is poised to transform palliative care through strategic automation. Operating the Henry Etta & Bruce Hinkle Hospice House and a robust home-care network, the organization faces critical nursing shortages and logistical burdens. The robotic integration strategy focuses on telepresence for rural reach, AMRs for facility logistics, and therapeutic robotics for patient comfort. This approach shifts the value curve from purely labor-intensive care to technology-augmented compassion.

Pathway A: High Value

Telepresence & AI Monitoring

Scope: Remote monitoring hubs and avatar robots for home patients.

Impact: Reduces unbillable travel time by 20-30% and increases caseload capacity without burnout.

ROI: Immediate mileage savings and OT reduction.

Pathway B: Facility Ops

Inpatient Service AMRs

Scope: Autonomous TUG-style robots for linens, meals, and meds at Hinkle House.

Impact: Reclaims 1-2 nursing hours per shift; reduces staff injury risk.

ROI: Medium-term payback via retention and labor efficiency.

Pathway C: Clinical Quality

Therapeutic Robotics

Scope: Biofeedback pets (e.g., Paro) for dementia/anxiety.

Impact: Reduces reliance on psychotropic meds; improves family satisfaction.

ROI: High intangible value; quality metric improvement.
