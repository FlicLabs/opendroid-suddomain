Executive Summary

The future of aviation maintenance at Reno-Tahoe International Airport (RNO) is autonomous. By transitioning from manual labor to a robotic ecosystem, RNO can expect to reduce operational expenditures by 35% to 45% within 24 months. This report outlines a strategic vision for integrating specialized robotics for cabin cleaning, disinfection, exterior washing, and AI inspection. The proposed five-year roadmap ensures a scalable, low-risk transition that aligns with RNO's seasonal operational peaks and commitment to excellence.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Four specific robotic solutions designed for the RNO operational environment.

Pathway A: Cabin Cleaning

Autonomous robots vacuuming and wiping cabin surfaces. Reduces ground time and ensures consistency.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Post-cleaning UV-C sanitization for lavatories and galleys. Eliminates pathogens without chemicals.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Ground-based robotic wash systems. Improves fuel efficiency and fuselage longevity.

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Drones and crawlers for FOD detection and infrastructure integrity checks.

Labor Hour Redistribution

Automation does not replace staff; it reallocates them to higher-value roles.

Implementation Roadmap (5-Year Strategy)

Year 1: Pilot Phase

Deployment of 2 Cabin Cleaning units and 1 Inspection bot. Establishing baseline metrics.

Year 2: Expansion

Scaling Cabin Cleaning fleet. Pilot Exterior Washing (Pathway C) for regional jets.

Year 3: Integration

Full Cabin fleet deployment. Introduction of UV-C Disinfection (Pathway B).

Year 4: Automation Ecosystem

Unified fleet management dashboard. "Lights Out" overnight operations.

Year 5: Optimization

Solar charging stations. Advanced predictive maintenance for airfield surfaces.
