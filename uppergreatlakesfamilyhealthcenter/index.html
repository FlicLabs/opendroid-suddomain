Upper Great Lakes Family Health Center: Robotics & Automation Portfolio

Executive Summary: UGL operates in a challenging rural environment across Michigan's Upper Peninsula. The strongest ROI lies not in surgical robotics, but in Telepresence (access), Pharmacy Automation (efficiency), and Drone Logistics (transport). Implementing these levers can generate roughly $1.1M in annual net benefit while solving critical rural staffing and access shortages.

Organizational Profile

Model: Federally Qualified Health Center (FQHC).

Region: Michigan's Upper Peninsula (9 counties, 20+ locations).

Economics: Dependent on PPS reimbursement, 340B pharmacy savings, and grants. Margins are tight; efficiency is paramount.

Key Constraint: Severe winter weather and vast distances create logistical bottlenecks for patients and lab supplies.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

PATHWAY A: Telepresence

Concept: Mobile robotic carts in remote clinics and schools connecting to central specialists.

Impact: Reduces provider windshield time; captures ~6,000-8,000 new visits/year.

Est. Value: $800k - $1.2M / year.

PATHWAY B: Pharmacy Auto

Concept: High-volume robotic dispensing to automate filling/labeling.

Impact: Reduces errors; frees pharmacists for billable clinical services (MTM).

Est. Value: $200k - $400k / year.

PATHWAY C: Drone Logistics

Concept: Autonomous UAVs for lab sample transport over rugged terrain.

Impact: Cuts courier costs by 40%; reduces turnaround time from 24h to 2h.

Est. Value: $100k - $200k / year.

Strategic Positioning

Adopting this portfolio transforms Upper Great Lakes Family Health Center into a Tech-Enabled Rural Health Hub. It solves the geography problem through technology, attracts top-tier talent willing to work in rural settings, and secures federal grant funding by demonstrating a scalable model for modern rural healthcare.
