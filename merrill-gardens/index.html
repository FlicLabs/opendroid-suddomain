Core Robotics Pathways

Merrill Gardens, a family-owned senior living operator with over 60 communities, faces industry-wide challenges: labor shortages and rising operational costs. By deploying robotics and automation, the company can shift the focus from repetitive physical tasks to high-value resident care. The portfolio focuses on three key pathways: Logistics Automation, Assistive Robotics, and Facility Monitoring.

Implementation Roadmap

YEAR 1: Pilot Pathway A (Logistics Automation). Select 2-3 flagship communities for AMR deployment. Establish baseline metrics for labor efficiency and safety.

YEAR 2-3: Scale Pathway C (Monitoring/IoT) across the portfolio. Launch pilot for Pathway B (Assistive Robotics) in high-acuity units to test resident adoption and clinical outcomes.

YEAR 4-5: Full rollout of logistics automation. Expansion of clinical robotics based on data. Marketing "Smart Senior Living" enabled by Open Droids technology.
