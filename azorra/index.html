Research & Automation Strategy: Azorra

Executive Summary

Azorra stands at a pivotal juncture in the regional and crossover aircraft market. As a modern lessor and fleet manager focusing on the Embraer E2 and Airbus A220 families, the integration of robotics into maintenance and cleaning protocols represents a significant opportunity to protect asset value and offer superior service level agreements to lessees. This report outlines a vision where Azorra deploys autonomous cleaning and inspection units to reduce ground time and standardize compliance.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cabin Cleaning

Autonomous vacuuming and debris removal navigating narrow aisles of regional jets (E2/A220).

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Hospital-grade sanitization without harsh chemicals, preserving interior materials and value.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Automated gantries or drones for washing, essential for corrosion prevention and paint longevity.

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Computer vision drones creating digital twins for indisputable lease return condition reporting.

5-Year Implementation Roadmap

  • Phase 1 (Year 1): Pilot deployment at one primary MRO partner facility (2 Interior Bots, 1 Inspection Drone).
  • Phase 2 (Years 2-3): Expansion to three major global hubs and integration of UV-C standards.
  • Phase 3 (Years 4-5): Full fleet autonomy, centralized AI command center, and global certification.
