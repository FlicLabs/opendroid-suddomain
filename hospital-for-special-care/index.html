1. Hospital for Special Care: Robotics Analysis & ROI Model

Executive Summary

Hospital for Special Care (HSC) is a nationally recognized, free-standing chronic care hospital in New Britain, Connecticut, specializing in complex rehabilitation, respiratory care, and autism services. As a 260-bed facility with deep ties to UConn Health and the Institute for Rehabilitation, HSC is positioned to lead in assistive and therapeutic robotics. Unlike general acute-care facilities, HSC’s value proposition rests on functional outcomes, reduced length of stay, and the management of high-acuity chronic conditions. The strongest robotics levers are: (1) Robotic Rehabilitation & Mobility Systems for intensive neuro-motor recovery, (2) Patient Handling & Transfer Automation to reduce staff injury and improve workflow, and (3) Surgical & Diagnostic Precision Support (via partnership models) for specialized orthopedic cases. The dominant ROI driver is operational efficiency—specifically, nurse retention and throughput gains driven by safer patient handling and higher-intensity therapy sessions.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A – Robotic Rehabilitation & Mobility (Highest Strategic Value)

  • Scope: Deployment of exoskeletons, robotic gait trainers, and balance therapy systems for SCI, TBI, and stroke populations.
  • Key Levers: High-repetition task-specific training; Objective data capture; Expansion of outpatient capacity.
  • Impact: Reduction in average inpatient rehab length of stay by 3–5 days; Improvement in FIM scores; Capacity to treat 20% more patients per therapist annually.

Pathway B – Patient Handling & Transfer Automation (Immediate Operational ROI)

  • Scope: Ceiling-mounted lift systems, mobile patient transfer robots, and sit-to-stand assistive devices.
  • Benefits: Reduction in workplace injuries by 40–60%; Faster patient turn times; Improved patient dignity and safety.
  • Profile: Medium capex, low regulatory risk, high immediate impact on staff retention.

Pathway C – Telepresence & Remote Monitoring (Scalable Efficiency)

  • Scope: Telepresence robots for specialist consultations and remote monitoring for ventilator-dependent patients.
  • Benefits: Reduction in specialist travel time; Enhanced family visitation; Early detection of respiratory deterioration.

2. The Institute for Rehabilitation at HSC

The Institute for Rehabilitation (IFR) at HSC is the R&D and clinical testing ground for assistive technology. Current infrastructure includes hydrotherapy and body-weight support systems. The IFR is ideal for piloting next-generation rehabilitation exoskeletons and robotic balance systems, offering a controlled environment to prove functional outcomes before broader deployment.

Strategic Opportunity: Leverage robotics capital to attract NIH or industry-sponsored trials, offsetting equipment costs and enhancing the hospital's reputation as a technology-enabled rehab center.
