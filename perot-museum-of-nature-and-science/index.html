Perot Museum of Nature and Science

Robotics Analysis & ROI Model

The Perot Museum, a 180,000 sq ft cultural institution with ~1M annual visitors, faces operational challenges in facility maintenance, security, and peak-time visitor flow. The most immediate automation opportunities lie in autonomous facility management (cleaning/security) and AI-driven visitor engagement. Implementing these robotics pathways creates a cleaner environment, reduces labor overhead, and modernizes the brand experience.

Organizational Profile

  • Model: Non-profit 501(c)(3) science museum.
  • Scale: 1M+ visitors, 5 exhibit floors, 180k sq ft.
  • Operational Pain Points: Overnight turnaround times, vertical transportation (elevator) queues, complex architectural cleaning.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Facility Management

Deployment of Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) for floor care and telepresence security sentries.

Pathway B: STEM Education & Outreach

Integration of "Cobots" and Boston Dynamics-style units into the TECH Truck fleet to drive grant funding and engagement.

Pathway C: Visitor Experience

Service robots for lobby queue management and wayfinding to alleviate elevator congestion.

Strategic Positioning

Executing this roadmap positions the Perot Museum not just as a displayer of science, but as an active user of applied robotics. This "Living Exhibit" strategy improves operational margins while reinforcing the core mission of STEM inspiration.
