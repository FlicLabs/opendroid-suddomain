Riggs Community Health Center: Robotics Portfolio

Executive Summary

Riggs Community Health Center (Riggs CHC) is a vital FQHC in Lafayette, Indiana. The primary automation opportunities lie in 340B pharmacy optimization, revenue cycle RPA, and autonomous facility logistics. Implementing these technologies addresses the "triple aim" of cost reduction, improved patient access, and staff burnout mitigation.

Organizational Profile

Model: Federally Qualified Health Center (FQHC) & PCMH.
Scale: High-volume safety-net provider for Tippecanoe County.
Economics: Heavy reliance on Medicaid, Grants, and 340B Pharmacy Revenue.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Pharmacy Automation

Scope: Robotic pill dispensing and packaging.

Impact: Reduces fill time from 5 mins to <30 secs. Increases volume capacity by 40%.

Status: Highest Priority / Immediate ROI

Pathway B: Admin RPA

Scope: Software bots for Medicaid eligibility & billing.

Impact: cuts administrative denial rates by 15-20% and reduces manual entry hours.

Status: High Strategic Value

Pathway C: Service Robots

Scope: Autonomous cleaning & lab sample transport.

Impact: Saves nursing time and reduces janitorial contract costs.

Status: Operational Efficiency

Strategic Positioning

Adopting this portfolio transforms Riggs CHC into a Tech-Enabled Integrated Care Hub. It secures the economic future of the clinic by maximizing the high-margin 340B pharmacy program and ensuring that human staff focus on complex patient needs rather than repetitive administrative or logistical tasks.
