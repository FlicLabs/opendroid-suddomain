01. Executive Vision

Zero Hunger via Zero Latency

To meet the growing demands of the NWA region, the Northwest Arkansas Food Bank must transition from a manual hub-and-spoke model to a data-driven, automated logistics center. By integrating AMRs and AI, we target a reduction in food waste and an increase in calorie distribution velocity, ensuring donor funds directly translate to community nourishment rather than operational overhead.

03. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

A.

AMR Transport

Deploying Autonomous Mobile Robots for dock-to-staging transport. Reduces volunteer physical strain and forklift traffic in pedestrian zones.

B.

Robotic Piece Picking

Utilization of "Follow-Me" bots for kitting mobile pantry boxes and VLM systems for high-density cold storage optimization.

C.

AI Logistics

Predictive analytics for inbound donation spikes and outbound agency demand, ensuring FIFO compliance and reduced spoilage.

04. Implementation Roadmap

YR 1
Foundation & Safety: WMS Audit, Safety Protocol establishment, and deployment of 2 pilot AMRs.
YR 2
Intelligent Inventory: Integration of AI-tracking and "Zone Picking" assistance for dry goods.
YR 3
Cold Chain: Installation of high-density freezer automation to reduce energy costs.
YR 5
Autonomous Hub: Full ecosystem integration serving as a model for the Feeding America network.
