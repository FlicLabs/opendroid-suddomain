SC AVIATION AUTOMATION STRATEGY

Strategic Research for Aviation Cleaning and Maintenance Automation

Prepared by Open Droids Senior Consultation & Research Agent

Executive Summary

The aviation industry is shifting towards automation to enhance efficiency and safety. SC Aviation must integrate robotic cleaning systems to streamline operations, ensure hygiene consistency, and reduce turnaround times. This strategy leverages autonomous systems to handle routine tasks, allowing human staff to focus on complex quality control and passenger interaction.

Organizational Profile

Fleet: Narrow & Wide-body mix.

Hub Presence: Major metropolitan airports with high volume.

Current Model: Labor-intensive manual cleaning. Scalability limited by headcount; variable quality and high operational costs.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

LiDAR-mapped navigation for vacuuming and wiping. Ensures rapid turnover and consistent hygiene standards.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Mobile units for chemical-free sterilization. Critical for enhancing passenger safety perception.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Robotic gantry systems reducing water waste and safety risks while improving aerodynamic efficiency.

Pathway D: AI Inspection

Thermal and visual sensors for proactive maintenance. Detects micro-cracks before failure.

Implementation Roadmap (5-Year Phased Strategy)

Year 1: Assessment & Pilot Program (Cabin & UV-C robots at primary hubs).
Year 2: Integration & Expansion (Domestic hubs, AI Inspection start).
Year 3: Full Fleet Deployment (100% Cabin & UV-C coverage).
Year 4: Exterior Systems & Scale (Major bases, full fleet inspection).
Year 5: Optimization & AI Maturity (Predictive maintenance, full ROI).
