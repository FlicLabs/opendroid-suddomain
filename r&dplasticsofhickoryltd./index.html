Executive Summary

This research outlines a strategic roadmap for R & D Plastics of Hickory Ltd. to transition from traditional injection molding workflows to a flexible, Smart Factory model. By leveraging Industry 4.0 technologies, the organization can mitigate regional labor shortages, improve cycle time consistency, and unlock "lights-out" manufacturing capabilities.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobots

Machine Tending & Assembly: Focus on safety and precision. Automated gate trimming and insert loading to reduce cycle time variance and repetitive strain.

Pathway B: AMRs

Material Transport: Autonomous Mobile Robots for moving raw resin to hoppers and finished goods to shipping, smoothing WIP flow.

Pathway C: QC Automation

Inspection & Palletizing: Vision systems for 100% flash detection and automated end-of-line palletizing for maximum throughput.

Financial Model

Estimated CAPEX: $60k - $120k per robotic cell depending on vision system integration.

OPEX Impact: Target 20-30% increase in Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE).

ROI Strategy: Payback achieved in under 18 months via labor reallocation ($45k/shift/year savings) and scrap reduction.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1 Pilot Phase: Deploy 2 Machine Tending Cobots.
  • Year 2 Integration: Expand to assembly & introduce Vision QC.
  • Year 3 Logistics: Deploy AMRs for material handling.
  • Year 4 Optimization: Predictive maintenance & ERP integration.
  • Year 5 Smart Factory: Semi-autonomous lights-out manufacturing.
