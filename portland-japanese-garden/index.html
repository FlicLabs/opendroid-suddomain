Portland Japanese Garden

Robotics Opportunity Portfolio & ROI Analysis

01. Executive Summary

The Portland Japanese Garden (PJG) is a world-class cultural institution facing unique operational challenges due to steep topography, meticulous aesthetic standards, and high visitor volume. This analysis identifies robotics not as a replacement for the artistry of gardening, but as a critical infrastructure layer to solve accessibility and logistics.

The primary ROI driver is Pathway A: Automated Accessibility, which opens the garden to mobility-impaired demographics, driving ticket revenue and inclusivity.

04. Strategic Deep Dive

Pathway A: Automated Accessibility (Revenue Driver)

Scope: Deployment of "WHILL" style autonomous personal mobility devices that can navigate steep switchbacks without staff pushing.

Impact: Captures the senior travel market previously deterred by the terrain. Reduces the physical burden on staff. Increases visitor throughput.

Pathway B: Landscape Logistics

Scope: "Follow-me" robotic carts for transporting soil, stone, and tools up steep grades.

Impact: Reduces worker compensation claims related to back injuries. Allows skilled gardeners to focus on pruning rather than hauling.

05. Implementation Roadmap

Year 1 Pilot Phase: Deploy 5 autonomous chairs on main routes. Integrate shuttle scheduling AI.
Year 2 Logistics Scale: Introduce utility rovers for gardening staff. Begin drone canopy analysis for tree health.
Year 3-5 Full Integration: Expand mobility fleet to 20 units. Automated environmental controls for LEED buildings.
