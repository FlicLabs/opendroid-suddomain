1. Executive Summary

The casual dining landscape is shifting. For Frisch's Big Boy, the strategic imperative is to modernize operations while preserving brand heritage. By integrating collaborative robotics and AI, Frisch's can transition to a smart dining ecosystem, automating repetitive tasks like food running and drive-thru order taking to liberate human staff for high-value hospitality.

2. Robotics Pathways

Pathway A: FOH Serving Robots

The Runner Model: Deployment of shelf-style AMRs to handle buffet clearing and food delivery. This keeps servers in their sections.

Pathway B: Automated Frying

Consistency Engine: Auto-basket frying for fries and onion rings to ensure exact cook times and improve safety.

Pathway C: AI Drive-Thru

Voice Agents: Conversational AI to handle order taking, ensuring 100% upsell attempt rates and accurate KDS entry.

3. Financial Model

  • CAPEX: Approx $45,000 per store (2 Robots + 1 AI License).
  • OPEX Impact: Target 20% reduction in support staff hours.
  • Net Savings: Est. $3,200/mo per location in labor offset.
  • Breakeven: Achieved in under 11 months via labor savings and increased table turnover revenue.

Projected cumulative savings based on labor reduction and efficiency gains across a 10-store pilot program.
