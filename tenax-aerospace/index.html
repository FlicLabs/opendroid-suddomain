ORGANIZATIONAL PROFILE

Tenax Aerospace operates as a dynamic carrier with a diverse fleet profile, currently managing approximately 75 aircraft. The primary operational hubs are situated in high-traffic international airports, necessitating rapid turnaround times and consistent service quality. The current operational model relies heavily on manual labor for cabin cleaning, exterior washing, and routine inspections. This dependency introduces variables related to staffing availability, training consistency, and labor costs.

ROBOTICS OPPORTUNITY PATHWAYS

Pathway A: Autonomous Cabin Cleaning

Implementation of autonomous mobile robots designed for routine cabin upkeep. These units are equipped with advanced navigation systems to sanitize high-touch surfaces, allowing staff to focus on deeper cleaning requirements.

Pathway B: UV-C Disinfection

Deployment of mobile UV-C light systems for rapid cabin sanitization. These robots neutralize airborne and surface pathogens, significantly reducing the risk of cross-contamination during short turnaround windows.

Pathway C: Exterior Washing

Adoption of automated exterior washing systems to replace manual fuselage cleaning. These systems ensure uniform coverage, reduce water consumption, and minimize aircraft downtime.

Pathway D: AI-Powered Inspection

Integration of AI-powered drones for pre-flight inspections. These units detect surface anomalies and structural damage, providing real-time data analytics to streamline maintenance protocols.
