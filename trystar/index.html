01. Executive Summary

Trystar is positioned to revolutionize the power distribution sector by transitioning from manual assembly to a flexible, digitally integrated ecosystem. The strategy targets high-mix, low-volume (HMLV) customization, deploying modular automation to handle repetitive fabrication and material transport while preserving skilled labor for complex electrical configurations.

02. Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A: Cobots

Focus: Machine Tending & Assembly

Deployment of collaborative robots for press brake tending and torque-sensitive screw driving. Reduces RSI risks and ensures consistent enclosure fabrication.

Pathway B: AMRs

Focus: Material Transport

Autonomous Mobile Robots to manage WIP flow of heavy copper wire and steel between fabrication, paint, and assembly, reducing non-value-added walking time.

Pathway C: End-of-Line

Focus: Palletizing & QA

Automated palletizing for heavy load banks and cable crates, paired with Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) to verify wiring continuity before shipping.

04. Implementation Roadmap

  • Year 1: Pilot & Proof of Concept
    Deploy 2 Cobots in fabrication. Single AMR route for raw copper. Establish OEE baselines.
  • Year 2: Expansion of Logistics
    Scale AMR fleet for paint-to-assembly transport. Introduce automated palletizing.
  • Year 3: Smart Assembly
    Integrate Cobots for electrical fastening. Begin Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) testing.
  • Year 4-5: Autonomous Ecosystem
    Full IT/OT integration. Inventory triggers AMR movements automatically. Predictive maintenance active.
