Executive Strategy: The "Mezze" Logistics Model

Oren's Hummus Shop operates on a high-touch "mezze" model, requiring frequent staff trips for small plates, pita refills, and bussing. In the high-cost Bay Area labor market, our strategy focuses on Pathway A (FOH Logistics) and Pathway B (BOH Consistency). By automating the physical transport of food and dirty dishes, we protect the brand's hospitality while removing the bottleneck of physical labor.

PATHWAY A

FOH Serving Bots

Deployment: 2 units per location.

Function: Deploying shelf-based robots to handle heavy tray running and bussing. This allows servers to stay in their sections, increasing water refills and upsell opportunities.

PATHWAY B

Automated Frying

Deployment: 1 Auto-lift Fryer Station.

Function: Automating the falafel frying process ensures precise cooking times, reducing food waste and preventing kitchen injuries during the lunch rush.

PATHWAY C

Voice AI Agents

Deployment: System-wide Cloud Integration.

Function: AI management of phone orders to prevent missed revenue during peak hours and ensure consistent upselling of sides and beverages.
