Research Portfolio: Tennyson Center

Executive Summary

Tennyson Center for Children is transitioning toward high-impact community-based care. The integration of Robotic Process Automation (RPA) and Socially Assistive Robotics (SAR) offers a path to stabilize the workforce and expand care capacity. The primary ROI driver is the reduction of administrative burden, directly correlating to staff retention and increased billable clinical hours.

Robotics Opportunity Pathways

Pathway A Administrative RPA (Highest ROI)

Scope: Automated documentation, billing reconciliation, and intake processing.

Impact: Saves ~5-8 hours/week per clinician. Prevents revenue leakage from denial management.

Pathway B Socially Assistive Robotics (SAR)

Scope: AI-driven robots (e.g., Moxie) in K-12 classrooms for emotional regulation.

Impact: Reduces behavioral incidents, provides consistent social scripting for neurodivergent students.

Pathway C Environmental Automation

Scope: Non-invasive sensors for safety monitoring and sensory room control.

Impact: Lowers insurance liability and improves incident response times.
