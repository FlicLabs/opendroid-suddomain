01 // Executive Vision

Wilson Logistics is positioned to transcend traditional trucking by integrating high-velocity automated transfer hubs. Our research indicates that transitioning from manual cross-docking to Robot-Augmented Operations will secure market resilience.

This strategy targets a 40% reduction in dwell time and introduces value-added fulfillment services that manual competitors cannot match.

$1.2M Est. Initial CAPEX
35% Labor Cost Reduction
18 Mo ROI Payback Period

02 // Robotics Pathways

  • Pathway A: AMRs Immediate Impact

    Automated transport for cross-docking. Replaces long-haul forklift travel, keeping humans at the dock face.

  • Pathway B: ASRS & Piece Picking

    Shuttle technology increasing storage density by 300% for micro-fulfillment services.

  • Pathway C: AI Logistics

    Computer vision at gates/docks for damage inspection and predictive yard management.

04 // Financial Projection (5-Year)

The implementation follows a strict Pilot > Scale > Optimize cadence. While Year 1 incurs heavy CAPEX for hardware acquisition (AMRs & ASRS), the operational savings compound significantly by Year 3.

  • Reduction in overtime & temp labor.
  • "Lights-out" energy savings in ASRS zones.
  • Reduced claim processing via AI inspection.
